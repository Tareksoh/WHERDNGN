# v1.5.3 Pattern Mining Audit

Agent 1 of 5 — pattern mining pass. Goal: enumerate every deterministic
or near-deterministic decision point a skilled human could memorize and
exploit. Mechanical/forced plays (must-follow, only-legal-card) excluded.

## Summary

- **Total decision points cataloged: 47**
- **Fully deterministic (no jitter, no rollout): 26 (55%)**
- **Has bounded randomness (jitter / breaker / probabilistic): 18 (38%)**
- **ISMCTS-driven (Saudi Master only, non-deterministic): 3 (6%)**

The bot has a *lot* of deterministic surface area. Of 47 catalogued
choice points, 26 always make the same play given identical state. Of
the 18 with randomness, 11 use small jitter (±6 to ±18) on a threshold
— the underlying decision *direction* is still readable; only the
exact firing edge wobbles. True policy-level mixing (probabilistic
fire) appears at 7 points (mostly v1.2.x–v1.5.x audit retrofits).

ISMCTS at Saudi Master tier injects genuine sample noise but only
between ≥2 cards that survive the heuristic filter; many decisions
narrow `legal` to one card before ISMCTS sees it.

## Highest-leak decision points (ranked top 10)

These are the hardest for the bot to mask and easiest for a human
to memorize and exploit.

1. **L-06 (Bidder Hokm trump-pull)** — `pickLead:3401–3423`. Default
   bidder-team Hokm lead is "highest trump excluding A-of-trump if
   non-A trump available and tricks<5". A skilled human watching
   the *first* trump leadcard knows two things instantly: bidder
   has either J or 9 (one not played) AND has trump count ≥ 2.
   The "preserve A" rule makes the absence of A-trump on trick 1–2
   broadcast the J-or-9 holding. No randomness; pure rank pick.

2. **F-08 (Second-lowest re-entry signal)** — `pickFollow:4799–4823`.
   When partner-winning + can't-beat + ≥2 follow cards, the bot
   plays the SECOND-lowest. A pro human reading "bot threw 8 of
   ♥ when it could've thrown 7" instantly infers "bot has a higher
   ♥ to take a return". Sun + Hokm-non-trump-led both fire it
   (v1.1.0 H4 extension). Fully deterministic.

3. **L-12 (Sun establishing Mask Allon)** — `pickLead:3688–3783`.
   M3lm Sun bidder team will lead the *highest live boss* (A or T)
   from any 3+-card suit. The opening lead in Sun *announces*
   "I have ≥3 in this suit and I hold the boss." Combined with the
   A+T mardoofa rule (L-02 on trick 1), a human knows the bidder's
   shape from one card. Round-end T-deferral (only at trick≤3 +
   partner won 0) is itself a tell — leading T at trick 4
   *announces* underContractPressure or partner-has-won.

4. **L-13 / L-14 (Sun shortest-suit / longest non-trump)** —
   `pickLead:3826–3963`. The default Sun lead is "lowest from
   shortest non-trump suit"; the default Hokm-defender lead is
   "lowest from longest non-trump". A human counts the bot's
   non-trump suits via observation and infers shape from the
   *suit identity* the bot opens. shuffledSuits randomizes the
   tied case but not the suit-length pick. **Suit chosen reveals
   short suits (Sun) or long suits (Hokm-defender).**

5. **F-24 (Belote K+Q preservation)** — `pickFollow:6013–6024`.
   Through trick 3, if bot holds K+Q of trump, neither comes out
   on a discard. A human counting trump rounds knows: "bot has
   K and Q of trump in trick 1–3" → predicts +20 Belote bonus →
   plans escalation accordingly. Defenders should NOT Bel against
   a bidder with Belote signal.

6. **F-11 (Defender J/9 burn protection on bidder low-trump probe)**
   — `pickFollow:5045–5074`. When opp bidder leads 7/8/Q of trump
   and defender has J or 9 + a duck card, defender ducks. The
   *act of ducking* a 7-of-trump tells the bidder "defender holds
   J or 9 of trump" — which is exactly what the bidder's probe was
   trying to learn. The bot's "protection" reveals the protected
   card.

7. **F-03 / F-04 (Smother + non-consecutive Tasgheer)** —
   `pickFollow:4410–4556`. Smother always plays the *highest*
   pointCard of led suit when partner-winning + feedSafe (sorted
   descending, take [1]). A human reads "bot dumped its A on me"
   = "bot also has T or lower in suit" (the second-pointCard
   gate). v1.2.1 G8 non-consecutive Tasgheer (play LOWER of pair)
   IS a useful deviation but its 5-condition gate is deterministic.

8. **E-01 (Score-desperation Bel hand-bypass)** —
   `PickDouble:6587`. When `oppCum ≥ target-26` AND
   `myCum ≤ oppCum-50`, bot Bels regardless of hand strength.
   No jitter. The score is public — humans know the trigger
   exactly. Hand-shape Bel-readers expect this Bel; defenders/
   bidder calibrate accordingly. Saudi pro reasoning is sound, but
   the deterministic firing edge is a clear tell.

9. **F-19 (Trick-8 M5 make-or-break)** — `pickFollow:5550–5720`.
   The trick-8 logic computes a meld+Belote-aware target, then
   picks `highestByRank` if `gap ∈ (0, 30]` else `highestByFaceValue`.
   A human at trick 8 with full information can compute the same
   target and predict the bot's choice deterministically. Used
   correctly this is *good* play — but the lack of any noise in
   the choice means a human reverse-engineers it perfectly.

10. **L-08 / L-09 / L-10 (Free-trick suit / single-opp-void
    boss / partner-void ruff setup)** — `pickLead:3538–3608`.
    Three deterministic exploits of *observable* voids. Once a
    void surfaces (opp fails to follow on trick N), the bot's
    next-lead suit becomes predictable: lead the void-suit boss
    or lead-low-to-partner-void. The *suit a human chooses to
    discard from on trick 1–2 dictates the bot's trick 3 lead*.
    A patient human can manipulate the bot's lead by choosing
    discard suits.

## Notes on randomness budget

Per round, conservative entropy estimate (8 tricks, ~5 calls per round
that hit a probabilistic gate):

| Source | Calls/round (est.) | Bits/call |
|---|---|---|
| jitter() on bid/escalation thresholds | 1–6 | ~3.6 (uniform [-6,6] = 13 outcomes) |
| pickRandomTied on `lowestByRank`/`highestByRank` | 8–16 | ~1.5 (typical 2-3 ties) |
| shuffledSuits | ~4 | ~4.6 (24 perms = log2 24) |
| L-17 partner-AKA-leadback (0.85) | ≤1 | ~0.6 |
| F-02 Faranka 5-factor (0.05–0.95) | ≤1 | ~0.4–1.0 |
| F-16 pos-3 K-hold-back (0.30/0.40) | ≤1 | ~0.9 |
| F-20 deceptiveOverplay (0.40/0.25) | ≤1 | ~1.0 |
| F-28 tasgheer near-lowest (0.07/0.03) | ≤1 | ~0.4 |
| A-02 AKA withhold (0.10/0.04) | ≤1 | ~0.5 |
| A-03 AKA noise (0.03) | ≤1 | ~0.2 |

**Total round entropy: ~30–60 bits**. That sounds like a lot but it
is dominated by jitter on inputs that humans can't observe (e.g.,
bid threshold ±6 is invisible because the strength score is hidden).
The *observable*, action-level randomness is much smaller — at most
~5–10 bits per round (from probabilistic gates F-02/F-16/F-20/F-28
+ A-02/A-03, plus pickRandomTied across ~10 calls).

For comparison, a fully-random play picker on 8 cards = 8! = ~15.6
bits per hand of *legitimate* shape entropy. The bot's observable
randomness is well *below* the natural shape-noise floor — meaning
a careful human, after a few rounds of observation, can predict
the bot's *direction* (which suit, which card class) at each
decision point with high accuracy. Only the *exact* card from a
tied set or the *firing edge* of a threshold is genuinely
unpredictable.

### Where ISMCTS helps and doesn't

ISMCTS (Saudi Master tier only, `BotMaster.PickPlay`) injects
sample-noise across 30–100 worlds — but:

- **Single-card legal** at line 1048 returns immediately, no rollout.
  Many forced positions skip ISMCTS entirely.
- The rollout policy *delegates back to* `Bot.PickPlay` (line 889) →
  the *opponent simulations* are themselves running the deterministic
  heuristics. Variance comes from deal sampling (which cards each
  opp seat holds) — NOT from policy noise.
- Budget cap at 0.5s (`K.BOT_ISMCTS_BUDGET_SEC`) caps the
  determinization count under load, falling back to deterministic
  picks when the timer expires.
- The `getStrongCards` / `getDefenderCards` / `getPartnerCards`
  weights bias the sampler toward Saudi-canonical hand-shape
  *priors*, which means ISMCTS doesn't explore "weird" worlds —
  it converges on the same hand-shapes a human would predict.

Net: ISMCTS *narrows* outcome variance toward the policy mean of
the heuristic ensemble. It does not break the predictability of
the heuristic decision points themselves; it picks the
*best-expected* card under those heuristics. A human modeling the
bot's heuristics can predict ISMCTS picks too — to a useful
degree.

## Conclusion

The bot is **bulletproof in legality and rules adherence** (covered
by other agents) but **highly predictable in policy**. Of 47 choice
points, 26 are fully deterministic and another 11 use small bounded
jitter on hidden inputs. The 7 genuinely probabilistic gates
(F-02, F-16, F-17, F-20, F-28, A-02, A-03) are recent (v1.2.1+)
audit-driven retrofits that target *exactly* the highest-leak
points — but they don't cover most of the deterministic surface.

A skilled Saudi-tournament human, after 4–6 rounds of observation,
will likely:

1. Predict the bot's lead suit on most tricks within ±1 candidate.
2. Read AKA-suppression silence as "bot doesn't have the boss"
   (Saudi Master ~10% withhold doesn't break this strongly).
3. Manipulate the bot's leads by controlling the suits discarded
   in trick 1 (free-trick suit / single-opp-void exploits trigger
   on observable voids).
4. Time their Bel/Triple/Four to hit the bot's deterministic
   threshold edges (jitter ±6 to ±18 on threshold means the
   *firing decision* is correct ~70%+ of the time given hand
   shape).

The bot's strongest defenses against this are F-20 deceptiveOverplay
(plays J instead of cheapest 40% in Sun), F-28 tasgheer-near-lowest
(plays second-lowest 7% to corrupt count-reads), and A-02/A-03
AKA withhold/noise (Saudi-Master only) — all deliberately small
because over-deviating from canonical play is *itself* a tell to a
Saudi pro.

Recommended targets for follow-up audits (other agents):
- L-06 trump-pull: add small probabilistic A-of-trump
  variance (currently A is *never* picked at trick<5 if non-A
  available — that's the strongest tell on the bidder side).
- F-08 second-lowest: probabilistic absolute-lowest emission to
  break the "bot threw second-lowest = has higher" tell.
- L-13/L-14: small probability of leading from second-shortest
  / second-longest suit so suit-identity isn't a perfect shape
  read.
