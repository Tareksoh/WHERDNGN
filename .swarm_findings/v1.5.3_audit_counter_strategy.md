# v1.5.3 Counter-Strategy: Calibrated-Unpredictability Proposals

Agent 5 of 5 — counter-strategy synthesis. Surgical proposals that
close human-exploitable predictability without breaking partner-bot
signal comprehension.

## Design principle

Saudi Baloot bots play in pairs; partner-bots read each other through
canonical signals (Tahreeb, AKA, touching-honors, Faranka, escalation
rungs). Randomizing a signal-bearing decision blinds both opponent
AND partner — net effect in pure-bot play is usually a wash or loss.

Safe randomness is therefore **non-signal-bearing decision noise**:
places where the strategy doc rates the decision "Common" not
"Definite", where the score function is genuinely a flat-top
(close-tie EVs), where the bot already acts deterministically among
substitutable choices, or where partner-bot doesn't read the
randomized dimension. There's a narrow "tier-gated bluff" lane where
partner is upgraded to *track* the bluff (the existing v1.2.1 A2
Saudi-Master AKA noise emission is the precedent pattern). "Definite"
rules from `decision-trees.md` are off-limits — those define Saudi
flavor.

## Tier policy

| Tier         | Acceptable variance                                       | Forbidden                       |
|---           |---                                                        |---                              |
| Basic        | 0 (deterministic, easy to learn against — by design)      | bluff, slow-play, signal noise  |
| Advanced     | + small jitter on borderline thresholds (already ±6 / ±10) | bluff, AKA noise                |
| M3lm         | + capture/duck breaker (Faranka 0.40-0.60 band), + escalation timing-jitter | partner-confusing bluff |
| Fzloky       | + selective Faranka-rule-deviation across 5-factor framework, + lead-suit shuffle on flat candidates | partner-confusing AKA-suit lie |
| Saudi Master | + already-present AKA noise (3%) + AKA withhold (10%) + adaptive opp read | break "Definite" rule under any pretext |

Variance MUST monotonically grow with tier — Basic must remain a
deterministic baseline so users can learn against it; Saudi Master is
where ISMCTS rollouts already inject world-sampling noise (one
opportunity to leak to lower tiers — see CS-08 / CS-09).

## Proposals (ranked by ROI)

### CS-01: Faranka borderline capture-vs-duck breaker (HIGH ROI)
- **Current** (Bot.lua:4274-4377): factor-additive `captureRate`
  starts at 0.50, ±0.10 per factor, clamped to [0.05, 0.95]. The
  factor framework deterministically maps board state → captureRate.
  An opp who memorizes the framework can predict bot behavior on
  every Faranka shape: "F1 cover-J + F3 sweep + F5 LHO-bidder = 0.20
  capture" — i.e., bot will Faranka 80% from this state. Skilled humans
  observe the pattern over 2-3 rounds and hard-counter.
- **Problem**: factor-mapping is `Common`-rated for many factors
  (notably F4 score-flip and F5 LHO-bidder), but the bot treats them
  with `Definite` precision. Pro variance on these states (per video
  #20 framing «التحكم في الدور» — control by mixing decisions) shows
  ~10-15% bluff fraction at borderline rates.
- **Proposal**: at the M3lm-only entry point, when `captureRate` is in
  the genuinely-borderline band [0.40, 0.60] (the majority of mid-game
  Faranka eligible states), add a ±0.10 random kick BEFORE the clamp.
  This shifts the actual roll across the Faranka/capture flip threshold
  for ~15-20% of rolls that were previously fully predictable.
- **Code change** (Bot.lua, between line 4373 and the clamp at 4374):
  ```lua
  -- After all factor adjustments, before the [0.05, 0.95] clamp.
  -- Borderline-state breaker: only when none of the factors have
  -- pushed the rate to a clear extreme. Rate the bot is genuinely
  -- uncertain → add a coin-flip-grade kick that humans can't predict.
  if Bot.IsM3lm and Bot.IsM3lm()
     and captureRate >= 0.40 and captureRate <= 0.60 then
      captureRate = captureRate + (math.random() * 0.20 - 0.10)
  end
  ```
- **Partner impact**: minimal. Partner-bot doesn't predict the Faranka
  outcome before it happens — partner reads the *result* (whether bot
  ducked or grabbed) and adjusts only style ledger downstream. The
  framework's F4/F5 factors are score-state-derived, so partner-bot
  observes the same state and forms identical priors; ±0.10 wobble
  shifts roughly 1 in 5 borderline outcomes, which is well inside the
  noise budget partner-bot already absorbs from the existing 0.50
  base.
- **Opp impact**: borderline-state predictability drops from 100% to
  roughly 80%. Skilled humans lose the "I know exactly what the bot
  does at A+T pos-4 trick-1 LHO-bidder" tell. Average human-exploit
  edge against M3lm Faranka pattern probably -2 to -4 points/game.
- **Test impact**: existing test suite (177/177) likely needs a
  source-pin update for any test that asserts deterministic Faranka
  rate at a borderline state. Mark v1.6.0.

### CS-02: Escalation rung timing-jitter (HIGH ROI)
- **Current** (Bot.lua:6749, 6925, 7007, 7061): each rung uses
  `jitter(th, RUNG_JITTER)` with rung-specific magnitudes (Bel ±10,
  Triple ±12, Four ±15, Gahwa ±18). The threshold roll is
  per-call, but the *trigger order* is deterministic — seat with
  the strongest hand at the lowest rung's threshold always fires
  first. Humans can read "if the bot Bel'd, then their hand strength
  is between BEL_TH-jitter and TRIPLE_TH-jitter" with a ~22-point
  resolution. That's a tight read on hand strength.
- **Problem**: the jitter magnitude defines a tight strength-range
  read on every escalation. Humans counter-escalate (Four off Bel)
  with high confidence on the implied strength. Bel rate is currently
  ~8% per round, so this leak fires roughly twice per game.
- **Proposal**: split each rung's jitter into two components — a
  STATELESS one (existing) and a per-game style component sourced
  from the bot's own `Bot._partnerStyle[seat]` lifetime counter
  (already populated). Bots that have escalated a lot get a wider
  effective threshold band; bots that have rarely escalated get
  narrower. Tier-gate to Fzloky+ (need partner-style infra). This
  decorrelates "this bot's Bel" from "this bot's hand-strength
  band" — the same Bel can come from a stronger or weaker hand
  depending on opp-tracked history.
- **Code change** (Bot.lua, in PickDouble around line 6749):
  ```lua
  local jth = jitter(th, BEL_JITTER)
  if Bot.IsFzloky and Bot.IsFzloky() and Bot._partnerStyle then
      local style = Bot._partnerStyle[seat]
      if style and style.bels then
          -- Self-style asymmetry: a seat that has Beled often has a
          -- "loose Bel" reputation; widen jitter ±4. A seat with 0
          -- Bels has a "tight Bel" reputation; tighten by -2 (clamped
          -- non-negative). Net: opp can't bank "all bots Bel from
          -- the same range".
          local extra = math.min(4, math.max(-2, style.bels - 1))
          jth = jth + math.random(-extra, extra)
      end
  end
  ```
  Apply analogous deltas in PickTriple (line 6925), PickFour (7007),
  PickGahwa (7061). Wider extra-jitter on Four/Gahwa (rare rungs;
  per-game style noisier).
- **Partner impact**: partner doesn't predict our escalation in
  advance — they observe `S.s.contract.cardMult` change and adapt.
  Wider jitter means same hand may or may not Bel; partner-bot
  already handles "no Bel from defender" as a non-event, and seeing
  the Bel itself triggers Triple-eval on the standard threshold
  ladder. Self-jitter only changes WHICH hands get to Bel, not the
  meaning of a Bel that does fire.
- **Opp impact**: opp's "implied strength range from Bel" widens by
  ±4-±18 (rung-dependent). The 22-point Bel→Triple gap that allows
  opp to read "bot Beled but won't Triple" becomes a 26-30 point
  gap — much harder to bait Four-counter from the implied range.
- **Test impact**: escalation rate calibration tests may need to
  re-measure at p25/p75. Existing pure-bot multiseed metrics likely
  shift by <2 percentage points (well within noise envelope).

### CS-03: Lead-candidate flat-top randomization (HIGH ROI)
- **Current** (Bot.lua, pickLead at 2427+): pickLead resolves through
  a long chain of branch-priorities (closed-trump filter → trick-8
  targeting → sweep-pursuit → tahreeb-pref → AKA continuation →
  defender-style → lowestByRank fallback). Each branch returns one
  card; if no branch fires, fallback returns lowest. The branch
  order is deterministic and the within-branch tiebreak uses
  pickRandomTied (good!). But the cross-branch selection — "first
  branch that fires" — is fully deterministic, and many of those
  branches fire on Common-tier rules that opps can predict.
- **Problem**: when 2+ candidate suits are within a small EV margin
  (e.g., a Tanfeer-avoid suit and a sweep-pursuit suit both available),
  the bot's branch-order priority always picks the same one. A
  perfect-memory opp can pre-compute the bot's branch outcome.
- **Proposal**: introduce a `pickLeadCandidates` collector layer for
  Fzloky+ that gathers up to 3 viable candidates from the top-priority
  branches that did fire, scores them with a simple +1/+1 tally, and
  picks RANDOMLY from the tally-tied set. When all candidates have
  equal tally (the common case in mid-game), the bot picks at random
  within that set. Collector list builds inside the existing
  branches; no rewrite needed.
- **Code change** (Bot.lua, sketch — new helper near line 2425):
  ```lua
  local _leadCandidates = nil
  local function _addLeadCandidate(card, priority)
      if not _leadCandidates then return end
      _leadCandidates[#_leadCandidates + 1] = { card = card, pri = priority }
  end
  local function _resolveLeadCandidates(legal, contract)
      if not _leadCandidates or #_leadCandidates == 0 then return nil end
      table.sort(_leadCandidates, function(a, b) return a.pri > b.pri end)
      local top = _leadCandidates[1].pri
      local tied = {}
      for _, e in ipairs(_leadCandidates) do
          if e.pri == top then tied[#tied + 1] = e.card end
      end
      _leadCandidates = nil
      return tied[math.random(#tied)]
  end
  ```
  Then in pickLead, instead of `return X` on each top branch,
  conditionally `_addLeadCandidate(X, priority_for_branch)` and
  resolve at end (Fzloky+ only; lower tiers keep first-match
  behavior).
- **Partner impact**: minimal. The candidate set is already
  Saudi-canonical (every branch corresponds to a Definite/Common
  rule); partner-bot's read-side logic depends on the *suit* led, and
  the candidate set is restricted to suits the convention already
  permits. Partner sees a Tanfeer-led-suit either way; the choice of
  WHICH Tanfeer-eligible suit is the substitutable-choice space the
  v1.1.0 `shuffledSuits()` helper already addressed. CS-03 extends
  the same principle from "within suit" to "across cross-branch
  candidates of equal priority".
- **Opp impact**: opp's lead-suit prediction degrades from ~85% (with
  shuffledSuits factored in) to ~60% on flat-top states. Tanfeer
  defense gets harder; sweep-pursuit lead-suit gets harder.
- **Test impact**: lead-suit assertion tests will need source-pin
  refresh; likely 5-10 tests touched. Refactor effort small if
  done with `_addLeadCandidate` shim above.

### CS-04: Cross-tier ISMCTS variance leak to Fzloky (MEDIUM ROI)
- **Current** (BotMaster.lua:1142-1146): Saudi Master ISMCTS picks
  the single highest-score card across worlds. When two cards have
  near-equal scores (delta < epsilon), the deterministic "first
  match" loop returns whichever was iterated first — a hand-order
  tell at the very top tier. Lower tiers (Fzloky / M3lm / Advanced)
  have no ISMCTS and rely entirely on heuristic determinism.
- **Problem**: Saudi Master has variance from world-sampling, but
  its tie-resolution is deterministic. Fzloky has no variance from
  world-sampling at all. The Fzloky tier's pickFollow is a 1700-line
  decision tree that maps state → exact card (modulo the tie-break
  randomization at lowestByRank/highestByRank). A perfect-memory opp
  facing Fzloky can predict 90%+ of Fzloky's plays once they've seen
  a few tricks of opening style.
- **Proposal**: (a) add an epsilon-tiebreak at the BotMaster.PickPlay
  selection (line 1142-1146) so close-score cards get random pick;
  (b) for Fzloky tier, after the heuristic returns a card, run a
  thin "epsilon-perturbation" pass that re-samples the legal set
  with 5% probability — pick the heuristic's choice 95% of the time,
  pick a random *non-trump non-A non-J* legal card 5% of the time
  (those exclusions preserve signal-card fidelity).
- **Code change** (BotMaster.lua near line 1142):
  ```lua
  local best, bestScore = legal[1], -math.huge
  local tiedBest = {}
  local tieEps = 0.01
  for _, c in ipairs(legal) do
      if scores[c] > bestScore + tieEps then
          best, bestScore = c, scores[c]
          tiedBest = { c }
      elseif math.abs(scores[c] - bestScore) <= tieEps then
          tiedBest[#tiedBest + 1] = c
      end
  end
  if #tiedBest > 1 then best = tiedBest[math.random(#tiedBest)] end
  return _restore(best)
  ```
  And in Bot.PickPlay around line 6452 (Fzloky-only post-processor):
  ```lua
  local choice = pickFollow(legal, hand, trick, contract, seat)
  if Bot.IsFzloky() and not Bot.IsSaudiMaster()
     and #legal >= 2 and math.random() < 0.05 then
      local pool = {}
      for _, c in ipairs(legal) do
          local r = C.Rank(c)
          local isTrump = contract.trump and C.Suit(c) == contract.trump
          if not isTrump and r ~= "A" and r ~= "J" then
              pool[#pool + 1] = c
          end
      end
      if #pool > 0 then return pool[math.random(#pool)] end
  end
  return choice
  ```
- **Partner impact**: 5% perturbation only fires on *non-signal*
  cards (no trump, no Aces, no Jacks). Partner-bot's signal reads
  (Tahreeb on lows, touching-honors on K/Q, Faranka with A+cover)
  are intact because the perturbation never replaces a card it
  would read. The exclusion list is the partner-readable card set
  per `decision-trees.md` Sections 6/8/10.
- **Opp impact**: Fzloky becomes 5% genuinely unpredictable on
  middle-tier card plays — exactly the cards humans don't read but
  the bot was being deterministic about. Long-game pattern memory
  decays.
- **Test impact**: 5% perturbation will flake any test that asserts
  exact pickFollow return on a non-signal card. Recommend gating
  the perturbation behind a `WHEREDNGNDB.fzlokyVariance` flag
  off-by-default for tests, on-by-default in real play.

### CS-05: Slow-play substitution at borderline strong hands (MEDIUM ROI)
- **Current** (Bot.lua: lead trick-1 / trick-2 with a strong hand):
  bidder + strong hand always plays the canonical opening lead
  (trump pull, AKA boss, etc.). Skilled humans recognize the
  opening sequence and infer hand strength.
- **Problem**: opening-trick lead encodes hand strength deterministically.
  Bidder with J+9 of trump + side A always opens with trump-pull —
  reveals the J+9. A more pro-mimetic move sometimes withholds the
  trump pull to slow-play, baiting opp into an attacking lead from
  insufficient strength.
- **Proposal**: at Saudi Master tier only (variance budget
  permits), add a "slow-play-lead" branch on bidder + strong hand
  that 8-12% of the time delays the canonical opener by one trick,
  leading a side-suit-low instead. This is canonical Saudi pro
  behavior per video #07 + #25 (cited in `decision-trees.md`
  Section 1 row "Round 1 first lap, borderline" — but applied here
  to lead, not bid).
- **Code change** (Bot.lua, in pickLead around line 2540, after
  trick-8 targeting and before sweep-pursuit-early):
  ```lua
  -- Saudi Master slow-play option on bidder strong-hand trick 1.
  if Bot.IsSaudiMaster and Bot.IsSaudiMaster()
     and isBidder and trickNum == 1
     and contract.type == K.BID_HOKM and contract.trump then
      local trumpTops = 0
      for _, c in ipairs(legal) do
          if C.Suit(c) == contract.trump then
              local r = C.Rank(c)
              if r == "J" or r == "9" or r == "A" then
                  trumpTops = trumpTops + 1
              end
          end
      end
      -- Strong-hand slow-play: 10% chance to lead a side low
      -- instead of pulling trump immediately.
      if trumpTops >= 2 and math.random() < 0.10 then
          local nontrumps = {}
          for _, c in ipairs(legal) do
              if C.Suit(c) ~= contract.trump then
                  local r = C.Rank(c)
                  if r == "7" or r == "8" then
                      nontrumps[#nontrumps + 1] = c
                  end
              end
          end
          if #nontrumps > 0 then
              return nontrumps[math.random(#nontrumps)]
          end
      end
  end
  ```
- **Partner impact**: low. Partner-bot's trick-1 read (trump-led →
  bidder pulling; non-trump-low-led → bidder slow-playing or
  weak-trump) is documented in signals.md but not strictly
  enforced. The slow-play is rare enough (10% of bidder trick-1s)
  that partner's overall trick-1 prior is barely shifted.
- **Opp impact**: opp's "did the bidder open trump" tell weakens.
  Opp may second-trick a non-trump expecting bidder is weak, only
  to walk into the saved J+9 on trick 3.
- **Test impact**: low. The 10% rate should not break any source-pinned
  trick-1-lead tests if they sample with a fixed seed; otherwise
  add a `WHEREDNGNDB.saudiMasterVariance` deterministic-mode flag.

### CS-06: Adaptive-opp ledger (MEDIUM ROI)
- **Current**: bot has `Bot._partnerStyle[seat]` for each seat (4 of
  them), populated with bels/triples/leadCount/baitedSuit. The
  ledger is *informational* — read for prediction — but bot decisions
  don't currently adapt their own plays based on a *specific opponent's
  read history*. There's no "this opponent always Faranka-counters
  my lead-back, so I should NOT lead-back this round" branch.
- **Problem**: skilled humans adapt their reads of bot patterns
  faster than the bot adapts back. Result: a long match against the
  same human player progressively favors the human. Bot needs a
  meta-level "they keep counter-Farankaing me, switch up" register.
- **Proposal**: add a single `oppCounterPattern` counter to
  `_partnerStyle[seat]` keyed on the situation type (Faranka,
  AKA-lead-back, Tahreeb-return). Increment when an opp's response
  to our signal was a successful counter (ducked our Faranka, refused
  to take our AKA-bait, etc.). When the counter exceeds 3 against a
  given opp seat, FLIP the bot's behavior on that exact pattern with
  60% probability — i.e., we know they counter, so don't even try.
  Saudi Master only.
- **Code change** (Bot.lua, ledger addition around line 326):
  ```lua
  -- v1.6.0 (CS-06): adaptive opp counter-pattern ledger. When an opp
  -- repeatedly counters our signaling, mark the pattern and adapt.
  oppCounterPattern = { faranka = 0, akaLeadBack = 0, tahreebReturn = 0 },
  ```
  Then in the Faranka block (Bot.lua:4377), gate the M3lm capture
  flip on opp pattern:
  ```lua
  if Bot.IsSaudiMaster() and Bot._partnerStyle then
      local opp1 = (seat % 4) + 1  -- LHO
      local opp2 = ((seat + 1) % 4) + 1  -- RHO
      local opp1Style = Bot._partnerStyle[opp1]
      local opp2Style = Bot._partnerStyle[opp2]
      local oppFarankaSavvy = math.max(
          opp1Style and opp1Style.oppCounterPattern
              and opp1Style.oppCounterPattern.faranka or 0,
          opp2Style and opp2Style.oppCounterPattern
              and opp2Style.oppCounterPattern.faranka or 0)
      if oppFarankaSavvy >= 3 and math.random() < 0.60 then
          captureRate = 1.0  -- opp keeps countering, just take the trick
      end
  end
  ```
  The increment lives in `OnTrickEnd` — when our Faranka was
  followed by opp recapture next trick, increment opp's counter
  for `faranka`.
- **Partner impact**: zero. Adapting our OWN behavior based on
  observed opp counter-rate doesn't change our signaling vocabulary
  — partner-bot reads the canonical Tahreeb/AKA/touching-honors
  signals identically. The adaptation is in the *application
  cadence*, not the signal language.
- **Opp impact**: skilled humans who farm bot Farankas with
  consistent counter-play stop benefiting after 3 round-iterations.
  They have to vary their own counter to keep edge.
- **Test impact**: needs a unit test for OnTrickEnd ledger increment
  (3-trick scenario). Behavior tests may need source-pin refresh
  for the 60%-flip branch.

### CS-07: Selective Faranka-rule deviation at Fzloky tier (MEDIUM ROI)
- **Current** (Bot.lua:4274-4377): the 5-factor Faranka framework
  always applies the same factor weights (0.10 each). Pros vary
  the factor weighting hand-to-hand; the bot doesn't.
- **Problem**: factor 4 (score-flip) is the highest-information
  factor — it varies game-to-game. Factor 1 (cover-J) is structural
  — same every hand. Treating them with equal 0.10 weight makes the
  bot maximally legible: F4 is the leverage point opps don't track
  precisely, F1/F2/F3 are the ones they do.
- **Proposal**: at Fzloky+ tier, randomize the factor weights per
  round (keep them stable within a round to avoid mid-round
  inconsistency). Each round, sample weights independently from a
  small range — F1/F2/F3 in [0.06, 0.14], F4/F5 in [0.08, 0.12].
  Stored in `Bot._roundFarankaWeights`. Still totals to roughly the
  same expected captureRate over many rounds, but the per-round
  factor sensitivity differs in ways an opp can't read until late
  in the round.
- **Code change** (Bot.lua, at round-start around line 204):
  ```lua
  function Bot.ResetMemory()
      Bot._memory = emptyMemory()
      -- ... existing reset code ...
      -- v1.6.0 (CS-07): per-round Faranka factor weights at Fzloky+.
      if Bot.IsFzloky and Bot.IsFzloky() then
          local function jitW(lo, hi)
              return lo + math.random() * (hi - lo)
          end
          Bot._roundFarankaWeights = {
              F1 = jitW(0.06, 0.14), F2 = jitW(0.06, 0.14),
              F3 = jitW(0.06, 0.14), F4 = jitW(0.08, 0.12),
              F5 = jitW(0.08, 0.12),
          }
      else
          Bot._roundFarankaWeights = nil
      end
  end
  ```
  Then at each `captureRate = captureRate - 0.10` site in the
  Faranka block (Bot.lua lines 4304, 4321, 4333, 4342), use the
  per-round weight if available:
  ```lua
  local f1w = (Bot._roundFarankaWeights and Bot._roundFarankaWeights.F1) or 0.10
  if cover and C.Rank(cover) == "J" then
      captureRate = captureRate - f1w
  end
  ```
- **Partner impact**: minimal — partner-bot reads the
  capture-vs-duck *outcome*, not the factor weights. Partner's
  prior on opp Faranka is over the same roughly-symmetric
  distribution; the per-round variation is small (max ±0.04 per
  factor) and decorrelates partner's prior from a rigid framework
  read.
- **Opp impact**: pro humans cannot bank "F4 always shifts captureRate
  by exactly -0.10" — the leverage point varies. Same observed
  Faranka rate, but harder to back-derive board state.
- **Test impact**: any test that asserts a specific captureRate
  threshold needs an explicit `Bot._roundFarankaWeights = {0.10
  each}` reset to keep deterministic behavior. Otherwise
  measurement-based tests on aggregate rate (over 1000 rounds) are
  unaffected.

### CS-08: Hold-back probability widening (LOW ROI)
- **Current** (Bot.lua:5486-5499): the K-hold-back branch fires at
  flat 0.30 (M3lm) / 0.40 (Saudi Master) when all conditions match.
- **Problem**: 30% / 40% are the same fire rate every round, so the
  per-state predictability is identical to a discrete coin flip with
  known bias. Pros adjust their own hold-back rate match-by-match.
- **Proposal**: vary fire-rate within the Tier as a function of
  game-position (clutch vs early), already implemented for
  Saudi-Master AKA noise (Bot.lua:6362). Borrow that same
  clutch-aware adjustment.
- **Code change** (Bot.lua around line 5486):
  ```lua
  local fireRate = 0.30
  if Bot.IsSaudiMaster and Bot.IsSaudiMaster() then
      fireRate = 0.40
  end
  -- v1.6.0 (CS-08): clutch-aware hold-back rate. Less hold-back in
  -- clutch (we need the trick), more in low-pressure.
  if S.s.cumulative and S.s.target then
      local myTeam = R.TeamOf(seat)
      local meCum = S.s.cumulative[myTeam] or 0
      local oppCum = S.s.cumulative[(myTeam == "A") and "B" or "A"] or 0
      local clutch = (oppCum >= S.s.target - 26)
                     or (meCum >= S.s.target - 26)
      if clutch then fireRate = fireRate - 0.10 end
  end
  ```
- **Partner impact**: zero. Hold-back is a self-decision; partner
  doesn't predict it.
- **Opp impact**: opp's "bot will hold-back at 30%" simulation is
  off by ±10% depending on score state. Marginal improvement.
- **Test impact**: low. Existing 0.30/0.40 assertions are about
  ratio over many rolls; clutch-state shifts are second-order.

### CS-09: ISMCTS softmax world-aggregation (LOW ROI)
- **Current** (BotMaster.lua:1142-1146): ISMCTS picks `argmax`
  across worlds. The argmax is brittle — if best card and 2nd-best
  card scores are within 1.5%, the argmax flip flops on minor
  noise.
- **Problem**: a perfect-memory opp running their own MCTS in their
  head finds the same argmax 70%+ of the time on trick 1-3 (where
  bot rolls 100 worlds and converges hard). Bot becomes legible on
  the very tier that should be hardest to read.
- **Proposal**: replace argmax with softmax-temperature sampling.
  At trick 1-2 use moderate temperature (T=0.7) so the softmax has
  meaningful variance over close-score candidates; at trick 3+
  cool to T=0.3 (more deterministic) since bot has more state.
  Saudi Master only.
- **Code change** (BotMaster.lua around line 1142, replaces the
  `for _, c in ipairs(legal) do if scores[c] > bestScore...`
  block):
  ```lua
  local cards = {}
  local maxScore = -math.huge
  for _, c in ipairs(legal) do
      cards[#cards + 1] = c
      if scores[c] > maxScore then maxScore = scores[c] end
  end
  -- Softmax over (score - max), temperature trick-dependent.
  local temp = (numTricks <= 2) and 0.7 or 0.3
  local weights, total = {}, 0
  for _, c in ipairs(cards) do
      local w = math.exp((scores[c] - maxScore) / temp)
      weights[c] = w
      total = total + w
  end
  local roll = math.random() * total
  local acc = 0
  for _, c in ipairs(cards) do
      acc = acc + weights[c]
      if roll <= acc then return _restore(c) end
  end
  return _restore(cards[#cards])  -- defensive fallback
  ```
- **Partner impact**: ISMCTS rollouts already use heuristic
  rollout policy (Bot.PickPlay recursive); partner-bot is one of
  those heuristic rollouts inside the world. No change to
  signaling vocabulary. Softmax just chooses among
  near-tied-score real-world plays.
- **Opp impact**: opp can no longer assume "Saudi Master picks the
  argmax" on trick 1-2; they have to weigh the top-3 plays as
  potentially viable. Adds 1-2 bits of uncertainty on the most-watched
  decisions.
- **Test impact**: medium. ISMCTS deterministic assertions will
  flake at T=0.7. Add a `WHEREDNGNDB.ismctsTemp` knob to set
  T=0 for tests (recovers argmax behavior).

### CS-10: Lead-back delay rebalance (LOW ROI)
- **Current** (Bot.lua:2911): `leadBackRoll < 0.85` fires lead-back
  on AKA-suit. The 85% rate is reasonably noisy but still a hard
  threshold.
- **Problem**: 85% is calibrated against video #19's «دائما خصم
  يحتفظ قوته في الاخر» — but the framing also varies trick-by-trick.
  Pros lead-back faster on early tricks, slower on late.
- **Proposal**: vary `leadBackRoll` threshold by trick count: 0.95
  on tricks 2-3 (eager lead-back when partner just AKA'd), 0.85 on
  tricks 4-5 (current rate), 0.65 on tricks 6+ (saving pattern).
  Saudi Master only.
- **Code change** (Bot.lua around line 2911):
  ```lua
  -- Trick-aware lead-back rate (CS-10).
  local trickN = #(S.s.tricks or {}) + 1
  local leadBackTh = 0.85
  if Bot.IsSaudiMaster and Bot.IsSaudiMaster() then
      if trickN <= 3 then leadBackTh = 0.95
      elseif trickN >= 6 then leadBackTh = 0.65 end
  end
  if leadBackRoll < leadBackTh then
      tahreebPrefSuit = su
      tahreebPrefFlavor = "want"
  end
  ```
- **Partner impact**: low. Partner reads the suit lead-back as a
  positive signal; the trick-aware curve mirrors what humans expect
  (early-eager, late-saving). Partner's own model predicts faster
  lead-back early without code changes.
- **Opp impact**: lead-back timing tells become trick-dependent;
  opp has to model the curve. Marginal.
- **Test impact**: low. Single-roll assertion tests need source-pin
  per-trick.

## Don't-do list

These are attractive-sounding ideas that would actually break partner
comprehension or canonical convention. Documented to prevent later
agents from re-proposing them.

1. **Don't randomize Tahreeb signal direction** (decision-trees.md
   Section 8). Tahreeb is `Definite` source-rated. Bottom-up means
   "want", top-down means "don't want". Inverting the direction
   even 5% of the time would catastrophically corrupt partner-bot's
   suit-preference inference. The whole Saudi-Baloot partnership
   convention rests on this; breaking it means the bot is no longer
   playing Saudi Baloot. **Forbidden at every tier.**

2. **Don't add AKA noise on the receiver side**. AKA reception is
   `Definite`-rated (Section 6, source 05+18). The sender-side
   `Bot.PickAKANoise` (Bot.lua:6390) is acceptable because it's
   tier-gated to Saudi Master AND tracked in
   `Bot._memory[seat].akaSent` so PARTNER-bot's `partnerAkaSuit`
   ledger gets the same suit marker — partner is "in on the bluff".
   Receiver-side noise (false-positive AKA detection) would have no
   parallel ledger; partner-bot would read its own bluff signal as
   real. **Don't extend.**

3. **Don't randomize escalation rung order** (e.g., random Triple
   without prior Bel). The Saudi escalation chain
   Bel→Triple→Four→Gahwa is canonical (CLAUDE.md +
   `decision-trees.md` Section 2). Each rung is a counter to the
   previous. Skipping rungs breaks the legality gate enforced by
   `Rules.lua` and the entire scoring premise. **Don't even
   prototype.**

4. **Don't randomize Bargiya (Ace-on-partner-winning) signal**.
   Decision-trees.md Section 8 row 1 + Section 7 row 8: Bargiya is
   the strongest Saudi "lead this back" signal. Sending a fake
   Bargiya would confuse partner's lead-suit preference inference
   (which the addon's `tahreebSent[suit]` tracks). Treat Bargiya
   the same way we treat AKA: emission is canonical, not bluffable.
   **Don't randomize.**

5. **Don't apply CS-04 / CS-09 at lower tiers**. Variance MUST
   monotonically grow with tier. If Fzloky has more variance than
   Saudi Master on a given decision, the tier ladder is broken
   (users can't reliably play against a more-deterministic Master
   tier). The ISMCTS softmax fix and the Fzloky perturbation are
   tier-anchored and that anchoring matters.
