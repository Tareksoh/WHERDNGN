# v1.5.3 Signal Leakage Audit

Audit angle: catalog every signal the bot emits during play, then classify
by what info leaks to opponents who know Saudi convention.

## Summary

- **Total signals cataloged: 13**
- **Public-convention signals (opps read same as partner): 10**
- **Bluff/false-signal capability: 3**
  - S-03 AKA: 3% noise-AKA emission (`Bot.PickAKANoise`, Bot.lua:6390)
  - S-12 Tasgheer mis-low: 3-7% near-lowest substitution (Bot.lua:6162)
  - S-13 Deceptive overplay (Shayb-J / T-bait): 25-40% over-pos-4 (Bot.lua:5747)
- **Estimated leak rate to opps qualitative**:
  - HIGH: 6 signals
  - MEDIUM: 4 signals
  - LOW: 3 signals
- **Aggregate**: A skilled opp reading every Saudi convention can typically
  pin 50-70% of the bot's hand shape by trick 4 against pure-honest play
  (signals 1, 4, 6, 7, 8, 11). The 3 bluff arms knock that down by ~10pp
  but probabilities are low enough that opps effectively assume HONEST
  by default and only get fooled rarely.

## Per-signal leak severity

### S-01: Tahreeb-Bargiya (A-discard) — HIGH leak

**What it tells partner**: "I have the slam in suit X — lead it back."

**What it tells opps reading the same convention**: Same thing.
Specifically:
- A of X is in bot's hand at moment of discard.
- Bot held ≥1 cover (or was cornered with only X non-trump).
- Bot is **void in led suit** (precondition: trick.leadSuit and we don't follow).

A skilled opp seeing "bot discards A♠ on partner's winning trick" infers:
1. Bot has further coverage in spades (T or K).
2. Spades will be led next time bot or partner gets the lead.
3. Bot is planning a partner-supported run on spades.

Defensive opp counter: hold up trumps to ruff the eventual spade lead-back.
Saudi convention publishes this — it's video #14 verbatim.

Bot does NOT lie here (0% bluff rate). All Bargiya gates require real
shape match.

### S-02: Tahreeb "want, no Ace" (low ascending) — HIGH leak

**What it tells partner**: "Want X back, I have ≥3 cards but no A/T."

**What it tells opps**: Same — and worse, because:
- Opps now know bot holds zero A/T in X.
- If both opps' hands have a high in X, they can free-ruff or boss-take.
- The signal itself locks in the negative information ("no A, no T") that
  partner uses, but opps use it for **direct attack lines**.

Bot does NOT lie. Always honest.

### S-03: AKA call — HIGH leak (mitigated by 3% noise)

**What it tells partner**: "I hold the highest unplayed of suit X."

**What it tells opps**:
- Bot has K/Q/J of X (whichever is currently highest unplayed).
- Bot is NOT void in trump (would suppress AKA per precondition).
- Bot is NOT in clutch withhold mode (90-96% reliability).

Mitigation actually present:
- **Withhold ~10% / clutch ~4%** (Bot.lua:6356-6369) — silence isn't a hard tell.
- **Noise-AKA ~3%** (Bot.lua:6390) — emits on K-when-A-out as false claim.

Opps can still read "AKA on X = bot has top-live X" with ~95% confidence.
The bluff dampens the silence-as-tell but doesn't dampen the
banner-as-tell.

### S-04: Faranka duck (cover under partner-winning A) — HIGH leak

**What it tells partner**: "I have A+cover, ducking so my A holds for next trick."

**What it tells opps**:
- Bot has both A and a cover (T or K) of led suit.
- Bot has exactly 2 cards in led suit.
- Bot is on bidder-team.

Capture/Faranka decision is probabilistic (0.05-0.95 range, base 0.50)
based on 5 factors. But **the played card identity** (A vs cover) IS the
signal. When bot plays cover under partner's winning trick AT POS-4 in Sun,
opps with the played-cards memory can deduce A is still in bot's hand.

Bot does NOT lie about shape — gate filters on real A+cover holding.

### S-05: Pos-2 K-on-A-dead — MEDIUM leak

**What it tells partner**: Implicit AKA-equivalent (boss claim by play).

**What it tells opps**: Bot held K of X. Once K is played, opps know
that K is gone — but the **TIMING** (taking with K vs ducking) reveals
whether bot considers K "top-live" or not. If A is dead and bot takes
with K, opps learn K-is-boss state was correctly read. Not much new info
since the played-cards ledger is shared.

NO bluff arm.

### S-06: Pos-3 hold-back (Sun K-bait) — LOW leak (deception-positive)

**What it tells partner**: "Trust me, partner; we're winning this anyway."

**What it tells opps**: This is the ONLY major DECEPTIVE signal.
- 30% M3lm / 40% Saudi Master.
- Bot ducks LOW at pos-3 holding K, fakes "void/weak" in suit X.
- Opp's intended read: "bot has nothing in X, can lead X back safely."
- Reality: bot ambushes with K next round.

Severity is LOW from opp-perspective because the SIGNAL is intentionally
corrupted — opp reads false info ~30-40% of the time. The downside: the
other 60-70% of pos-3-low-on-partner-mid plays are HONEST, so a
sophisticated opp can still index the conditional probability.

### S-07: Pos-3 K-doubled bait — MEDIUM leak

**What it tells partner**: Same as S-06 (deferred K).

**What it tells opps**: Same shape as S-06 but **deterministic** on
2-card K+low when opp leads 7/8/9. Opps with Saudi convention reading
will index "bot ducked K-doubleton at pos-3 vs low lead = bot has K
for trick 3+." This is the canonical Saudi pattern.

NO bluff arm — bot doesn't fake.

### S-08: Sun establishing lead (boss-long suit) — HIGH leak

**What it tells partner**: "I have A or T of X with ≥3 cards."

**What it tells opps**: Same. Worse:
- Opps now know X is a LONG and STRONG bot suit.
- Opps can avoid leading X (denies bot tempo) and instead force bot to
  lead other suits where bot is presumably weaker.
- Counter-tactic: opps lead other suits, force bot to discard from X
  and reveal more.

Honest 100% — fires only on real boss-and-long.

### S-09: Round-end T-deferral — MEDIUM leak (silence-as-signal)

**What it tells partner**: "I'm saving T for late."

**What it tells opps**: This is a **silence-as-signal** — bot DOESN'T
lead X early when bot has T-boss in X. A careful opp watching the bot
play several rounds learns: "if bot avoids leading X early with no
obvious other-suit reason, bot likely has T of X."

Mitigation: gate is narrow (partner-zero-tricks AND trick≤3 AND not
under-contract-pressure). The silence is hard to detect because the
trigger is sparse.

NO bluff arm — bot truly holds T when this fires.

### S-10: Touching-honors (K under A, T under K, etc.) — HIGH leak

**What it tells partner**: "Next-down rank is in my hand" (e.g., K under
A → I have Q+J or higher cover).

**What it tells opps**: SAME EXACT INFO — opps read the same touching
convention. Per signals.md Section 4 the convention is published.

This is one of the highest-leak signals because:
- Card identity is hard-public (everyone sees the play).
- The convention's interpretation is fixed.
- Counter: opps lead the suit forcing bot to expose the "next-down"
  middle honor.

NO bluff arm.

### S-11: Bel/Triple/Four/Gahwa escalation — HIGH leak

**What it tells partner**: "My hand strength ≥ threshold N."

**What it tells opps**: SAME. The threshold map is:
- Bel (×2) ≥ ~70 strength (sunStr + suitStrTrump + voids + sideAces)
- Triple ≥ ~90
- Four ≥ ~100
- Gahwa ≥ ~135

Opps reading the public Saudi escalation convention can lower-bound
the escaler's hand quality. They also learn:
- Sun escalator → ≥10 bonus baked in (Sun is harder for bidder).
- Hokm escalator → has trump strength + voids + side-Aces.

Mitigation:
- **Jitter ±10/12/15/18** (rising with rung) randomizes cutoffs.
- **Score-desperation arm S-15** breaks "Bel ⇒ strong hand" inference.
- **Style-ledger reads** (gahwaFailed, triples, sunFail) modulate
  thresholds, but those don't help opps' read of THIS bot's signal.

NO bluff arm proper, but jitter + score-bypass corrupt opp inference.

### S-12: Belote announce — LOW leak

**What it tells partner**: "+20 bonus achieved (K+Q-trump played)."

**What it tells opps**: That seat held K+Q-of-trump from deal. But the
information is post-hoc — both K and Q have been played by the time the
announce fires. The +20 is required by rule. Saudi tournament play
mandates verbal announcement. NO information advantage to opps that they
didn't already have from observed plays.

NO bluff arm — required by Saudi rule.

### S-13: Tasgheer low-discard — MEDIUM leak

**What it tells partner**: (none — pure card play).

**What it tells opps**: "If bot played 7, bot has nothing below 7"
(video #5 explicit: «بنسبه كبيره جدا ما عنده الاكه بنسبه ٩٠%»).
Opps' reads from low-discard are ~90% reliable per Saudi convention.

Mitigation:
- **3-7% mis-tasgheer** (Bot.lua:6162-6189) — bot plays 2nd-lowest
  instead of absolute lowest, but only when 2nd-lowest is within 3 ranks
  (so it's plausibly lowest from opp's view).
- Clutch-aware: 3% in clutch, 7% non-clutch (desync from AKA 4%/10%).

A genuine bluff. Reduces tell strength from ~95% to ~92% — small but real.

### S-14: Tanfeer sender (suit-want under opp-winning) — HIGH leak

**What it tells partner**: "Lead X back; I have A or T of X."

**What it tells opps**: SAME. Opps read the public Tanfeer convention
inversion (opp-winning context flips Tahreeb semantics to Tanfeer-positive).

NO bluff arm. Tier-gated to bot-partner-only (signals to humans = noise),
which actually makes it safe because human partners are off-channel.
But against M3lm+ opps, the same convention applies — opps decode it.

### S-15: Score-desperation Bel hand-bypass — LOW leak

**What it tells partner**: (none direct).

**What it tells opps**: This actually CORRUPTS opps' Bel-strength
inference. When score-desperation fires (oppCum ≥ target-26 AND we're
behind by ≥50), bot Bels regardless of hand strength. Opps using the
"Bel ⇒ strong hand" model now see false positives. This is information
DENIAL — leak severity LOW.

Helps opps in one narrow way: they can index "Bel from the team that's
60+ behind in match-score = score-desperation, hand likely weak." That
narrow context is itself a tell, but score-state is public so opps would
have known anyway.

## Aggregate exposure

A skilled M3lm+ opp who reads ALL conventions tracks the bot's hand via:

| Trick | Avg cards visible per opp | What signals fired by trick N | Bot hand pinned (% of 8 cards) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 (lead) | AKA at lead, possibly | 12-25% (1-2 cards if AKA fires) |
| 2 | 4 played | Pos-2 K-take, Faranka duck, Sun establishing | 25-40% (2-3 cards) |
| 3 | 8 played | Tahreeb-Bargiya/want, opp-Tanfeer reads | 40-55% (3-4 cards) |
| 4 | 12 played | Touching-honors chain, Belote announce | 55-70% (4-6 cards) |
| 5+ | Diminishing returns — most cards visible | (further reads from continuation patterns) | 70-90% |

**Estimate**: A skilled opp pins ~50-70% of the bot's hand by trick 4 in
honest-play mode. The 3 bluff arms (S-03 noise 3%, S-06 hold-back 30-40%,
S-13 mis-tasgheer 3-7%) inject noise but the bluff probabilities are too
low for opps to rationally re-weight away from "honest" priors. Opps
effectively assume bot is honest; bluff lands rarely but hard.

**Risk profile**: The bot is **bulletproof against opps who don't know
the conventions** (basic/advanced human play) and **predictable against
opps who read Saudi conventions thoroughly** (Saudi-Master humans /
similar bots). The bluff inventory is a defensive layer but at low rates;
in a duel of tournament-grade opponents the bot is roughly read as
"~90-95% honest sender" which means the 5-10% noise is too small to
defeat conditional-probability inference.

**Top exploitable leaks** (highest opp-EV when read):
1. **S-08 Sun establishing lead** — directly broadcasts long-and-strong
   suit; opps redirect to attack other suits.
2. **S-11 Escalation strength bands** — opps lower-bound hand on Bel/Triple
   /Four/Gahwa with no bluff capability.
3. **S-01/S-02 Tahreeb-Bargiya/want** — opps know exactly which suit bot
   wants returned and adjust.
4. **S-10 Touching-honors** — public convention, public read, public counter.

**Recommendation directions** (documentation only):
- Increase noise-AKA rate (3% is barely detectable).
- Add `tahreeb_noise_arm`: emit a Bargiya/want from a strong shape on rare
  occasions to corrupt the "Tahreeb only on weak" prior.
- Consider Bel jitter floor floor (currently strength→Bel is monotonic
  given threshold; opps still bound).
- Pos-3 hold-back at 30-40% is the right shape; replicate the
  "deceptive arm" pattern at other signal sites.
