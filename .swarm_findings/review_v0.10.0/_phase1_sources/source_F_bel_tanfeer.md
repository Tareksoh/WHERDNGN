## CLUSTER 1 — Bel / Doubling Multipliers (F1)

### Rule F1.01 — Bel = English "double" = ×2 multiplier on round score **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:00:15 – 00:00:33)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `الدبل من اسمه ... ضرب في اثنين ... راح تضرب نتيجه الحسبه`
- **English:** "Dabal" (Bel) means "double" — multiplies the final round-score (نتيجه الحسبه = "result of the count") by 2.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit definition.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A (mechanic).
- **Phase:** Bidding/Doubling phase, before play.
- **Thresholds:** ×2 multiplier.

### Rule F1.02 — Four escalation rungs: Bel → Three → Four → Gahwa **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:00:37 – 00:01:05)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `الدبل ... 3 ... الفور ... القهوه`
- **English:** Four named rungs of doubling exist: (a) Bel/Dabal = ×2, (b) Three/Thrī = ×3, (c) Four/Fōr = "Bel-Bel" = ×4, (d) Gahwa = "ends the game; whoever wins this Sakka wins the entire session."
- **Confidence:** HIGH — narrator enumerates all four.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Doubling phase.
- **Thresholds:** ×2, ×3, ×4, instant-win-session (Gahwa = "152 nuqta كامله").

### Rule F1.03 — Gahwa = whole-session instant win **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:01:00 – 00:01:09)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `القهوه ... راح ينتهي الجيم ... 152 نقطه كامله`
- **English:** Gahwa terminates the game — winner of this Sakka wins the entire session ("الجلسه كامله"). Equivalent to 152 nuqta full.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Doubling phase.
- **Thresholds:** Full session, 152 points.

### Rule F1.04 — Window to call Bel: before cards-revealed lockout **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:01:33 – 00:01:50)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `اذا كشفت الورق خلاص وما قلت دبل ممنوع تدبل`
- **English:** Window to declare Dabal: "around 5 seconds depending on the session," but **the hard ceiling is**: once any player has revealed/exposed (`كشف`) the remaining cards, no Bel may be declared. Some sessions require declaration before any reveal at all; others permit it as long as the declarer themselves hasn't revealed.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit "ممنوع تدبل" (forbidden to dabal).
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** After contract chosen, before card reveal/play start.
- **Thresholds:** ~5s timer (session-dependent); hard cutoff = first reveal of remaining cards.

### Rule F1.05 — Cards-revealed lockout: even one player revealing locks out further Bel
- **Source:** F1 (00:01:52 – 00:02:01)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `لو اي لاعب من هذول اللاعبين كشف ورق ... ممنوع انك تدبل`
- **English:** Some sessions: if ANY player has revealed their cards, Bel is forbidden for everyone. Other sessions allow Bel as long as the **caller** hasn't seen their own remaining cards yet.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Pre-play.
- **Thresholds:** First reveal = lockout (in strict variant).

### Rule F1.06 — Defender calls Bel; bidder team responds with silence-or-Three
- **Source:** F1 (00:02:13 – 00:02:25)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `الخصم واحد منهم قال دبل ... هذولي اما يسكتوا ... او يقول 3`
- **English:** Bidder buys Hokm/Sun → opposing team (`الخصم`) is the one who calls Dabal. After Dabal, bidder's team has the choice: stay silent (play at ×2) or escalate to "Three" (×3).
- **Confidence:** HIGH — order explicitly described.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Doubling phase.
- **Thresholds:** Defender starts the chain; bidder's response is silence-or-escalate.

### Rule F1.07 — Defenders may re-escalate to Four; bidders may push to Gahwa
- **Source:** F1 (00:02:25 – 00:02:46)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `وهذولي اما انه يمشوا اللعب 3 او يقولوا 4 ... قهوه`
- **English:** After bidder calls Three (×3), defenders may either accept (play ×3) or escalate to Four (×4). After Four, bidder may either accept or escalate to Gahwa.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit chain in alternating-sides.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Doubling phase.
- **Thresholds:** Strict alternating control: defender → bidder → defender → bidder; chain rungs ×2, ×3, ×4, Gahwa.

### Rule F1.08 — In Hokm: Bel and Four (×2 / ×4 i.e. EVEN multipliers) make the game **مقفول** ("closed") **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:03:55 – 00:04:14)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `الاعداد الزوجيه ... اللعب هنا راح يكون مقفول`
- **English:** Under Hokm, **even-numbered multipliers** (Dabal=×2 and Four=×4) put the game in "Maqfūl" (closed) mode = `ما تربيعي بحكم` (cannot lead trump / cannot start trick with trump suit).
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit terminology.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A — round-state rule.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** EVEN multiplier (×2, ×4) AND contract = Hokm → Maqfūl mode active.

### Rule F1.09 — Maqfūl rule: cannot lead trump unless trump-only in hand **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:05:09 – 00:05:25)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `ما يلعب اول ورقه في الارض حكم ... الا اذا باقي له حكم فقط`
- **English:** While Maqfūl is active: (a) leader cannot play trump as the first card of a trick, (b) any player must play any non-trump suit unless they have ONLY trump remaining in hand. Even an opponent without the led suit who holds a trump cannot ruff under Maqfūl-conditioned trick? — **clarification**: the narrator says "if his side leads trump, his partner can't play trump" — practically, leading-trump is what's barred. Defenders not having the led suit may still ruff (rule says specifically about leading).
- **Confidence:** HIGH for "no leading trump"; MEDIUM for ruffing details (narrator focuses on leading).
- **Hand-shape:** Trump-only hand is the only exemption to lead trump.
- **Phase:** Play phase under Maqfūl.
- **Thresholds:** Hand contains ONLY trump suit → may lead trump; otherwise no.

### Rule F1.10 — In Hokm: Three (×3, odd multiplier) and Gahwa = open play **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:05:28 – 00:05:46)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `يلعبون الدبل والفور لعب مفتوح ... ثري ... عدد فردي`
- **English:** Three (×3, odd) and Gahwa keep play "open" (`مفتوح`) = normal Belote play, you can lead trump and ruff freely. Some sessions even play Dabal/Four as open — but the canonical rule is that EVEN multipliers = Maqfūl.
- **Confidence:** HIGH for ×3/Gahwa = open; explicit narrator statement.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** ODD multiplier (×3) or Gahwa → open play; some sessions = open for ×2/×4 too.

### Rule F1.11 — "Hokm-Daiman" naming penalty (session-dependent)
- **Source:** F1 (00:05:55 – 00:06:20)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `قال اسمه الحكم راح يتقيد على اللاعب`
- **English:** In some sessions, after Hokm contract is taken under doubling, if any player asks "what's the trump?" (`ايش الحكم`) and someone NAMES the trump suit ("Hokm Da'iman" or names the actual suit), that player is **penalized** (binding "تقيد" applied to the player who said the name). Other sessions allow naming freely.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM — narrator describes session-dependent etiquette penalty.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Pre-play, after contract+doubling locked.
- **Thresholds:** Session house-rule.

### Rule F1.12 — Anti-grief: some sessions ban Gahwa under Hokm to prevent malicious destruction **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:06:17 – 00:06:40)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `بعض الجلسات ما تسمح بالقهوه في اللعب في الحكم`
- **English:** Some sessions DISALLOW Gahwa under Hokm contracts because malicious players angry about a previous game could escalate Bel→Three→Four→Gahwa from 0-0 score to deliberately wreck the session. Anti-grief rule.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit motivation given.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Doubling phase under Hokm.
- **Thresholds:** Session house-rule. Especially relevant at session start ("صفر صفر").

### Rule F1.13 — Sun rule: NO Gahwa, often no Three either (just Bel/Dabal allowed) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:06:42 – 00:06:59)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `الصن ما في قهوه ... خلاص دبل يعني تظرف اثنين فقط`
- **English:** Under Sun contract: NO Gahwa exists. Most sessions also forbid Three (`ما في ثري ولا قهوه`) — only Dabal (×2) is allowed. Some sessions DO permit Three and Four under Sun, but the default canonical rule is Sun = ×2 only.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Doubling phase under Sun.
- **Thresholds:** Sun → Dabal only (×2 max default); session may extend to ×3/×4.

### Rule F1.14 — Sun ASYMMETRIC Bel-100 gate (the "Bel-100 legality gate") **[FOCUS]** **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F1 (00:07:18 – 00:07:47)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `في الصن لازم يكون فريق 100 نقطه او اعلى والفريق الثاني يكون اقل من 100`
- **English:** **Crucial asymmetric rule.** Under Sun, calling Dabal requires: (a) one team has ≥100 game-score points, AND (b) the other team has <100 game-score points. The team **below 100** has the legitimate right (`لوحه حقيقيه`) to call Dabal. The team **above 100 has NO right to call Dabal under Sun** ("ماله حق انه يدبل"). Hokm has NO such restriction — Bel always allowed in Hokm regardless of score.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — narrator emphasizes the contrast with Hokm twice.
- **Hand-shape:** Score-position dependent, not hand-shape.
- **Phase:** Doubling phase, Sun contracts only.
- **Thresholds:** **Bidder team threshold:** if bidder team is ≥100, bidder team CANNOT call Dabal under Sun (only the <100 team can). **Defender team threshold:** symmetric — same gate; whichever side is <100 can call. Threshold = 100 nuqta game-score.

- **English:** Under Hokm, Dabal is ALWAYS legal regardless of score (>100 or <100, leader or trailer).
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit contrast with Sun.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Doubling under Hokm.
- **Thresholds:** None — always allowed.

## CLUSTER 2 — Tanfeer / Tahreeb taxonomy (F2)

### Rule F2.01 — Tanfeer = "smuggling/getting rid of" — a defined Arabic-card term
- **Source:** F2 (00:00:12 – 00:00:24)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `التنفير ... في اللغه العربيه تجي بكلمه تهريب او تهرب`
- **English:** Linguistic root: Tanfeer (`التنفير`) translates to Tahreeb (`تهريب`) = "smuggling" / "to smuggle off." Same semantic class as "to be able but not actually do (the trick-taking)."
- **Confidence:** HIGH — defined.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Play phase, signalling layer.
- **Thresholds:** N/A.

### Rule F2.02 — Tanfeer is the parent class; Tahreeb is a SUBSET (intent-bearing) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F2 (00:01:38 – 00:02:03)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `كل تهريب تنفيذ لكن ليس كل تنفيذ تهريب`
- **English:** **Set relation:** Every Tahreeb is a Tanfeer, but not every Tanfeer is a Tahreeb. Tanfeer (parent) = any discard of an unwanted card. Tahreeb (subset) = an INTENTIONAL discard with a specific shape/suit signalling goal.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — narrator states this verbatim.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Play phase, throughout.
- **Thresholds:** Tanfeer ⊃ Tahreeb (set inclusion).

### Rule F2.03 — Two types: intentional (=Tahreeb) and unintentional (=plain Tanfeer)
- **Source:** F2 (00:01:41 – 00:02:11)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `التنفيذ نوعين اما يكون تنفير بقصد او تنفيذ بدون قصد`
- **English:** Tanfeer has two flavors: (a) Tanfeer **bi-qasd** = with intent → this becomes Tahreeb (deliberately throwing a shape to convey "I want the opposite suit" or "I'm voiding"); (b) Tanfeer **bidūn qasd** = without intent → forced discard, no signal meaning.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** Intent flag distinguishes the two types.

### Rule F2.04 — Tahreeb requires player to be CUTTER (not respondent) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F2 (00:01:44 – 00:01:51)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `تهريب يعني تهرب اي ورقه بشرط تكون قاطع`
- **English:** **Precondition:** Tahreeb only happens when the player is a "cutter" (`قاطع`) = void in the led suit, free to play any card. If you're a "respondent" (`مجاوب`) = following suit, your discard isn't Tahreeb (you're forced).
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit precondition.
- **Hand-shape:** Void in led suit.
- **Phase:** Play phase, mid-trick.
- **Thresholds:** must-be-cutter (`قاطع`) gate.

### Rule F2.05 — Tahreeb signal direction: "opposite of the suit you discarded"
- **Source:** F2 (00:03:38 – 00:03:45)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `يعني ما يبغى الشكل الا اللعب فتمشي على نظام التهريب عكس اللون عكس الشكل`
- **English:** Tahreeb code: discard suit X → "I don't want X; partner please lead the OPPOSITE color/suit." Tahreeb works on opposite-color/opposite-suit signalling.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** Void in led suit + holding wanted suit elsewhere.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** Discard the unwanted-suit card → asks for opposite.

### Rule F2.06 — Counter-signal: a HIGH discard ("بَرقِيّة" Bargiya) means "I WANT this suit"
- **Source:** F2 (00:03:38 – 00:03:50)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `اللي اذا اعطاك برقيه معناته يبغى نفس الشكل`
- **English:** **Bargiya** (`برقيّة` = "telegram"): if cutter discards a HIGH-value card of a suit (instead of a low one), this is a **reverse signal**: "actually I want THIS suit." Or alternatively interpreted as "play from-bottom-to-top." Inverts the default Tahreeb.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** Cutter holding strong/high cards.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** High-card discard inverts the opposite-suit reading.

### Rule F2.07 — Default partner-cutter assumption: "partner is Tahreeb-ing for you" **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F2 (00:03:32 – 00:03:42)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `لما يقطع خويك ... دائما تفترض مبدئيا انه خويك قاعد يهرب لك`
- **English:** **Initial assumption:** when YOUR PARTNER cuts (is void in led suit), default reading is that they're Tahreeb-ing TO you (sending you a signal about which suit they want returned). This is the prior; refine later.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — narrator says "مبدئيا" (initially).
- **Hand-shape:** Partner void.
- **Phase:** Play phase, mid-trick.
- **Thresholds:** Default belief = partner discarding intentionally.

### Rule F2.08 — Default opponent-cutter assumption: "opponent is Tanfeer-ing (not Tahreeb)" **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F2 (00:04:01 – 00:04:25)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `الخصم اذا قطعت تعتبر انه قاعد ينفذ ... عنده قوته`
- **English:** **Opposite default:** when an OPPONENT cuts/discards in a suit, default reading is that they're Tanfeer-ing (just dumping; they HOLD strength in the discarded suit — Ace, Ten). This protects you from misreading enemy signals. Tahreeb is for partner; Tanfeer is for opponent.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit asymmetry.
- **Hand-shape:** Opponent void.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** Default = enemy holds strength in discarded suit.

### Rule F2.09 — Refined opponent-cutter reading depends on whether YOU are taking the trick
- **Source:** F2 (00:04:09 – 00:04:18)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `هذه في حاله اذا انت ماكل الحله تزيد لكن اذا انت ما انت ماكل الحله حيبدا يزيد ... التهريب`
- **English:** Modifier: if you're winning (`ماكل`) the trick, the "opponent has strength in discarded suit" reading is reinforced. If you're NOT winning the trick, opponent's discard starts to look more like genuine Tahreeb (= they want opposite suit, lacking strength here).
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** Trick-winner status conditioned.
- **Phase:** Play phase, mid-trick.
- **Thresholds:** Ambiguous; trick-winner state changes the reading.

### Rule F2.10 — "Faranka" exception: opponent can't beat you, so won't show **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F2 (00:01:23 – 00:01:38)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `كفرنك وما يلعب عليك في هذه الحال انت اكلت الحله`
- **English:** **Faranka exception** (`كفرنك` / `فرنك`): a special play state where the opponent has the higher card (e.g. Ace) but **chooses not to overplay you** because they're "Faranka" — i.e., they can't beat the trick anyway / it would waste their power. In this situation, even though you took the trick, your card-play counts as Tanfeer (you discarded a card you didn't want, but didn't intend a signal — you weren't trying to take the trick).
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH — narrator uses this as the canonical "Tanfeer without taking the trick deliberately" example.
- **Hand-shape:** Mid-trick, opponent holds higher but declines to play it.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** Special-case override of the "did I win or not" reading.

### Rule F2.11 — Tanfeer-without-Tahreeb: "discard a 7 to keep a 10 for later"
- **Source:** F2 (00:02:48 – 00:03:04)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `نفرت السبعه يعني ما ابغى السبعه صرفتها لكن مش شرط اني ما ابغى الشكل`
- **English:** Example of plain Tanfeer (no Tahreeb): you hold 10+7 of a suit. Opponent leads it. You play the 7 to "smother under" their 10 — you Tanfeer-ed the 7 (didn't want THE CARD), but you DO want the suit (you kept the 10). This is Tanfeer (rejecting a card) without Tahreeb (no anti-suit signal).
- **Confidence:** HIGH — narrator uses as the explicit illustrative example.
- **Hand-shape:** Holding 10+lower in a suit.
- **Phase:** Play phase, follow-suit.
- **Thresholds:** Hand-shape: keep top, drop bottom.

### Rule F2.12 — Forced-but-noted discard: even when forced, a high-card discard IS reportable
- **Source:** F2 (00:05:36 – 00:05:54)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `لعبت شريه هذا قهري سواء من تحت لفوق او من فوق لتحت`
- **English:** When forced (no choice), playing the higher card is "qahri" (forced). Whether you played from-bottom or from-top, even forced, partners can still read information from the rank you happened to drop. Doesn't change the Tahreeb/Tanfeer category, but adds reading nuance.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM.
- **Hand-shape:** Forced-discard situations.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** Forced ≠ intentional, but rank still informative.

### Rule F2.13 — Tahreeb forms rule of thumb: "the discard means I don't want THE CARD; not always = I don't want THE SUIT"
- **Source:** F2 (00:02:43 – 00:02:48)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `لما تلعب ورقه تنفرها معناته ما تبغى الورقه بذاتها لكن مش شرط ما تبغى الشكل`
- **English:** Definition refinement: discarding a card means rejecting that specific card. It's not necessarily a rejection of the SUIT (which would be Tahreeb). Tahreeb adds the suit-rejection layer.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** Card-rejection ≠ suit-rejection (Tahreeb adds suit-rejection).

### Rule F2.14 — All-respondent case: every player followed suit → no Tanfeer at all
- **Source:** F2 (00:03:25 – 00:03:32)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `اذا كل اللاعبين كانوا مجاوبين`
- **English:** If all four players follow suit, no one is Tanfeer-ing — discards aren't applicable since no one is void.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** All four respondents.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** Tanfeer signals only emit when at least one player goes void.

### Rule F2.15 — "Don't Tanfeer Sherya" (don't dump a Sherya/seven) heuristic **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F2 (00:04:43 – 00:05:24)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `ليش قاعد تنفذ شريعه ... كان احتفظت ما كان نفرته`
- **English:** Heuristic warning between partners: "Why are you dumping a Sherya/seven (small trump or "السبعة")? You should keep it." Explanation: when partner gets the lead later, they want the seven preserved so partner can take a future trick with it. Dumping it early = wasted resource. Holding it lets partner take "two extra tricks" (`تاكل حلتين زياده`).
- **Confidence:** HIGH — explicit player-criticism scenario.
- **Hand-shape:** Holding low trump (Sherya/7).
- **Phase:** Play phase, mid-game.
- **Thresholds:** Don't dump 7 of trump if partner can make use of it.

### Rule F2.16 — When you have NO power, dumping a card IS valid Tanfeer **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** F2 (00:05:42 – 00:06:01)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `لو انت فعلا ما عندك اي قوه ... هذا يعتبر تمثير عادي`
- **English:** If you genuinely have no strength (no Ace, no 10, no realistic trick-taking power), then dumping/Tahreeb-ing the Sherya or any card is FINE — counts as ordinary Tamthīl (regular card-play / signalling). The "don't dump 7" rule applies only when you HAD strength to spare.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** Hand with no Ace, no 10, no controllable cards.
- **Phase:** Play phase.
- **Thresholds:** No-power hand → discarding is ordinary, not a mistake.

### Rule F2.17 — Tahreeb code DIFFERS between "to-partner" and "to-opponent" interpretations
- **Source:** F2 (00:04:18 – 00:04:23)
- **Arabic ≤15:** `يختلف نظام التهريب الخصم عن التهريب خويه`
- **English:** The Tahreeb-system the OPPONENT uses (when reading their cuts) is different from the system used between PARTNERS. So when you analyze who's signalling what, you must apply the right interpretation rules per the player-relationship.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.
- **Hand-shape:** N/A.
- **Phase:** Play phase, signal-reading.

## SPECIFIC WATCHPOINT ANSWERS

### Bel-100 Legality Gate (F1)
**ASYMMETRIC RULE EXACTLY:** Under SUN ONLY, Dabal requires one team ≥100 and the other team <100. The team **<100** has the right; the team **≥100** has NO right. **Hokm is symmetric** — any team can call Bel regardless of score.

- **Bidder-team threshold:** if bidder team is at ≥100 game-score, bidder team CANNOT call Dabal under Sun. Only the <100 team can.
- **Defender-team threshold:** identical asymmetric gate; <100 team has the right, ≥100 team does not.
- **Round-1 anti-grief:** YES — some sessions disallow ANY Bel at the very start of the Sakka (when both teams at 0-0). Marked as session-house-rule.
- **Cards-revealed lockout:** YES — once any player reveals their cards (variant: only the caller's reveal), Bel is forbidden.
- **"Maqfūl" under even-multiplier Hokm:** YES — Hokm + Dabal (×2) and Hokm + Four (×4) → **Maqfūl** = no leading trump unless trump-only in hand. Three (×3, odd) and Gahwa = open play.

### Tanfeer Taxonomy (F2)
- **Parent class:** Tanfeer = any "I'm getting rid of this card" discard.
- **Subset:** Tahreeb = INTENTIONAL discard with signal meaning ("I want the opposite suit").
- **Tahreeb requires being CUTTER (void in led suit).**
- **Set relation:** Tamthīl ⊇ Tanfeer ⊇ Tahreeb.
- **Variants/forms enumerated:**
  1. Tahreeb (intentional, opposite-color signal).
  2. Bargiya (`بَرقِيّة` = "telegram") — high-card discard inverts the signal to "I want THIS suit."
  3. Tanfeer-without-Tahreeb (e.g., dumping 7 to keep 10 — keeps suit, rejects card).
  4. Faranka exception (`فرنك` — opponent declines to overplay; you're not actively trick-winning, your throw counts as Tanfeer not strategic-take).
  5. Forced discard (still Tanfeer; no signal intent attached, but rank may still inform).
- **Default partner-cutter reading:** partner is Tahreeb-ing TO you.
- **Default opponent-cutter reading:** opponent is Tanfeer-ing (holds strength in discarded suit).
- **Trick-winner conditioning:** if you are not winning the trick, opponent's discard starts to look more like genuine Tahreeb (no strength).

### "K-tripled" / Mathlooth (F3)
- **Mistranslation:** "K-tripled" appears to be a romanization artifact. The actual subject is **J-tripled** (Mathlooth = "tripled") — the JACK with 2 sidekicks (3 cards in a suit) under Sun contract.
- **Bel-x2 connection:** F3 does NOT explicitly tie Mathlooth to Bel-x2 multipliers. Mathlooth is a **hand-shape concept**, not a multiplier mechanic. It's about J becoming the 3rd-highest-card under Sun rank-order (A > 10 > J), so a 3-card holding lets you survive long enough to deploy J on trick 3.
- **When can you call?** Mathlooth isn't a "call" — it's a defensive/offensive holding pattern. The relevant CALL is whether to call **Kabūt** (full trick-sweep) given knowledge of opponent's possible Mathlooth.
- **K versus J ambiguity:** narrator allows the term to be applied to Q (`بنت`) or K (`ولد`) holdings (with 2 sidekicks), but the canonical and strongest case = J (Shayeb).

### Bel hand-strength thresholds
- **NEGATIVE FINDING:** No numerical hand-shape threshold for WHEN to call Bel is given in any of these 3 transcripts. The 3 sources cover:
  - F1: WHEN you may legally call (timing + score + cards-revealed gates).
  - F2: Discard-intent semantics (orthogonal to Bel calling).
  - F3: Mathlooth holding tactic (orthogonal to Bel calling).

- **Qāṭiʕ** (`قاطع`) = cutter (void in led suit)
- **Mujāwib** (`مجاوب`) = respondent (following suit)
- **Qahri** (`قهري`) = forced
- **Sakka** (`الصكّة`) = round
- **Jīm** (`الجيم`) = "game" / session
- **Nuqta** (`نقطة`) = point (game-score unit)
