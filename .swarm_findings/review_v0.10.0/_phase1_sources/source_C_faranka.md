## Rules extracted

### Definitional rules

#### Rule F-01 — What Faranka IS
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 0:21–1:00
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "الفرنكه ... التجاهل يعني تتجاهل اكبر ورقه موجوده عندك"
- **Rule (English):** Faranka = "ignoring/skipping". You hold the biggest card of the suit (or biggest in the trick) but deliberately play a smaller card you also hold so the trick goes elsewhere.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** You hold ≥2 cards of the led suit, including the biggest live card.
- **Phase scope:** Mid-trick following.
- **Numerical thresholds:** none.

#### Rule F-02 — Faranka requires that the trick will NOT come back to you
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 0:32–0:35
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "الحله ما تكون عندك تكون عند احد الثلاثه اللاعبين الاخرين"
- **Rule (English):** A play only counts as Faranka if the resulting trick is taken by one of the other three players, not by you. By definition you let it go.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** Mid-trick.
- **Numerical thresholds:** none.

#### Rule F-03 — Two domains exist: Sun-Faranka and Hokm-Faranka
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 1:00–1:05; Video 04 @ 0:09 ("الفرنكه في الحكم")
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "نوعين اما فرنكه في الصن او فرنه في الحكم"
- **Rule (English):** Faranka splits into two top-level types: Sun-Faranka (in no-trump / Sun contracts) and Hokm-Faranka (in trump contracts). They behave very differently.
- **Confidence:** Definite

#### Rule F-04 — Three sub-categories within Hokm-Faranka **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 2:01–2:21
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "ثلاثه اقسام ... اوراق الحكم فقط ... الحكم مع الصمت ... اوراق السنه"
- **Rule (English):** Hokm-Faranka has three sub-types:
  1. **Type 1 (trump-only)** — Faranka inside the trump suit itself.
  2. **Type 2 (trump + side suit)** — mixed; trump is led but a side-suit consideration drives the choice.
  3. **Type 3 (side-suit only)** — non-trump suits in a Hokm contract; effectively the "Sun-Faranka" rules apply because no trump is played in this trick.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** Hokm contracts only.
- **Note:** This taxonomy is the spine of video 04's exception list — each exception cites which type it belongs to.

#### Rule F-05 — Three "shapes" / forms of Faranka opportunity
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 3:20–3:40
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اول شكل اذا هذا لعب في البدايه ثاني شكل ... ثالث شكل"
- **Rule (English):** Three positional shapes that produce a Faranka decision:
  1. The opponent on YOUR right led the trick.
  2. Your partner is winning the trick (will eat it).
  3. The opponent on your left is winning / will play after you.
  Faranka favourability differs between these three.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** All; Sun and Hokm.

### Three "uses" / benefits of Faranka

#### Rule F-06 — Use #1: Eat more than one trick (تاكل اكثر من حله) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 2:25–2:50
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اول شيء انك تاكل اكثر من حله"
- **Rule (English):** By Farankaing on the current trick (letting partner take it), you preserve your top card to win a later trick — net result: 2 tricks instead of 1.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Numerical thresholds:** Net +1 trick.

#### Rule F-07 — Use #2: Achieve Kabout (تجيب كبوت) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 2:50–3:06
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "تجيب كبوت ... ما صار كبوت وانتم عارفين الكبوت كم نقاطه"
- **Rule (English):** When partner already has 6 tricks and you have the dominant cards, Farankaing prevents the opponents from stealing the 7th — keeping the line clean for Kabout (Capot/sweep). Speaker explicitly cites Kabout point value as motivating this.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** Late hand (partner already eaten 6 tricks per example).
- **Numerical thresholds:** Partner must have ≥6 tricks.

#### Rule F-08 — Use #3: "Hunt" the 10 (تصيد العشره) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 3:06–3:20
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "تصيد العشره زي ما سويت برضو في نفس هذا المثال"
- **Rule (English):** Faranka can be used to capture the opponents' 10 of a side suit later in the round — by holding back your top card so the opponent is forced to discard the 10 on a trick you eventually win.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Numerical thresholds:** Worth 10 points (the 10 itself).

### Sun-Faranka — the 5-factor weighting **[FOCUS]**

The speaker explicitly counts: "راح اعطيك خمس عوامل رئيسيه" — "I'll give you five main factors". Stated in priority order, factor #1 first.

#### Rule F-09 — Sun-Faranka Factor #1: You hold the King (الشايب) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 5:21–5:29
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اول شيء اذا كان عندك الشايب يعني شايب مع عكه"
- **Rule (English):** Factor #1 (highest priority): Holding the K (Shayeb) of the led suit — especially in combination with the A (Akkah / Eka). The K is "very important strength" (قوه جدا مهم). When you hold K+A as the second-highest pair, Faranka favourability rises dramatically.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** Must hold K of the suit being led; A is a strong adjunct.
- **Phase scope:** Sun (and Hokm-side-suit / Type 3).
- **Numerical thresholds:** none stated, but framed as the strongest single factor.

#### Rule F-10 — Sun-Faranka Factor #2: Partner is winning the trick (خويك حل) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 5:29–5:36
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا خويك كان حل يعني الحله تجيك وخويك اللي ماكلها"
- **Rule (English):** Factor #2: The trick is currently winning for your partner — either partner led, or partner was the player just before you. This is the canonical situation: partner has it covered, you should hold your top card.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** All.

#### Rule F-11 — Sun-Faranka Factor #3: The hand is heading toward Kabout **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 5:36–5:45
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا اللعب كان رايح كبوت يعني الفرنكه هذه اذا سويتها"
- **Rule (English):** Factor #3: If the round is plausibly heading toward a Kabout (sweep) for your team, Faranka favourability rises sharply. Letting partner take the trick keeps the Kabout alive.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** Mid-to-late hand once trajectory is clear.

#### Rule F-12 — Sun-Faranka Factor #4: Faranka would lose Game-points to opponents (تخسر الجيم على خصمك) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 5:45–5:59
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا الفرنكه هذه راح تخسر الجيم على خصمك"
- **Rule (English):** Factor #4: If the extra trick gained by Faranka tips the round-points so the **opponents** (الخصم) take a loss they were previously avoiding, Faranka is favoured. **Crucially: speaker requires "لازم يكونوا الخصم هم المشترين"** — opponents must be the bidders/buyers for this factor to apply.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** Late hand; need to be tracking running totals.
- **Bidder-team membership:** **Explicitly conditional on opponents being the bidder team** (انت تتابع اللعب وتكون متوقع تقريبا النتيجه النهائيه).

#### Rule F-13 — Sun-Faranka Factor #5: Left opponent leads the next trick AND your team are bidders **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 5:59–6:30
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا اللاعب اللي يسارك اللعب اول واحد ... في الحله الجديده"
- **Rule (English):** Factor #5 (most conditional): Normally when the left opponent leads the next trick, Faranka favourability **drops** — because they are positionally strong. **Exception:** if the next trick will be the *new opening trick of the hand* AND **your team are the bidders** ("سواء انت كنت مشتري او خويك كان مشتري صن"), then Faranka is preferred. Reasoning: the player who leads the next opening trick is assumed to have strength (and likely the 10), so let your partner cover that strength.
- **Confidence:** Definite (single explicit statement, but unambiguous)
- **Bidder-team membership:** **Explicitly bidder-team only.** Speaker says: if opponents are the bidders and the left partner leads anything, Faranka favourability drops.
- **Numerical thresholds:** none.

### Sun-Faranka anti-rules (when NOT to Faranka)

#### Rule F-14 — Anti-rule: Last-player and trick is going to opponent → MUST take it **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 6:45–7:09
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا كنت اخر لاعب انت لازم تتفرنك اذا هذا الخصم لعب في البدايه"
- **Rule (English):** When you are the **last** player on a trick and the opponents are currently winning, you **must take** the trick if you can. Speaker frames this as "very obvious and easy" (جدا واضحه وسهله). The example: opp leads J of side-suit, partner plays 9, second-opp plays 7, you have A+K — you take with A. Reverse (Faranka the A) gives the trick away.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** You are 4th to play; opponents are currently winning.
- **Phase scope:** All.
- **NOTE:** The speaker's Arabic literally says "must Faranka" (لازم تتفرنك), which **appears to be a transcription/speech artifact**. The full context (and the immediately following "و لو عكست ما تفرمت الاكا بعدها راح تلعب الشايب وهذا راح ياكل بالعشره" — "if you reverse and DON'T Faranka the Akkah, then play K, opp eats with 10") shows the intended rule is: **DO NOT Faranka, take the trick with your A**. **[FOCUS — possible code/lexicon trap if a reader takes the literal Arabic.]**

#### Rule F-15 — Anti-rule: When you hold the **two biggest live cards**, never Faranka **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 9:30–9:54, 10:50–11:01
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا عندك اكبر ورقتين موجوده في اللعب لا تترنك ابدا"
- **Rule (English):** If your hand contains the **two highest cards still in play of the led suit** (e.g. K and 10 when A is gone, or A and K when both still live), **never Faranka, ever** — regardless of seat position (last player or not). Reasoning: you can take this trick AND the next, so holding back loses nothing and Farankaing risks the partner not having coverage.
- **Confidence:** Definite (speaker emphasises "ابدا" — never)
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** Hold the top 2 live cards of the suit.
- **Phase scope:** All.

#### Rule F-16 — Anti-rule: Don't Faranka if you don't hold the K (الشايب) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 11:43–11:58
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "ما تفرنك اذا ما عندك الشاي ... كل ما تكون الورقه اللي ما عليك اصغر"
- **Rule (English):** If you do NOT hold the K (Shayeb), Faranka favourability drops sharply, especially if the next-best card you hold is small. Without the K backing you up, partner is vulnerable.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** You lack K of the suit being Faranka'd.
- **Phase scope:** All Sun-Faranka.

#### Rule F-17 — Anti-rule: Don't Faranka with a hand long in the suit (≥3 cards) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 11:58–12:53
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "ما تترنك اذا عندك اكثر من ورقتين ... عندك ايكا ولد بنت"
- **Rule (English):** Don't Faranka if you have **more than 2 cards** of that suit. Reasoning given: with you holding 3+ cards in suit X, only 5 cards (instead of 6) are distributed among the other 3 players — making it more likely the opponents will void out and the 10 will drop on your top card naturally. So just take the trick.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** Hold ≥3 cards of the led suit.
- **Phase scope:** All Sun-Faranka.
- **Numerical thresholds:** ≥3 cards triggers anti-rule. Speaker explicitly compares 2 vs 3 vs 4 holdings: 2 = 6 outstanding, 3 = 5 outstanding, 4 = 4 outstanding — "the more you hold, the worse Faranka becomes" because the 10 drops sooner naturally.

#### Rule F-18 — Anti-rule: Don't Faranka in Hokm at all (general default)
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 13:27–13:34; reiterated by Video 04
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "لا تتفرنك في الحكم الا اذا طمعت في كبوت فقط"
- **Rule (English):** "Do NOT Faranka in Hokm — except if you covet a Kabout, only." General-case anti-rule. Speaker pivots into video 04 here ("we'll talk about Hokm-Faranka in the next video").
- **Confidence:** Definite

### Sun-Faranka positional rules (Faranka favourability vs. who is winning)

#### Rule F-19 — Faranka most favoured when right-opponent is winning, you play last
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 3:35–3:42
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "كل ما تكون الحله عند اللاعب اللي يمينك وانت اخر واحد راح تلعب"
- **Rule (English):** Faranka chance peaks when the right-opponent is currently winning the trick and you are the **last** player to act. You see all 3 cards before deciding.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** none.
- **Phase scope:** Mid-trick, last seat.

#### Rule F-20 — Faranka favourability LOWER when partner led the trick
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 3:42–3:50
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا كانت عند خويه في لعب في البدايه حتقول لي النسبه شويه"
- **Rule (English):** When PARTNER led the trick (so partner went first, you don't yet know what the third player will do), Faranka favourability is "a little" — lower than the right-opponent-leading case.
- **Confidence:** Definite

#### Rule F-21 — Faranka favourability LOWER when left-opponent led the trick
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 3:42–4:24
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا كان عند هذا الخصم حتقول لي نسبه شويه"
- **Rule (English):** When LEFT-OPPONENT led the trick, Faranka favourability is also "a little". Reason: opponent could hold the 10 and play it on partner — making your Faranka backfire.
- **Confidence:** Definite

#### Rule F-22 — When opp leads small (e.g. 8) and the 10's location is unknown — moderate Faranka chance, depends on partner
- **Source:** Video 06 @ 4:31–4:46
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اذا كان عند خويك شيء كويس فممكن هنا حتجي نسبه الفرمته"
- **Rule (English):** When opp leads a small card (e.g. 8) and you don't know where the 10 is, Faranka favourability rises ONLY if partner is likely to have something good — many people just play A and avoid Faranka. Conditional rule.
- **Confidence:** Common
- **Phase scope:** Sun.

### Hokm-Faranka exceptions (Video 04) — DEFAULT NO, narrow exceptions in order

Video 04 opens (0:09–0:16): "اغلب لاعبين البلوت راح يقول لك لا تدفنك في الحكم ولو تجي تسالني نفس الكلام بشكل عام الافضل لا تترن في الحكم لكن فيها تفصيل" — "most players say don't Faranka in Hokm and if you ask me, same answer; in general it's best not to Faranka in Hokm, BUT there are details." The exceptions are then enumerated.

#### Rule F-23 — Hokm DEFAULT = NO Faranka **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 0:09–0:16; closing @ 5:55–end ("دائما في الحكم حاول قدر المستطاع لا تتفر")
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "بشكل عام الافضل لا تترن في الحكم"
- **Rule (English):** Default Hokm-Faranka decision = **NO**. Always. The exceptions below are narrow.
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** Hokm contracts.

#### Rule F-24 — Hokm Exception #1: Type-3 (side-suit only) Faranka to dump the trick on opponents (cabotage / تخسرها على الخصم) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 1:20–1:37
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "في حاله اذا تبغى تكبت اللعب زياده على الكبوت ... تخسرها على الخصم"
- **Rule (English):** **First exception** stated in video 04. If you want to extend a Kabout-style sweep beyond what's already secured, by deliberately losing this trick onto the opponents (so they take points they can't unload), then Faranka is acceptable — but ONLY in **Type 3** (side-suit-only Hokm-Faranka). "Because Type 3 is essentially the same as Sun" (نفس الصمت).
- **Confidence:** Definite
- **Phase scope:** Hokm Type 3 only.
- **Bidder-team membership:** Implicit — you are advancing your own team's Kabout, so this is bidder-side reasoning. **[FOCUS — verify code restricts this exception to Type 3 only.]**

#### Rule F-25 — "Other than that, NEVER Faranka [in Hokm types 1, 2]" **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 1:30–1:37
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "اما غير كذا ما انصحك ابدا"
- **Rule (English):** "Other than that [Type 3 with cabotage], I never advise it." Hard-stop on Type 1 / Type 2 Hokm-Faranka by default — exceptions to follow are explicitly opt-in special cases.
- **Confidence:** Definite

#### Rule F-26 — Hokm Exception #2: Type 1 (trump-only) — when you hold the J (Walad) AND the trick will eventually pass via partner's K to land 9 of trump on you
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 1:37–2:38 (the worked "you bought Hokm, partner cuts with 7-of-trump → opp likely will play J not 9 → you must NOT play 10, play J or have to take with K to draw the 9 out from leftopp")
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "تحتاج حاجه واحده تبغى تصير التسعه ... لازم تكون للاعب اللي قبلك"
- **Rule (English):** Type-1 Hokm-Faranka possible IF: you need only one specific outcome (the 9 of trump to fall) AND the 9 must be in the hand of the **player just before you** (the right-opponent). If 9 is with partner, Faranka won't help; if 9 is with the LEFT opponent (the one after you), they'll just play it and eat the trick — you've damaged yourself. Only Faranka if you have direct evidence the 9 is with right-opp.
- **Confidence:** Common
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** Hokm contract; you hold J of trump and a side card; J needs to be preserved.
- **Phase scope:** Type 1.
- **Numerical thresholds:** none.
- **Bidder-team membership:** Example given is "you bought Hokm" — bidder-team. **[FOCUS — does code restrict this to bidder-team only?]**

#### Rule F-27 — Hokm Exception #3: Weak Hokm holding (only 2 trumps) **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 2:18–2:38
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "ممكن تتفرنك اذا كان عندك حكمين فقط"
- **Rule (English):** If you bought Hokm but no second trump came in the deal — your trump holding is only 2 cards (e.g. J + small adjacent like 10) — you may Faranka, hoping you don't lose this trick on top of the other weaknesses. Speaker's framing: "your trumps are weak" (احكامك ضعيفه), so you accept the risk.
- **Confidence:** Common
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** ≤2 trumps in hand (after seeing your 5+3).
- **Phase scope:** Hokm.
- **Bidder-team membership:** Speaker example is bidder ("اشتريت حكم"), but the rule (weak trump holding) seems applicable to any seat with weak trump. **[FOCUS — ambiguous, default to bidder-only unless code already generalises.]**

#### Rule F-28 — Hokm Exception #4: You hold 9 of trump AND 9 is currently the live max of trump
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 2:38–3:49
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "افترض انه عندي التسعه والتسعه مردوفه معايا ... ايش الافضل"
- **Rule (English):** If you hold the 9 of trump (the second-highest trump in Saudi rules — see CLAUDE.md note) **and** also hold a redundant 10/A so you can throw, you may Faranka with the redundant card to preserve the 9. Caveat: speaker concedes that even here it's marginal — "you might lose the 9 anyway" (ممكن تروح عليك التسعه).
- **Confidence:** Sometimes
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** Hold 9 of trump + at least one redundant trump (10/A backup).
- **Phase scope:** Hokm Type 1.
- **Numerical thresholds:** Hold 9 + 1 redundant.

#### Rule F-29 — Hokm Exception #5: Faranka with the 9 only when J of trump is gone AND you have 9 = biggest live trump
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 3:19–3:50
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "متى تتفرلك بالتسعه ... اذا الولد لعب من اول"
- **Rule (English):** When can you Faranka the 9 of trump? Only after the J (Walad) of trump has already been played in an earlier trick — at which point your 9 is the **biggest live trump** card. With the 9 as top trump, if a trick comes where you're "knocked" with trump (دق بالحكم), play your smaller trump (Faranka the 9) rather than burning the 9. You may snipe the A (Akkah) or another 10 of trump on a later trick.
- **Confidence:** Common
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** J of trump already played; you hold 9 + one smaller trump.
- **Phase scope:** Hokm Type 1, mid/late hand.

#### Rule F-30 — Hokm Type 2: Bidder with A+K of side-suit and trying to extend to Kabout — slight Faranka favourability **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 5:00–5:25
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "وده انت كنت مشتري حكم وعندك ريكا وعندك الشايب هنا حزيد نسبه التفمك"
- **Rule (English):** When YOU are the bidder of a Hokm contract AND you hold A+K of a side-suit, **Faranka favourability rises slightly** if either (a) you covet Kabout (طمعت في كبوت) or (b) all trumps are out of all hands (اذا خلصت الاحكام من ايادي اللاعبين).
- **Confidence:** Common
- **Hand-shape preconditions:** You bought the Hokm; hold A+K of a side suit.
- **Phase scope:** Hokm Type 2.
- **Bidder-team membership:** **Explicitly bidder-only** ("انت كنت مشتري حكم").
- **Numerical thresholds:** none.

#### Rule F-31 — Hokm Type 2: Even if all trumps with partner, partner may not have shape — risk of partner forced to ruff
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 5:11–5:23
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "احيانا حتى لو حكم عند خويك ممكن انت خويك ما يكون عنده نفس الشكل"
- **Rule (English):** Caveat to F-30: even if remaining trumps are believed to be with your partner, partner might not have the led side-suit and would be forced to ruff (or just discard, depending on rules). So "trumps with partner" is not a sufficient signal alone — additional caution required.
- **Confidence:** Common

#### Rule F-32 — Hokm Type 2 anti-rule: Don't Faranka if Hokm bought by OPPONENTS **[FOCUS]**
- **Source:** Video 04 @ 4:11–4:19
- **Arabic phrase (≤15 words):** "ما انصحك تتفرلك ابدا بالذات اذا الخصم مشتري حكم"
