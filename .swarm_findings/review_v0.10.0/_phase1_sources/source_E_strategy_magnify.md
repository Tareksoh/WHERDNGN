# Source E — Strategy / Smart Move / Takbeer / Tasgheer cluster

Six Arabic SRT videos covering generic "smart" play, partner-direction signaling
(تكبير "magnification" — give partner the largest card; تصغير "miniaturization" —
give opponent the smallest), and game-control theory (مسك اللعب "holding play",
السيطره "control of suit"). NOT about bid-escalation rungs (Bel→Bel×2→Four→Gahwa);
"Takbeer" here means "play your highest card on partner-led tricks", not a contract
multiplier. This is a critical glossary disambiguation.

Per rule: **Source** | **Arabic ≤15 words** | **English** | **Confidence** |
**Hand-shape** | **Phase** | **Thresholds**.

`**[FOCUS]**` marks rules touching SWA / scoring / bot decisions.

## Video #07 — `b7EJYg7vpJo` Four Strategies (Strategy vs Tactic)

### E07-1 — "Strategy" defined as a plan / target

- **Source:** #07 L11-15
- **Arabic:** "استراتيجيه يعني خطه لازم تحط خطه في البلوت"
- **English:** Strategy = a plan; you must have a plan with a defined goal in Baloot.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** any
- **Phase:** all
- **Thresholds:** none — meta-frame.

### E07-2 — Two cases: bidder vs. defender

- **Source:** #07 L17-21
- **Arabic:** "حالتين الحاله الاولى من تكون مشترك والحاله الثانيه لما تكون غير مشكله"
- **English:** Strategy splits by role — bidder (مشتري) vs defender (غير مشتري).
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** any
- **Phase:** post-bid
- **Thresholds:** branches on contract role.

### E07-3 — Bidder strategy #1: don't let opponents *out-score* you `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #07 L33-41
- **Arabic:** "ما تخلي الخصم يخسرونها عليك"
- **English:** Don't let defenders score higher than you. Worked example: in a
  no-project hand, defenders scoring 14 vs your 12 = "khasrana ʿalaykum"
  (you lose by being out-pointed even with a contract).
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** post-deal evaluation
- **Phase:** trick play
- **Thresholds:** **bidder must out-point defender** (any margin loses; reaffirms
  Saudi rule: tied 81/81 fails the bidder; strict majority required).

### E07-4 — Bidder strategy #2: stabilize the contract by Kaboot when possible `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #07 L45-51
- **Arabic:** "تحاول تثبت اللعب يعني تجيب كبوت لازم تاكل جميع الاكلات"
- **English:** Try to "stabilize" play by going for Kaboot (sweep all 8 tricks).
  Kaboot scores higher in both Sun and Hokm — this is the second-tier goal after
  out-pointing the opponent.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** strong — when sweep is realistic
- **Phase:** trick play, especially trick 7-8
- **Thresholds:** "all 8 tricks" — full Al-Kaboot bonus.

### E07-5 — Tactic = how (technique); strategy = what (plan)

- **Source:** #07 L53-65
- **Arabic:** "تكتيك اللي هو كيف تمسك لعب كيف تروح لخويه كيف تهرب"
- **English:** Tactic = how you hold a suit, route to partner, escape, read partner,
  expect the situation, and decide whether to play your strength.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** any
- **Phase:** any
- **Thresholds:** none — vocabulary.

### E07-6 — Defender strategy #1: prevent Kaboot ("break the kaboot") `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #07 L91-101
- **Arabic:** "تحاول تكسر الكبوت تخرب الكبوت بطريقه قانونيه"
- **English:** First defender goal: break opponent's Kaboot — winning **just one
  trick** legally counts as a kaboot-break and is your "first success".
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** any (defender)
- **Phase:** trick play
- **Thresholds:** **1 defender trick won = Kaboot broken**.

### E07-7 — Defender strategy #2: out-score the bidder to set the contract `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #07 L101-109
- **Arabic:** "تحاول انت خويك تاكل حلاتها واكلات فيها اغلاط عشان تجيبه نقاط اعلى"
- **English:** Defenders try to take tricks containing high-value cards (errors)
  so their points exceed the bidder's, "losing" the contract for the bidder
  (i.e., bidder fails). Then defenders take the **full** contract score.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** any (defender)
- **Phase:** trick play
- **Thresholds:** defender > bidder ⇒ bidder bust ⇒ defender takes full score.

## Video #08 — `NW2GTyrqGXM` Smart Move (Pro-Tier J/T sacrifice)

### E08-1 — "Smart move" framing: do the OPPOSITE of natural intuition `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L21-29
- **Arabic:** "الطبيعي يسوي تصغر له ... هذه الحركه عكس تماما"
- **English:** Natural rule: when opponent has won the trick, give your **smallest**
  card. The "smart move" is the **exact opposite** — play your **largest** card.
- **Confidence:** Pro-tier (rare; "ما يسويها الا محترفين")
- **Hand-shape:** opponent leading + you have the trick-winner of suit
- **Phase:** mid-game (early or middle of round)
- **Thresholds:** signature pro deception move; deliberately discards highest.

### E08-2 — Sacrificing Shayb (J) — sends "I have the next-rank card"

- **Source:** #08 L57-65 (Sun example)
- **Arabic:** "لما لعبت له الشايب ... راح يفهم انه انت عندك الشايب تك"
- **English:** Playing your Shayb (J = "old man") tells the opponent you also hold
  the next-down card (the Q "bint"). They infer your partner does NOT hold the bint
  and won't lead toward it.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** holding J + Q in same suit; trick is lost regardless
- **Phase:** Sun, early/mid-game
- **Thresholds:** "stuck with consecutive top cards" — you lose 10 either way, so
  show the bigger one.

### E08-3 — Sacrificing the 10 — even bigger pro move `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L121-130
- **Arabic:** "ضحى بالعشره لو ليس شايف فرق العشره بعشره بالعشره هذا شيء كبير"
- **English:** Sacrificing the 10 (10 points) is much bigger — only the most
  professional players do this; "10-for-10 trade is a huge thing". Player must be
  highly confident the deception will pay off.
- **Confidence:** Master/Saudi-Master (rare)
- **Hand-shape:** holding 10 + lower trick-winners (e.g., 10+J+Q sequence)
- **Phase:** Sun mid-game
- **Thresholds:** "10-for-10 sacrifice" — professional confidence required.

### E08-4 — Goal of the smart move: force opponent's next lead into the suit `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L83-93
- **Arabic:** "هدفك الاول من هذه الحاله انه الخصم هذا يجيك في السبيت على طول"
- **English:** First goal: force opponent to lead Spades (or the same suit) on the
  next trick. Even if they win an interim trick, they should "return to spades" —
  setting up your eat with your concealed card.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** you control 2nd-rank in suit, want lead to come back
- **Phase:** mid-game
- **Thresholds:** "force return-lead in suit" objective.

### E08-5 — Why sacrifice: 4 conditional triggers `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L107-116
- **Arabic:** "اذا كان هو مشترين ... عشان تنقذك من خساره ... عشان تساوي"
- **English:** Reasons to do the sacrifice:
  1. **Break their kaboot** (if they're bidder)
  2. **Save you from loss** (you're bidder, in trouble)
  3. **Achieve SWA** (سوا — equality/draw) `**[FOCUS]**`
  4. Get partner the next-trick lead.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** any with sacrifice candidate
- **Phase:** mid-game, sometimes endgame
- **Thresholds:** SWA condition surfaces here as scoring trigger — Pro J/T sacrifice
  partly motivated by SWA goal.

### E08-6 — Pre-conditions for sacrifice: be LAST to play, not 2nd `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L141-149
- **Arabic:** "الافضل تكون انت اخر لاعب ما تكون ثاني لاعب"
- **English:** Sacrifice is best when you're **4th** to play in the trick (you've
  seen all cards). It's much weaker as 2nd player. If you're 2nd, you can still try
  it but probability of success drops.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** any with J/T sacrifice candidate
- **Phase:** trick decision
- **Thresholds:** seat-position threshold: pos 4 strongly preferred, pos 2 risky.

### E08-7 — Pre-condition: partner must have cut (qaṭa') the suit `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L149-153
- **Arabic:** "خويك لو قطع تزيد هنا احتماليه انك تسوي هذه الحركه"
- **English:** If partner has shown they're void in the same suit ("qaṭaʿ" = cut),
  the chance to safely run the sacrifice goes up.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** partner void-in-suit confirmed
- **Phase:** mid-game
- **Thresholds:** partner-void = green light for sacrifice.

### E08-8 — Pre-condition: card must be sandwiched between two of your strengths

- **Source:** #08 L153-163
- **Arabic:** "اذا هذا كمان لعب الورقه بين قوتين عندك"
- **English:** Sacrifice candidate ideally sits **between two of your strengths**
  (e.g., you hold 10 and J; opponent's Q sits between). Then sacrificing the J
  preserves both your 10 and your eventual eat.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** sandwiched-strength pattern
- **Phase:** mid-game
- **Thresholds:** "between two strengths" geometric condition.

### E08-9 — Sacrifice the 10 specifically: only when truly necessary `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L185-193
- **Arabic:** "العشره مو هي لاعب يسويها وغالبا لاعبين يخافون يضحون فيها"
- **English:** Sacrificing the 10 is rare — most players fear losing the 10.
  The professional sacrifices it when needed; amateurs hoard it.
- **Confidence:** Master
- **Hand-shape:** strong upper-suit
- **Phase:** trick decision
- **Thresholds:** psychological — "fear of losing 10" is amateur tell.

### E08-10 — Partner-coordination assumption: if partner plays high, assume they hold next-rank

- **Source:** #08 L191-200
- **Arabic:** "اذا خويك جاوب معاك ولعب ورقه كبيره دائما تفترض انه عنده الورقه اللي بعدها"
- **English:** **General reading rule:** when partner follows suit with a large card,
  assume they also hold the next-down card (sequential reading). Same logic that
  partner uses when reading you.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** partner played high
- **Phase:** any
- **Thresholds:** partner large-card → assume next-down card with them.

### E08-11 — When NOT to sacrifice: if outcome is uncertain, don't risk

- **Source:** #08 L237-256
- **Arabic:** "ممكن الحركه هذه ما تضبط معاك ... لازم برضو تحطها في راسك وتحسبها"
- **English:** Sacrifice can fail. Always **calculate** before doing it — track:
  is opponent void? Did partner cut? Did partner deny in this suit? If you can't
  rule out failure, don't sacrifice.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** any
- **Phase:** trick decision
- **Thresholds:** **mental EV check required** before pro-move execution.

### E08-12 — Hokm-tier example: feed Q (Bint) to fool re-trump `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L416-470
- **Arabic:** "وهمت انه ما عندك العشره ... هتلعب البنت ولو ما لعبت لك اكيد مئه في الميه حيربع بالتسعه"
- **English:** Hokm pro move: when bidder has trump-played J + 9 (showing both trump
  ranks), and you have Q + 10 (next trump rank): play Q first. Bidder will assume
  you don't hold 10, won't re-trump (won't "rb3" again), so you keep your 10 hidden.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** Hokm + you hold Q+10 of trump after bidder played J+9
- **Phase:** trick 2-3 of Hokm
- **Thresholds:** Hokm-only equivalent of Sun J-sacrifice; bidder must believe you're
  out of trump.

### E08-13 — Hokm Q-feed: only safe when you have ≥3 Hokm cards `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #08 L487-504
- **Arabic:** "اذا انت كنت خايف من الكب وت لو عندك ثلاثه اوراق منها يكه ما تسوي"
- **English:** Don't do the Hokm Q-deception if you're already kaboot-protected with
  ≥3 trump (one being the Ace) — you'll naturally take it without the deception.
  Only deceive when the math actually requires it.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** ≥3 trump w/ Ace = no need to deceive
- **Phase:** Hokm trick decision
- **Thresholds:** **3 trump w/ Ace = sacrifice unnecessary**.

## Video #20 — `ugE1Lsd2w-w` Control / Hold a Suit (مسك اللعب)

### E20-1 — "Hold a suit" definition

- **Source:** #20 L7-23
- **Arabic:** "تمسك شكل تمسك شكل يعني السيطر على شكل معين"
- **English:** "Mask shakl" = control a single suit by holding the highest cards
  (Ace, then 10/J, etc.) so you can win 1+ tricks in that suit. "Mask lownayn" =
  control two suits.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** has top cards in the suit
- **Phase:** post-deal evaluation
- **Thresholds:** "control" defined as holding highest remaining card in suit.

### E20-2 — Give partner the high card to **let them** hold the suit

- **Source:** #20 L43-55
- **Arabic:** "العقل يقول تعطيه اصغر ورقه ... كيف تخلي خويه يمسك اللون"
- **English:** Counter-intuitive: instead of throwing your smallest card to partner
  who took the trick, throw your 10 (or higher) to **transfer control** of the suit
  to partner. They now hold the master card next round.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** you have multiple cards in suit; partner won this trick
- **Phase:** mid-game
- **Thresholds:** transferring control via deliberate over-throw.

### E20-3 — Let weak-hand partner hold play

- **Source:** #20 L83-95
- **Arabic:** "ضعيف ممكن تخلي قويه يمسك اللعب خصوصا اذا كان اللعب في البدايه"
- **English:** If you're **weak**, let the strong (partner's) hand hold the play.
  Especially in opening tricks — you don't need to grab control yourself if you
  can't profit from it.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** weak (no top cards)
- **Phase:** opening tricks
- **Thresholds:** "weak holder" = no top cards → cede control.

### E20-4 — Benefits of suit-control: points + tricks + Kaboot disruption `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #20 L99-113
- **Arabic:** "تاخذ نقاط ومنها تاخذ اكل ومنها تاخذ حله ... تخرب كبوت"
- **English:** Holding a suit benefits: (1) points; (2) eats; (3) tricks; (4)
  multiple eats per trick; **(5) breaks opponent's Kaboot** when you grab even one
  trick from a kaboot-bound opponent.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** has suit control
- **Phase:** all
- **Thresholds:** 1 trick taken = Kaboot break (reinforces E07-6).

### E20-5 — Sun: holding 10 with one helper card → throw 10 to partner

- **Source:** #20 L141-185
- **Arabic:** "لو كان عندك 10 زي كده مردوفه بورقه واحده ... الافضل تعطيني عشره"
- **English:** Sun + you have 10 + 1 helper card in the suit + partner won this
  trick → give partner the 10 (let them hold it). With **only 1 backup** you
  can't realistically defend the 10 yourself.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** Sun, 10 + 1 in suit, partner ahead
- **Phase:** Sun trick play
- **Thresholds:** **2 cards in suit (10 + 1)** = give 10 to partner.

### E20-6 — Sun: holding 10 with 2+ helpers → option to keep 10 `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #20 L189-247
- **Arabic:** "اذا ما عندك ورق قوي نهائيا ... ممكن تعطيه العشره"
- **English:** Sun + 10 + ≥2 helpers + you have NO other strong cards (no Ace) →
  give the 10 anyway (no point hoarding). With strong hand → can keep 10. With
  3 backups, you have 3 winning tricks options regardless.
- **Confidence:** Often
- **Hand-shape:** Sun, 10 + 2-3 in suit
- **Phase:** Sun trick play
- **Thresholds:** "≥3 cards w/ 10" = consider keeping; "10 + 1 only" = give.

### E20-7 — Sun: 10 with 5+ cards → keep 10 unless sequence allows release `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #20 L257-272
- **Arabic:** "اذا كان عندك خمسه اوراق مع العشره لا ترمي العشره الا في حاله اذا كانت متتاليه"
- **English:** Sun + 10 + 4 more (5 total) → **don't throw the 10** unless the
  sequence is consecutive (10-J-Q-K or 10-J). Run lower cards first, save the 10.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** Sun, 5+ cards in suit including 10
- **Phase:** Sun trick play
- **Thresholds:** **5+ cards w/ 10** = hoard 10 (exception: consecutive run).

### E20-8 — Avoid playing high prematurely; route to partner first

- **Source:** #20 L317-333
- **Arabic:** "حاول قدر المستطاع ما تلعب ... ركز على تهريب خويك"
- **English:** When holding strong cards, **avoid leading** them prematurely.
  Priority: route ("tahreeb") to partner if they've shown they want a suit.
  Only if no eat / route / tahreeb / nothing works → spend the high card.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** holding strength
- **Phase:** mid-game
- **Thresholds:** priority order: tahreeb > eat > forced spend.

### E20-9 — Sun vs Hokm difference: Sun J/T are "Aces"; Hokm J/9 are guaranteed

- **Source:** #20 L343-409
- **Arabic:** "اكبر شيء في الصين اللي هو لكه ... في الحكم ايش اكبر ورقه الولد"
- **English:** Sun: A is the top, deception/sacrifice happens at 10 (and J/Q).
  Hokm: J (Walad) is top, **9 (Tisʿa) is 2nd** — both are guaranteed eats. So
  Hokm "sacrificing the 9" makes no sense (you're going to win that trick anyway).
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** any
- **Phase:** any (rule of contract type)
- **Thresholds:** **Hokm: 9 = guaranteed eat** (do not "sacrifice"). Sun: 10
  is sacrifice candidate.

### E20-10 — Hokm deception: send Q before 10 to suggest "no 9" `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #20 L417-470
- **Arabic:** "تلعب التسعه وهذا اخذ انت تقدر تاكل بالعشره ... وهمت انه ما عندك العشره"
- **English:** Hokm + bidder bought Hokm + you have 10 of trump → play **Q (Jada)**
  rather than **9 (Tisʿa)** if the goal is to suggest you don't hold 10. Bidder
  may then withdraw rather than re-pull trump — leaving you with the 10 to eat
  later.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** Hokm-defender + 10 of trump + Q of trump
- **Phase:** Hokm pull-trump phase
- **Thresholds:** post-bidder J-and-9 trump-pull; sends "no Tisʿa" signal.

### E20-11 — Don't use Hokm-deception when you have ≥3 trumps including A

- **Source:** #20 L487-503
- **Arabic:** "لو عندك ثلاثه اوراق منها يكه ما تسوي هذه الحركه"
- **English:** Don't deceive in Hokm if you already hold ≥3 trump including the
  Ace — you'll naturally take a trick anyway, so the deception is wasted.
- **Confidence:** Pro
- **Hand-shape:** Hokm, ≥3 trump w/ Ace
- **Phase:** Hokm trick decision
- **Thresholds:** **3 trump w/ Ace** = no deception needed.

### E20-12 — In Hokm: "force the cut" by leading non-trump that partner has cut

- **Source:** #20 L541-555
- **Arabic:** "خاص تلعب خاص عشان يدق حكم اهم شيء يقطع خويك مقطوع"
- **English:** Lead a side-suit that partner is **void** in. Partner can then
  trump (cut) it, taking the trick and asserting trump control.
- **Confidence:** Often
- **Hand-shape:** know partner is void in side-suit
- **Phase:** Hokm post-pull
- **Thresholds:** lead-void-suit-to-partner technique.

### E20-13 — Hokm: rb3 (re-pull trump) twice when controlling via 10

- **Source:** #20 L429-455
- **Arabic:** "افضل تربيه بحكم فراح تربي بالعشره هذا مضطر يطلع فوق العشره"
- **English:** When you control trump and want to "hold" the play: best to **rb3
  with Hokm** (re-pull trump). Lead the 10 — opponent must over-trump it,
  forcing them to spend high cards while you retain control.
- **Confidence:** Often
- **Hand-shape:** Hokm-controller, 10 + ≥1 more trump
- **Phase:** Hokm
- **Thresholds:** "lead 10 of trump to drag opponent's higher trumps".

## Video #21 — `Xxsf2QvaiU0` Magnify (تكبير) in Sun

### E21-1 — Definition: "Takbeer" = give partner your largest cards

- **Source:** #21 L11-16
- **Arabic:** "تكبير يعني تعطيه اكبر الاوراق اللي معك"
- **English:** **Takbeer** = give partner the largest cards in your hand,
  whether you're following suit or cutting (qaṭaʿ). NOT a contract escalation.
- **Confidence:** Always (terminology)
- **Hand-shape:** any
- **Phase:** when partner has won the trick
- **Thresholds:** **Vocabulary: Takbeer = "play your highest", not a bid level.**
  Critical glossary point.

### E21-2 — Anti-pattern: playing Q (Bint) when you also hold J = "saqart" (squashed)

- **Source:** #21 L21-26
- **Arabic:** "لو لعبت البنت انت كذا ما كبرت خويك بالعكس صقرت لاخويه"
- **English:** Playing Q when you also have J means you "squashed" your partner
  (the OPPOSITE of magnification). You wasted your J's potential by under-playing.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** holds J + Q of suit
- **Phase:** following partner-led trick
- **Thresholds:** if J held, never play lower in same trick.

### E21-3 — Benefit #1: deny opponents "abnāʾ" (children = pip points)

- **Source:** #21 L27-33
- **Arabic:** "تعطي خويك ابناء وممكن ما تعطي ابناط"
- **English:** Magnification gives partner the **abnāʾ** (point-value cards)
  while you play low pip-less cards (7, 8, 9). Sun pip values: J=2, Q=3, K=4,
  10=10, A=11.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** mixed
- **Phase:** trick decision
- **Thresholds:** numeric — Q has 3 pips, J has 2 pips → preserve them for partner.

### E21-4 — Benefit #2: signal "no card higher than this" `**[FOCUS]**`

- **Source:** #21 L35-43
- **Arabic:** "ترسل لخويك رساله ... ما عندك اكبر من هذه الورقه"
- **English:** Magnification is **a signal**: "I have no card higher than the one
  I just played." Partner reads this to plan their lead.
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** any
- **Phase:** any
- **Thresholds:** signal contract: card-played-is-highest.

### E21-5 — Sun pip-value math: J=2, Q=3 → prefer J sacrifice

- **Source:** #21 L43-51
- **Arabic:** "الشايب فين انت اعطيت خويك واعطيت نفسك ابناء اكثر لان الشايب باربعه والبنته ثلاثه"
- **English:** [Speaker uses 4 for J in Sun and 3 for Q — note may be regional
  variant]. The point: when you have J + Q + low cards, play **J** to partner
  (the higher pip), keep the Q for yourself. Hand shape with no abnāʾ → play
  9 to partner (cheaper for you).
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** J + Q + helpers
- **Phase:** trick decision
- **Thresholds:** **Sun pip table awareness required**. Numbers may vary regionally.

### E21-6 — Two patterns: consecutive (متتالي) vs non-consecutive (غير متتالي)

- **Source:** #21 L83-97
- **Arabic:** "ورقه متتالي ... او ورق غير متالي"
- **English:** Magnification has two card-shapes: **consecutive** (e.g., A+10 in
  Sun = A then 10 with no gap; J+10; Q+J; J+10+9) and **non-consecutive**
  (e.g., 10+Q with J missing).
- **Confidence:** Always
- **Hand-shape:** classification rule
