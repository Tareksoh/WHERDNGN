Terminology in this report:
- **Qaid (قيد)** = post-bid penalty call ("جيت" in some clips, treated as same word; "تجييد" / "تسجيله" / "وعَجَب" are aliases). Yields raw points to the calling team.
- **Kasho / Kosha (كوشه / تكويش)** = pre-bid table-flip / re-deal-no-points; also used as a penalty for some procedural errors (overlap with Qaid in some sessions).
- **Takweesh** is BOTH the noun "act of kasho" AND a verb form some speakers use loosely; in #29/30/34 it is the table-flip mechanic; in #36 the speaker uses "تكويش" interchangeably with Kasho.

Confidence scale: **High** (rule stated explicitly, multiple times, with example), **Med** (stated once but unambiguous), **Low** (implied / context-dependent / "بعض الجلسات").

## Video #27 — How to Bid Basics (turn order, R1 vs R2, ashkal)

- **Source:** #27 ~00:04

- **English:** Baloot divides into three phases — pre-bid (cut/deal), bid phase (الشراء), and play phase (post-bid).
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** All
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-27.2 Five bid-phase choices per player
- **Source:** #27 ~00:30
- **Arabic (≤15):** `اي لاعب مخير بين خمس خيارات`
- **English:** Each player has 5 options when their bid turn comes — buy (Hokm 1st-suit), buy (Hokm 2nd-suit), buy Sun, declare Ashkal, or pass (`بس` in R1, `ولا` in R2).
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Bid
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-27.3 Deal pattern: 3-3-2, then expose top card
- **Source:** #27 ~00:54
- **Arabic (≤15):** `توزع طبيعي ثلاثه ثلاثه ثلاثه ... بعدين اثنين ... وتكشف ورقه`
- **English:** Standard deal is 3-3-2 to each of the four players, then one card is flipped face-up on the table (the "ground card" / `الورقه اللي في الارض`). **[FOCUS]**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Deal
- **Thresholds:** Each player ends Round-1 holding 5 cards; 3 more remain undealt.

### H-27.4 Bid-turn priority starts at dealer's right
- **Source:** #27 ~01:12
- **Arabic (≤15):** `الافضليه ... للي على يمين الموزع وعنده البدايه`
- **English:** Bid-phase priority always starts with the player to the dealer's RIGHT and proceeds counter-clockwise. **[FOCUS]** (Note: same player has play priority too on hand opening.)
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —

- **Thresholds:** —

### H-27.5 R1 buy on the flipped card commits to its suit as Hokm

- **English:** In Round 1 (`اللفه الاولى` / `الدور الاول`), a Hokm bid is "Hokm 1st" — buyer takes the face-up card AND its suit becomes trump. They cannot pick a different suit. **[FOCUS]**

### H-27.6 R1 Sun bid: buyer takes the flipped card, plays it Sun

- **Arabic (≤15):** `اذا يبغاها صن ياخذ الورقه اللي في الارض`

### H-27.7 Ashkal: partner (`خويه` / `اللي قدامه`) takes the ground card

## Video #28 — Bid Rules (override, suit conversion, Hokm-2 declaration)

### H-28.1 Cannot convert Sun → Hokm after declaration
- **Source:** #28 ~00:33
- **Arabic (≤15):** `ممنوع انه يحولها من صل لحكم`
- **English:** Once you have bid Sun on a card, you may NOT later switch your contract to Hokm. Sun is locked. **[FOCUS]**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Bid R1
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-28.2 Sun bid CAN be overcalled by another player's Sun (with seat condition)
- **Source:** #28 ~00:55, ~01:32
- **Arabic (≤15):** `يكون اللاعب اللي قبله`
- **English:** A player may overcall another player's Sun bid with their OWN Sun, but only if they sit BEFORE the original bidder in turn-order priority. (Seat 1 / dealer's right has highest priority, dealer has lowest.) Sessional variation exists — some sessions allow any seat. **[FOCUS]**
- **Confidence:** High (rule); Med (the "any seat" variant is sessional)
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Bid R1 / R2
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-28.3 Sun cannot be overcalled by Hokm
- **Source:** #28 ~00:55
- **Arabic (≤15):** `الصن اكبر من الحكم والصن اقوى من الحكم`
- **English:** Once Sun is bid, no player may take it back as Hokm — Sun outranks Hokm in the bid priority. **[FOCUS]**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Bid R1
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-28.4 Sun > Hokm justified by raw point totals (26 vs 16)
- **Source:** #28 ~01:09
- **Arabic (≤15):** `الصن عدد نقاطه 26 والحكم عدد نقاطه 16`
- **English:** Sun is "stronger" because it carries 26 raw points (4 Aces + 4 Tens at 11+10 each — using A+T mardoofa scoring) vs Hokm's 16 (the trump-J = 20, trump-9 = 14 ranks shifting things, but the PENALTY-relevant raw count is 26 vs 16). **[FOCUS]** — These are the same numbers used as Qaid bonus values (#30, #36).
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Scoring / penalty math
- **Thresholds:** **Sun raw = 26, Hokm raw = 16**

### H-28.5 Hokm bid CAN be overcalled by Sun (anyone)
- **Source:** #28 ~02:53
- **Arabic (≤15):** `الصن اكبر من الحكم فمثلا لو هذا اللاعب اخذها حكم`
- **English:** If a player bids Hokm, ANY other player may overcall to Sun (and the original Hokm-bidder may also flip their own contract to Sun). Sun beats Hokm regardless of seat priority. **[FOCUS]**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Bid R1 / R2
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-28.6 Hokm cannot be overcalled by Hokm
- **Source:** #28 ~03:24
- **Arabic (≤15):** `لا فقط زي ما قلنا صن`
- **English:** A Hokm bid is NOT overcallable by another Hokm. The only escalation paths are (a) Sun overcall, (b) Ashkal (if the seat is eligible).
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Bid R1
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-28.7 Hokm-then-Ashkal allowed for eligible seats
- **Source:** #28 ~03:30
- **Arabic (≤15):** `الموزع واللي يساره يقدر يشكله عادي`

## Video #29 — Don't Takweesh Your Partner

### H-29.1 Title meaning: "takweesh" here = the KASHO mechanic
- **Source:** #29 ~00:00
- **Arabic (≤15):** `عندك ورق كوشه هل تكوش`
- **English:** "Don't takweesh your partner" refers to the KASHO call (table-flip / redeal-no-points), not the Qaid penalty call. The verb `تكوش` is the action of declaring kasho. **[FOCUS]** Important disambiguation: "takweesh" here ≠ overbidding the partner.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Pre-bid (deal phase)
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-29.2 Default rule when partner has bought: do NOT kasho
- **Source:** #29 ~00:02
- **Arabic (≤15):** `الافضل انك ما تكوش الا في حاله واحده`
- **English:** When your partner has bought (Sun or Hokm) and you happen to be holding a kasho-eligible (all 7/8/9) hand, the DEFAULT correct play is to suppress the kasho and let your partner play their contract. **[FOCUS]**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** All cards in {7,8,9}
- **Phase:** Pre-bid (deal-phase kasho window)
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-29.3 Single exception: partner hesitated AND took Sun AND game is early
- **Source:** #29 ~00:07
- **Arabic (≤15):** `شفت خويك متردد واخذ صن وكان في بدايه اللعب`
- **English:** The ONE exception where you may kasho on your partner: you observed your partner hesitate before buying, they took Sun, and the round is early in the match. In that narrow case kasho is acceptable. **[FOCUS]**
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** Kasho-eligible (5×{7,8,9})
- **Phase:** Pre-bid kasho window
- **Thresholds:** "early in the game"

### H-29.4 Suppression rationale: protecting partner's project
- **Source:** #29 ~00:28
- **Arabic (≤15):** `انت ممكن تخرب على خويك مشروع`
- **English:** Reason for suppression: partner usually only buys with strong cards / projects (sera, 50, 100, four-of-a-kind). A kasho destroys their project equity.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Pre-bid
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-29.5 If you DON'T kasho and partner loses, no blame
- **Source:** #29 ~00:18
- **Arabic (≤15):** `ما حد راح يلومك لانه اصلا خويك ما يعرف`
- **English:** Social rule: if you suppress kasho and partner subsequently loses, you are NOT blamed (partner doesn't know your hand). If you DO kasho and partner had a project, you ARE blamed. Asymmetric blame favors suppression.
- **Confidence:** High
- **Hand-shape:** —
- **Phase:** Pre-bid
- **Thresholds:** —

### H-29.6 Trust the upcoming context cards
- **Source:** #29 ~00:41
- **Arabic (≤15):** `خليك واثق في ورق السياق اللي راح يجيك`

### X-1 Sun-vs-Hokm raw points (26 vs 16) are LOAD-BEARING constants

### X-2 Multiplier scope rule (Qaid base × M, melds × M, BUT see #36 H-36.13)
- The escalation multiplier scope EXPLICITLY includes Qaid base AND own-meld bonus.
- This DIVERGES from the standard "Belote (K+Q of trump) is multiplier-immune" rule used in normal scoring. **[FOCUS]** The video does not address whether belote-meld is also multiplied under Qaid; the existing CLAUDE.md rule says belote is multiplier-immune, so the audit should verify Qaid's scope vs Rules.lua.

### X-3 Two distinct penalties confirmed (Kasho ≠ Qaid)
- Confirmed across #30 (entirety) and #36. The "two-distinct-penalties" claim in WHEREDNGN docs is **CORRECT**.
- **Kasho** (pre-bid, redeal-no-points, "light"): hand-shape trigger, declared cheating in deal phase, sometimes accidental-card-drop.
- **Qaid** (post-bid, points-bonus, "severe"): failed-follow / failed-ruff / failed-overtrump / undeclared meld / observed cheat / verbal slips / count error.

### X-4 Hokm-bid pre-conditions: NOT explicitly "must have an Ace"
- Across #27 and #28, the videos describe HOW Hokm bidding works mechanically — but DO NOT state an explicit "must have a side-suit Ace" pre-condition for the Hokm bid.
- The closest the videos get is showing example hands (e.g. #27 ~03:25: "ولد + 9 of spades" → buy Hokm) — these examples include a side-suit Ace IMPLICITLY but the rule is not stated.
- **[FOCUS]** The "side-Ace minimum for J+3-trump Hokm" appears to be a STRATEGIC guideline (likely from #26 / other strategy videos), NOT a stated bidding RULE in this cluster.

### X-5 Sun bid pre-conditions: NOT stated mechanically in this cluster
- These 6 videos do NOT discuss "A+T mardoofa requirement" or "3+ Aces direct-Sun preference" or quantitative thresholds for Sun.
- They only state the OVERRIDE / TURN-ORDER mechanics.
- The Sun threshold strategy material is presumably in other strategy videos (e.g. #26).

### X-6 Ashkal-eligibility: dealer + dealer's-LEFT only
- Confirmed in BOTH #27 (H-27.8) and #28 (H-28.7).
- This is the canonical "3rd/4th seat only" rule the audit asks about. **[FOCUS]**
