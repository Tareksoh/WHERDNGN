# WHEREDNGN v0.5 Bot Improvement Campaign — Final Synthesis Report

**Campaign dates:** 2026-05-04 (research) / 2026-05-04 (Sprint A: v0.5.0) / 2026-05-04 (Sprints B-H: v0.5.1)
**Agents used:** 20 of 300 authorized
**Patches shipped:** 15 total (7 in v0.5.0, 8 in v0.5.1)
**Baseline evidence:** `bot_baseline_metrics.json` (v0.4.13), `bot_baseline_metrics_sprint_A.json` (v0.5.0), `bot_baseline_metrics_sprint_BCDH.json` (v0.5.1)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The v0.5 campaign audited the WHEREDNGN Saudi Baloot WoW addon bot across 20 swarm agents, finding 5 critical structural defects and 9 high-priority strategy gaps. All 5 critical defects were patched across two release sprints (v0.5.0 and v0.5.1), alongside 10 of the high-priority items. The most significant measurable win: Saudi Master now runs ISMCTS — it was dead code in v0.4.13, producing byte-identical metrics to M3lm across all tournament configurations. Post-campaign, Master-vs-Basic forced tournaments flip from a Basic win to a Master win; Master avg gp/round in mixed-natural climbed from 8.8 to 11.7 (+33%). Three items remain deferred: `aceLate` / `leadCount` style-ledger wires and a future calibration sprint for the Bel strength formula when Four/Gahwa still fail to fire in symmetric bot play.

## 2. What Was Found — The 5 Critical Structural Defects

### C-1: Saudi Master ISMCTS was dead code

`Bot.PickPlay` routed directly to `pickLead` / `pickFollow` without first consulting `BotMaster.PickPlay`. The ISMCTS sampler was reachable only via Net.lua's `MaybeRunBot` — meaning AFK recovery, error-fallback paths, and every test-harness call ran raw heuristics regardless of the `saudiMasterBots` flag. Empirical proof: all_master and all_m3lm tournaments produced identical output across all 6 configurations (bel\_rate, sweep\_rate, avg\_gp\_delta all matching to 4 decimal places).

**Fix (v0.5.0):** 5-line delegation added at the top of `Bot.PickPlay`, gated by a new `Bot._inRollout` boolean that BotMaster sets before calling heuristic sub-picks to prevent recursive ISMCTS invocation.

### C-2: Bots never initiated SWA

`Bot.PickSWA` did not exist. Bots could only passively accept human SWA claims via the auto-approve flow; they never called the rest themselves even when holding an unbeatable hand. With 3-4 cards remaining, an unbeatable position occurs in roughly 5-15% of trick-7 positions — all of those points leaked to opponents trick-by-trick.

**Fix (v0.5.1):** New `Bot.PickSWA(seat)` function added. Triggers when the bot holds the highest unplayed card in every remaining suit. Wired into MaybeRunBot before the play-decision branch, with instant-claim for ≤3 cards and 5-second auto-approve flow for ≤4 cards.

### C-3: Bel/Triple/Four/Gahwa thresholds unreachable in pure-bot play

At BOT_BEL_TH=70, empirical test across 1000 random symmetric bot hands showed Bel firing at 4.2% of hands with 50% precision (coin-flip). The threshold was calibrated for asymmetric human hands where one side receives J+9+A-of-trump clustering. In symmetric random distribution, no defender hand clears 70. Style ledgers (`gahwaFailed`, `sunFail`, etc.) accumulated zero data, making all tier-4 features that depend on them effectively dead.

**Fix (two parts):** C-3a (v0.5.0): threshold lowered 70 → 60; F1 doubles from 0.137 to 0.286 in the calibration dataset. C-3b (v0.5.1): defender strength formula extended to add void-suit count × 5 (ruff potential) and extra-Ace count × 8 (sustained trick power). Four/Gahwa still do not fire in natural mode — the structural root cause (symmetric distribution vs. human hand asymmetry) is documented as a remaining gap.

### C-4: No last-trick bonus or Al-Kaboot awareness

`LAST_TRICK_BONUS` (10 raw points) was referenced zero times in Bot.lua. `pickFollow` pos-4 used `lowestByRank(winners)` on trick 8, meaning the bot won the final trick with a 7 when a Ten could have won it — surrendering 10 (bonus) + face value delta. Al-Kaboot pursuit (team at 7/0 → 250 raw bonus on trick 8) was likewise absent; bots played trick 8 identically to trick 1.

**Fix (v0.5.1):** `pickFollow` pos-4 on trick 8 now uses `highestByFaceValue` instead of `lowestByRank`. `pickLead` on trick 8 prefers boss cards; when the team has won 7 consecutive tricks, it enters Al-Kaboot pursuit mode with fully aggressive lead selection.

### C-5: numWorlds direction was backwards

`BotMaster.lua` allocated 30 worlds at trick 1 (maximum uncertainty, most valuable decision point) and 100 worlds at trick 6+ (near-deterministic state, least valuable). The inversion was first flagged in the 200-agent MASTER_REPORT campaign as finding H-2, then incorrectly marked resolved in the v0.4.7 audit. It was still present in production at v0.4.13.

**Fix (v0.5.0):** 4-line edit inverts the scaling: 100 worlds for tricks 1-2, 60 for tricks 3-5, 30 for tricks 6-8. Early-trick rollout sampling noise reduced by approximately 50%.

## 5. What Is Deferred

### `aceLate` and `leadCount` counters (H-9 partial)

`leadCount[suit]` is written by `OnPlayObserved` but read by zero pickers. `aceLate` is read only by the sampler's `pickProb`, not by any play picker. The `triples` counter was wired in v0.5.1; the other two remain dead infrastructure. Wiring `aceLate` into late-trick trump decisions and `leadCount` into the suit-establishment heuristic are the most plausible payoffs, but both require deciding on a behavioral policy before writing code.

### Bel/Four/Gahwa in natural bot play (calibration)

The escalation chain above Bel (Triple, Four, Gahwa) will not fire in natural symmetric bot play until either: (a) the test harness generates asymmetric hands matching human deal distributions, or (b) the strength formula is rebuilt from playtest data gathered from human games. The v0.5 campaign cannot address this without real game telemetry. A separate calibration sprint — ideally after v0.5 ships to production and generates player game logs — is the recommended path.

### Medium findings (M-1 through M-10)

None of the 10 medium/informational findings from the original research report were addressed. The most consequential are M-3 (heuristicPick rollout doesn't update voids mid-simulation) and M-4 (cheapest-winner cross-scale comparison in pickFollow). Both are self-contained and can be landed as standalone patches without risk.

### Bot SWA validation in real play

The C-2 implementation passes static analysis and syntax check, but SWA trigger rate in natural play is unknown — the test harness does not generate hands near the SWA threshold. A targeted playtest fixture generating ≤4-card unbeatable positions is recommended before v0.6.

## 6. Lessons Learned

### What worked

**Convergence at 20 agents, not 300.** The research campaign found every critical structural defect in the first 10 agents. Agents 11-20 produced patches for those defects. Prior 50-agent and 200-agent campaigns had already surfaced the same major issues (MASTER_REPORT, v0.4.7 audit). Running the remaining 280 agents would have re-discovered the same findings. The useful signal from additional agents past 20 was approximately zero. Bounded campaigns with early-convergence detection are more efficient than large flat swarms for this codebase.

**Empirical playtest scenarios found bugs static review missed.** The byte-identical M3lm/Master result in the 100-round tournament was the decisive proof of C-1. Static code reading confirmed the missing delegation; the tournament proved it was the production symptom. Similarly, the forced-mode Four/Gahwa 0% confirmed the threshold unreachability more clearly than any code audit.

**Four clean-applying patches out of 11 are worth the full campaign.** Even when most agent-generated diffs had line-anchor drift, the four that applied cleanly (C-3a, C-5, H-1, H-2) delivered measurable wins immediately. The design-spec patches (C-1, C-2, C-3b, C-4, H-4, H-5) served as accurate implementation specs even when the diffs could not be applied as-is.

### What did not work

**Line-anchor drift in agent-generated diffs required manual application.** 7 of the 11 original patches from the campaign were labeled "design-spec (line drift)" and could not be applied via `git apply`. The agent prompts specified file and approximate line ranges, but the generated diffs referenced stale line numbers from intermediate analysis states. For a codebase under active development, diff generation is best done at the moment of application against the live file rather than speculatively during research. For future campaigns, prefer semantic patch descriptions (function name + insertion site description) over line-anchored diffs.

## 7. Recommendations for v0.6+

**Priority 1 — Bel calibration from real game data.** The escalation chain is structurally correct but numerically miscalibrated for the symmetric bot distribution. If v0.5 ships and players generate game logs, extract defender hand profiles from rounds where a human chose to Bel (and won vs. lost) and refit BOT_BEL_TH and the strength formula against that corpus. This is a one-shot calibration that would unlock the Four/Gahwa chain.

**Priority 2 — `aceLate` and `leadCount` wire-up or removal.** These are the last remaining dead-counter items from H-9. Decision: either define the policy (if `aceLate >= 2`, prefer A-of-trump in late-trick leads; if `leadCount[suit] >= 3`, penalize void-suit leads) and wire them, or delete them and remove the accumulation code. Leaving them as dead writes is technical debt with no upside.

**Priority 3 — SWA playtest fixture.** Write a test that generates hands with ≤4 cards remaining where one team holds all winners. Verify `Bot.PickSWA` triggers correctly and that the MaybeRunBot wire fires before `PickPlay`. This closes the validation gap noted in section 5.

**Priority 4 — M-3: rollout void tracking.** `heuristicPick` inside the ISMCTS rollout does not update void information as it simulates tricks. This means the rollout's internal evaluation diverges from real play — a position where a simulated player voids a suit triggers no suit-voiding inference. Fixing this improves rollout accuracy for tricks 3-8 and is contained to BotMaster.lua.

**Priority 5 — Tier-ordering re-verification.** Now that Saudi Master and M3lm are empirically distinct, the M3lm-vs-Advanced boundary should be measured the same way: 100-round mixed tournament. If the ordering is already correct (Advanced < M3lm < Master), document it. If not, adjust thresholds.

**Non-priority — Cosmetic M-findings (M-5 through M-10).** These are real gaps but none are load-bearing for player experience at the current release quality level. Address them opportunistically when touching the relevant code, not as a dedicated sprint.
