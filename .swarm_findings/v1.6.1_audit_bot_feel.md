# v1.6.1 Bot Feel & Pacing Audit

## TL;DR

- Total issues: 18
- CRITICAL: 1 (no thinking indicator at all — game looks frozen on bot turn)
- HIGH: 6 (uniform pacing flattens tier feel and decision difficulty)
- MEDIUM: 8 (voice-cue overlap, missed escalation rhythm, leaked tells)
- LOW: 3 (polish)

Top 3 fixes that would meaningfully improve real-game "feel":

1. **BF-01 (HIGH) — Single uniform `BOT_DELAY_BID`/`PLAY`/`BEL` constants**
   (`Net.lua:4301-4303`). Three flat constants for ALL decisions at ALL
   tiers. A 4-Aces forced auto-Sun and a borderline pos-3-strong-Hokm-vs-Sun
   take exactly 1.6s; a singleton-legal play and a 5-card legal pos-4 fork
   take exactly 1.2s. Make the delay a function of `(decision, tier,
   difficulty score)`: easy + low tier → ~0.6s; hard + Saudi Master →
   3-5s; with ±20% jitter so bot pacing reads as human-variable.

2. **BF-10 (CRITICAL) — Zero "bot is thinking" indicator anywhere in the
   UI** (`UI.lua` searched: no `thinking` / `spinner` / `Bot is`
   string; `turnGlow` is a static texture). On a bot turn the user sees
   only the seat-glow, identical to the AFK-stuck human-seat state. Add a
   per-bot pulsing glow OR a small "…" dot/spinner anchored on the bot's
   seat badge while `BOT_DELAY_*` is in flight.

3. **BF-21 (HIGH) — `BOT_DELAY_BEL=1.4s` < BEL/Triple/Four/Gahwa MP3
   length (~2.5-3s)** (`Net.lua:4303` vs `sounds/BEL.mp3`=51KB MP3 ≈ 3s).
   In an open chain (Bel → Triple → Four → Gahwa), each rung's MP3 is
   still playing when the next decision fires. With `VOICE_INTERVAL=0.80s`
   throttle (`Sound.lua:33`), Triple's voice is suppressed silently OR
   overlaps mid-word. Either lengthen `BOT_DELAY_BEL` to ≥3s, OR sequence
   the rung dispatch through a "voice-clear" callback.

## Decision pacing — uniform vs variable

### BF-01: Three flat constants drive every bot decision regardless of difficulty
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptom:** A bot pondering a borderline Sun-vs-Hokm bid pauses for
  the same 1.6s as a bot mechanically auto-passing on a 7-8-8-9-9 R1.
  A pro pauses 4-6s on a hard play and snaps in <1s on the obvious one;
  the bot is monotone.
- **Root cause:** `Net.lua:4301-4303` defines `BOT_DELAY_BID = 1.6`,
  `BOT_DELAY_PLAY = 1.2`, `BOT_DELAY_BEL = 1.4` as module-level locals,
  used unmodified in every `C_Timer.After(...)` dispatch
  (`Net.lua:4429, 4482, 4533, 4579, 4710, 4761`).
- **Fix sketch:** Compute a per-decision difficulty score: number of
  legal options, score-margin from threshold, escalation depth, etc.
  Multiply base delay by 0.5-3.0× and add jitter `0.85-1.15`. Delay
  function table by tier — Basic: flat 0.8s; Saudi Master: variable
  1.0-5.0s scaled by ISMCTS world-count and tactical fork count.

### BF-02: Forced auto-Sun on 4-Aces still waits the full BID delay
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** A bot dealt 4 Aces returns `K.BID_SUN` instantly at
  `Bot.lua:1589-1592` — the line `if aceCount >= 4 then return K.BID_SUN end`
  is the very first branch. Yet the dispatch still parks for 1.6s before
  the Sun voice cue fires. The hand is a SLAM-DUNK Saudi tell ("forced
  Sun") but the bot pretends it took a full second to think.
- **Root cause:** `BOT_DELAY_BID` is fixed at the timer scheduling layer
  (`Net.lua:4710`); the picker has no signal back to the dispatcher
  about the decision difficulty.
- **Fix sketch:** Add a `Bot.PickBidWithMeta` that returns
  `(bid, difficulty, forcedReason)`; when `forcedReason == "S-4"` (4 Aces)
  or `forcedReason == "auto-pass-r1"`, snap to ~0.4s. A real Saudi
  player slaps the table at this hand.

### BF-03: All-pass round 1 takes 1.6×4 = 6.4s of mechanical waiting
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** When all four bots have weak R1 hands, the user sits
  through four 1.6s pauses to watch four `K.BID_PASS` resolve in turn.
  At a real table this would be 4×~0.5s of finger-snaps and
  shrugged "بَسْ" calls — pacing should ACCELERATE on a clearly-cold
  bidding round.
- **Root cause:** Each pass is independently scheduled with
  `BOT_DELAY_BID` (`Net.lua:4710`) — no awareness of "this is a hot/cold
  bid round".
- **Fix sketch:** When the dispatcher sees prior bids in the round are
  all `K.BID_PASS` AND the bot's pick is also `K.BID_PASS`, halve
  the delay. Each subsequent pass accelerates more. Conversely
  HOLD when a non-pass arrives mid-round so the contention "feels"
  meaningful.

### BF-04: Singleton-legal play burns the full 1.2s with no decision
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** `Bot.PickPlay` returns `legal[1]` instantly when
  `#legal == 1` (`Bot.lua:6557`). Forced-follow plays — ~30% of all
  trick positions in Hokm — wait 1.2s anyway. A Saudi player playing
  a singleton you-must-follow card slaps it down in <0.5s.
- **Root cause:** Same fixed-constant problem at `Net.lua:4761`.
- **Fix sketch:** Have the picker return `(card, "forced")` for the
  singleton case; dispatcher reads the meta and snaps to ~0.4s.

### BF-05: Saudi Master tier plays as fast as Basic (despite ISMCTS budget)
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptom:** `BotMaster.PickPlay` runs an ISMCTS sampler with a
  0.5s wall-clock budget (`Constants.lua:645` —
  `K.BOT_ISMCTS_BUDGET_SEC = 0.5`). The dispatcher then waits
  `BOT_DELAY_PLAY = 1.2s` REGARDLESS. So Saudi Master spends ≤0.5s
  thinking, then 0.7s of dead silence. Worse: Basic and Advanced
  return their pick in microseconds and ALSO wait 1.2s of dead
  silence. **The "tactical/calculator — long pauses" personality
  from `bot-personalities.md:81` is structurally absent**: a Saudi
  Master cannot actually pause longer than Basic.
- **Root cause:** Tier dispatch at `Bot.PickPlay` (`Bot.lua:6533-6548`)
  picks by tier but does NOT return a tier-aware delay. `Net.lua:4761`
  is tier-blind.
- **Fix sketch:** Saudi Master at hard decisions = up to 4-5s pause
  (genuinely use ISMCTS budget AND additional fake-think). Basic at
  easy decisions = 0.4s. Tie the dispatcher delay to `Bot.IsBasic()` /
  `Bot.IsM3lm()` / etc. AND to a returned-difficulty-meta from picker.

### BF-06: Bel decision (vs skip) fixed at 1.4s — way faster than Saudi 5s window
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptom:** Per `_transcripts/11_bel_beginners_extracted.md:13`,
  Saudi convention gives ~5 seconds for a defender to declare Bel —
  this is the actual SOCIAL window of suspense. The bot fires its Bel
  decision in 1.4s flat (`Net.lua:4303` × 4 dispatch sites). The
  defender-bot's Bel (or skip) lands before the user can even read
  "contract:" — losing the entire Bel-window drama.
- **Root cause:** `BOT_DELAY_BEL` is a constant; the Bel decision
  happens via `B.Bot.PickDouble(belSeat)` at `Net.lua:4372` inside the
  same MaybeRunBot loop at zero delay (sync), and the `belPending`
  iteration short-circuits as soon as ONE bot says yes.
- **Fix sketch:** Specifically for Bel/Triple/Four/Gahwa, set the
  base delay to 3.0-4.5s with tier-jitter. **Saudi Master should
  pause longer before Gahwa than before a routine Bel** — escalation-
  rung-aware delay scaling.

## Voice cue density / overlap

### BF-10: No "bot is thinking" indicator anywhere in the UI
- **Severity:** CRITICAL (immersion-breaking; user sees frozen UI)
- **Symptom:** When a bot's turn arrives, NOTHING distinguishes
  "bot is computing for 0.5s + waiting 0.7s" from "human seat
  AFK / WoW client frozen". The seat's `turnGlow` (a static gold
  alpha texture, `UI.lua:170, 1182-1190, 1613-1616`) is the entire
  UI signal. There is no spinner, no "…", no animated dots, no
  pulsing avatar, no breathing border. Searched UI.lua for any
  bot-aware feedback — zero matches for `thinking`, `spinner`,
  `Bot is`, `botPending`.
- **Root cause:** `Refresh()` at `UI.lua:2545-2551` shows the same
  static glow for any seat where `S.s.turn == seat`, regardless of
  whether that seat is bot or human. `U.PulseTurn()` at `UI.lua:3627`
  exists but only fires for AFK pre-warn (the local-bar red flash),
  never for "bot is thinking".
- **Fix sketch:** When `info.isBot AND S.s.turn == seat`, animate the
  seat's `avatar` (rotation or breathing alpha) OR show a small "…"
  text next to the bot's name. Stop animation as soon as a bid/play
  is broadcast. Consider tier-flavored animation: Saudi Master pulses
  more deliberately (signaling tactical depth); Basic flickers fast.

### BF-11: BEL.mp3 / three.mp3 / four.mp3 / gahwa.mp3 are ~3s but BOT_DELAY_BEL is 1.4s
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptom:** In an open Bel chain, bot at seat 2 Bels → BEL.mp3
  fires (3s long). 1.4s later, bot at seat 1 (bidder) decides Triple
  → three.mp3 fires WHILE BEL.mp3 is still playing. The
  `VOICE_INTERVAL = 0.80s` throttle (`Sound.lua:33`) is per-soundId,
  so different cues can stack. Result: garbled audio overlap on the
  most dramatic moment of the round.
- **Root cause:** `Net.lua:4303` set `BOT_DELAY_BEL = 1.4` based on a
  comment from when the cues were short OGG (~0.6s). User-supplied
  v1.0.2 MP3s are much longer but the delay was never bumped.
  File sizes confirm: BEL.mp3 50.9KB, three.mp3 47.2KB, four.mp3
  50.1KB, gahwa.mp3 51.8KB — all ~3s at 128kbps.
- **Fix sketch:** Either bump `BOT_DELAY_BEL` to 3.5-4.5s, OR play
  the voice cue at MaybeRunBot dispatch *before* the timer (so
  the "thinking" pause overlaps with the prior cue clearing).
  Better: add per-cue duration to a constant table `K.SND_DURATIONS`
  and have `BOT_DELAY_BEL = math.max(durationOfPrevCue, baseThinkTime)`.

### BF-12: Round-end cue cluster (TRICK_WON + SWEEP_TRACK + LAST_TRICK_WIN + KABOOT/BALOOT/HOKM_LOST) all fire in <1s
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** End of trick 8 in a Gahwa round won by local team's
  Kaboot: `SND_TRICK_WON` (`State.lua:1557`) → `SND_LAST_TRICK_WIN`
  (`State.lua:1693`) → on round-end `SND_KABOOT` and possibly
  `SND_BALOOT`. Source comments at `State.lua:1615-1619` explicitly
  call this "a single coherent close-of-round audio moment" but the
  cues are not actually sequenced — they all fire from `ApplyTrickEnd`
  / `ApplyRoundEnd` within microseconds. The throttle is per-cue, so
  4 different cues all play simultaneously = audio mush.
- **Root cause:** Each cue fires unconditionally inside its own
  `B.Sound.Cue` call, all within the same animation frame.
- **Fix sketch:** Build a cue queue with priority ordering:
  HOKM_LOST > KABOOT > BALOOT > LAST_TRICK_WIN > SWEEP_TRACK >
  TRICK_WON. Play one at a time spaced 0.6-0.8s apart; suppress
  same-bucket second wins.

### BF-13: AKA voice and card play fire within 150ms — no announce beat
- **Severity:** LOW
- **Symptom:** `S.ApplyAKA` (`State.lua:1809`) → `SND_VOICE_AKA` then
  immediately `S.ApplyPlay` → `SND_CARD_PLAY`. "إكَهْ"+swish+slap all
  within ~150ms — no "I'm announcing the boss" beat.
- **Root cause:** `Net.lua:4904-4925` synchronous ordering.
- **Fix sketch:** 300-500ms gap between AKA voice and card play.

### BF-14: Trump-cut cue overlaps card-play SFX
- **Severity:** LOW
- **Symptom:** When a bot ruffs, `SND_TRUMP_CUT` (`State.lua:1508`)
  and `SND_CARD_PLAY` (`State.lua:1479`) fire near-simultaneously
  inside the same `S.ApplyPlay` call. Slap+stinger overlap.
- **Fix sketch:** Trump-cut delay 200-300ms after the card-play SFX.

### BF-15: Belote (BALOOT!) auto-fire stacks 3 cues in <200ms
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** `Net._HostMaybeAutoBelote` (`Net.lua:4944`) on bot
  K-or-Q of trump play stacks `card_play.ogg` + `baloot.mp3` (~3s)
  + possibly `SND_TRICK_WON`.
- **Fix sketch:** Defer BALOOT cue 0.5s after card-play; push
  trick-end cluster 0.5s after BALOOT if imminent.

## "Bot is thinking" transparency

### BF-20: Overcall window: bot decisions land instant; user sees 5s of "nothing happening"
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptom:** Per `Net.lua:1443-1455`, on Sun-overcall window open,
  ALL bot seats record their decision SYNCHRONOUSLY (zero think-time)
  and fire `MSG_OVERCALL_DECISION`. The 5s timer then runs to
  completion. From the user's POV: contract appears, banner says
  "5s left · 3/4 decided" within 100ms, then 5 seconds of frozen
  countdown waiting on the user's own decision (or auto-WLA). All
  drama compressed into one frame.
- **Root cause:** Comment at `Net.lua:1443-1444` rationalizes:
  "Bots act immediately (their Bot.PickOvercall is deterministic
  given hand + thresholds; no need to wait 5s for them)". Logically
  correct — but emotionally robotic.
- **Fix sketch:** Stagger bot overcall decisions over 1-3.5 seconds
  with tier-aware delays. Sun-take-over decisions should pause
  longer than waive (the bot is "considering"). The user sees
  decisions land one-by-one — much more like a real table.

### BF-21: AFK timer is human-only; bot turns have NO timeout/visible-progress surface
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** `N.StartTurnTimer` at `Net.lua:4127` early-returns
  on `info.isBot`. `N.StartLocalWarn` at `Net.lua:4042-4119` is
  local-only. So when waiting on a bot, the user has NO timer
  surface — no countdown, no AFK pre-warn, no indicator of
  *anything*. By contrast a human seat shows a visible AFK pulse
  at T-10s. Bot dispatch could fail silently (one of the 9 known
  pcall recovery paths in MaybeRunBot) and the user has no
  feedback distinguishing "bot still thinking" from "bot picker
  errored, recovery pending".
- **Root cause:** Architectural — bots are presumed instant /
  reliable; UI doesn't model bot-pending state.
- **Fix sketch:** When a bot is the active turn, render a small
  "computing…" or animated dot on the bot's seat badge, matched
  to the actual `BOT_DELAY_*` deadline. On dispatch error /
  pcall recovery, show "thinking…" → "recovering" so user sees
  the table isn't dead.

### BF-22: Chained MaybeRunBot dispatches have no visible separator
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** Inside `MaybeRunBot` (`Net.lua:4309+`), a single bot
  decision can chain THREE successive bot turns (e.g., bidder bot
  resolves Bel-skip → Triple decision fires → Four eligibility
  decided → Gahwa). Each step calls `N.MaybeRunBot()` recursively
  with a 1.4s delay. To the user this looks like 3 silent freeze-
  pauses then suddenly all decisions appear. No "next decision is
  upcoming" anchor.
- **Root cause:** `Net.lua:4385, 4458, 4511` etc. recursively call
  MaybeRunBot from inside the timer callback after a previous
  decision is broadcast. Visual flow doesn't surface the
  "considering next rung" state.
- **Fix sketch:** Between rungs, briefly show a small phase label
  ("considering Triple…" / "Four window…") that fades during the
  bot's BOT_DELAY_BEL.

### BF-23: Preempt: 3 bot decisions cascade through one shared timer
- **Severity:** LOW
- **Symptom:** `Net.lua:4576-4699` — first eligible bot scheduled
  on a single 1.4s timer; subsequent bots re-enter via
  MaybeRunBot. Decisions visually collapse.
- **Fix sketch:** Stagger preempt-decision timers 200-600ms apart.

## Partner-bot signal feel

### BF-30: AKA banner is the ONLY visible partner signal — Tahreeb/Bargiya/style-ledger reads have ZERO surfacing
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptom:** The partner-bot at M3lm+ tiers reads partner's
  Tahreeb signals (5 forms), Bargiya (Ace tahreeb 2-flavor), and
  per-partner style ledger (Bot.lua extensively documents these
  at `bot-personalities.md:158-167`). The partner-bot may "see"
  via these signals that the bidder wants a specific suit led.
  But the human teammate sees NOTHING. AKA banner exists
  (`UI.lua:3469-3490`); zero matches for `Tahreeb` / `Bargiya` /
  `partnerHint` in UI.lua.
- **Root cause:** Saudi convention reads these signals from
  played cards (no explicit voice line). Bot honors them
  internally but the UI doesn't surface "partner is signaling
  diamonds via Tahreeb" — so the human partner has to figure it
  out alone, AND can't tell if the bot is honoring the partner's
  human-played signals.
- **Fix sketch:** Add a small text bubble above the partner-bot's
  seat: "reading: Tahreeb ♥ low" / "responding to your Bargiya".
  Tier-gated (M3lm+ only — Basic/Advanced shouldn't show "I'm
  reading you" because they aren't). Adjustable verbosity slash
  command. This is the single biggest "aha-moment" UX gap — the
  bot IS playing partner-coord, just invisibly.

### BF-31: Bot-side AKA noise (3% probability) has no tell-back to user
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** Saudi Master tier emits ~3% noise-AKA on
  second-highest cards (`Net.lua:4910-4925`, `Bot.PickAKANoise`
  at `Bot.lua:6484+`). This is a deliberate deceptive signal
  to opps. From the partner human's perspective, the noise-AKA
  is INDISTINGUISHABLE from a real AKA — they get the same
  banner. So if the human partner trusts the AKA and leads into
  it, they're being fooled by their own teammate. A real Saudi
  partnership would have at least an after-the-fact reveal.
- **Root cause:** `S.ApplyAKA` at `State.lua:1803` is signal-
  agnostic; doesn't differentiate noise-AKA from real-AKA.
- **Fix sketch:** Either disable noise-AKA when partner is human,
  OR after the trick reveal that the AKA was noise via post-hoc
  text "(noise call — partner held lower)".

### BF-32: Bel reason (confident-vs-desperation) not surfaced
- **Severity:** LOW
- **Symptom:** "Bel because I'm certain" vs "Bel because score-
  desperation comeback" feel identical to viewers.
- **Fix sketch:** Optional reason-string from PickDouble, tooltip on
  DOUBLE badge for partner-team only.

## Tier-feel differentiation

### BF-40: Bid pacing is identical across all 5 tiers
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptom:** From `bot-personalities.md:81-85`: M3lm = "studious player",
  Saudi Master = "tactical/calculator — long pauses, computes precise
  reads". Per code, all tiers schedule via `BOT_DELAY_BID = 1.6` flat.
  No `Bot.IsAdvanced/IsM3lm/IsFzloky/IsSaudiMaster` references in
  Net.lua (zero matches found). The tier difference is purely
  STRATEGIC, not EXPERIENTIAL — same bid arrives at same speed. To a
  human at the table the only tier difference is "did they bid Sun
  more often?" — completely invisible at the moment of bid.
- **Root cause:** Net.lua dispatch is tier-blind by design.
- **Fix sketch:** Per-tier base delays:
  Basic 0.6s / Advanced 1.0s / M3lm 1.4s / Fzloky 1.7s / SM 2.2s;
  each ±30% jitter. Plus difficulty-scaling (BF-01).

### BF-41: Play pacing same flat 1.2s for all tiers
- **Severity:** MEDIUM (related: BF-05 covers Saudi Master specifically)
- **Symptom:** Same as BF-40 but for play phase. Even the user's
  description framework — "feels different at the table" — is
  unreachable when every tier paces identically.
- **Root cause:** `Net.lua:4761` constant.
- **Fix sketch:** As BF-40.

### BF-42: ISMCTS budget is wasted / unused as a "tactical pause" cue
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** `BotMaster.PickPlay` budget is 0.5s wall-clock
  (`Constants.lua:645`). That budget is the literal "thinking time"
  of the strongest bot — but the dispatcher then waits 1.2s flat
  REGARDLESS of how much the budget was actually used. So Saudi
  Master "thinks 0.5s + dead-pauses 0.7s" while Basic "thinks 0.001s
  + dead-pauses 1.199s". The budget is invisible to feel.
- **Root cause:** Dispatcher doesn't see `BotMaster._lastWorldsCompleted`
  or actual think-time.
- **Fix sketch:** When tier == Saudi Master, set delay = max(actual
  think time, 1.5s) + scaled by complexity. On a hard fork (multiple
  near-equal world scores) the bot should pause 4-5s. On an easy
  forced-follow, snap.

### BF-43: "Aggressive vs patient" personality flavors unimplemented
- **Severity:** LOW
- **Symptom:** Per `bot-personalities.md:78`: "aggressive/reckless =
  Bels often, snaps fast" vs "patient/conservative = pauses long" —
  no pacing implementation. All tiers Bel/skip at 1.4s.
- **Fix sketch:** Aggressive snaps in <0.7s; conservative 3-4s.

## Escalation rhythm

### BF-50: Gahwa decision uses same 1.4s as routine Bel
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Symptom:** Gahwa is a MATCH-WIN call — the most consequential
  decision in Saudi Baloot. Yet `Bot.PickGahwa` dispatches via the
  same `BOT_DELAY_BEL = 1.4` (`Net.lua:4533`) as a routine Bel. A
  real Saudi player would pause 5-10 seconds before calling Gahwa,
  weighing the match. The bot snaps it in 1.4s.
- **Root cause:** `BOT_DELAY_BEL` is used for ALL escalation rungs
  uniformly.
- **Fix sketch:** Rung-aware delays:
  Bel 2.0s / Triple 2.5s / Four 3.0s / Gahwa 4.5s base.
  Each rung is louder/heavier; pacing should reflect.

### BF-51: Cascading Bel→Triple→Four→Gahwa in <6 seconds is anti-climactic
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Symptom:** Open chain: Bel (1.4s + voice ~3s) → Triple (1.4s
  + voice ~3s) → Four (1.4s + voice ~3s) → Gahwa (1.4s + voice
  ~3s). Total decision time: 5.6s. Voices: 12s queued. The
  decisions LAND in 5.6s but the audio is still firing for ~6s
  more — total "moment of escalation" lasts longer for the AUDIO
  than for the gameplay decisions. The drama beat is broken.
- **Root cause:** BF-11 (voice longer than delay) compounds with
  BF-50 (same delay all rungs).
- **Fix sketch:** Sync delays to actual voice length:
  `BOT_DELAY_RUNG_N = max(prevVoiceDuration + 0.4s, baseDelay×rungIdx)`.

### BF-52: No "considering Bel" hover state on defender seat
- **Severity:** LOW
- **Symptom:** Saudi players visibly hesitate before a Bel; bot has
  zero "considering" intermediate state.
- **Fix sketch:** Render "(thinking about Bel…)" hover on defender
  seat for ~50% of BOT_DELAY_BEL.

## Other "feel" findings

### BF-60: Bot reveals its difficulty by always responding in 1.6s
- **Severity:** MEDIUM (predictability leak)
- **Symptom:** Because `BOT_DELAY_BID/PLAY/BEL` are deterministic
  constants, a watching human can identify "decision difficulty"
  ONLY when bot lags behind 1.6s due to dispatch contention. The
  bot LEAKS uniformity — every decision feels equally thought-out
  to the bot, every decision feels equally forced to the human.
  Perfectly predictable pacing IS a tell, even when strategy is
  unpredictable.
- **Root cause:** Constants without jitter.
- **Fix sketch:** Already captured in BF-01. Add jitter ±20% as
  the minimum baseline regardless of difficulty/tier.

### BF-61: Card-play SFX same volume for slam-down vs sacrifice
- **Severity:** LOW
- **Symptom:** Aggressive-bot slamming a winning A and patient-bot
  releasing a sacrificial 7 use identical `K.SND_CARD_PLAY`.
- **Fix sketch:** Add `card_play_strong.ogg` for A/J-of-trump or
  ≥40-strength-margin wins.

### BF-62: Bot-pending state has no "late" recovery surface
- **Severity:** LOW
- **Symptom:** When `Net.lua:4741-4751, 4947-4990` pcall recovery
  paths fire, user sees extended silence with no recovery cue.
- **Fix sketch:** If bot turn extends >2× expected delay, surface
  "thinking…" → "recovering" text.

## What feels right

- **Voice cue suppression on round-1 mid-bid pass** (`State.lua:998-1006`):
  smart suppression of `بَسْ`/`ولا` voice once a non-pass bid is on the
  table prevents confusing "pass-pass-hokm-pass" cue noise. This is
  the only voice-overlap path that's actually been thought through.
- **AWAL / THANY 0.5s delay** (`State.lua:907, 942, 2332`): round-start
  voice cues are deliberately delayed 0.5s so visual UI lands first.
  This "audio follows visual" discipline is exactly the right pattern
  and should be applied to BEL/TRIPLE/FOUR cues too.
- **Trick-resolve buffer 2.2s** (`Net.lua:1995`): explicit 2.2s hold
  on full 4-card trick view "for the player to read it" — well
  thought-out, comment makes the intention clear. Don't degrade this.
- **AKA banner styling per team color** (`UI.lua:3483-3487`): partner
  AKA = green, opp AKA = red. Right pattern; extend to other signals.
- **Bel-skip pass voice suppression** (mentioned in CHANGELOG v0.5
  era): not playing the pass voice when a bot skips Bel was a real
  feel improvement. Same logic should apply to escalation skips.

Searched CHANGELOG for "BOT_DELAY", "voice", "pacing":

- **v0.5 era (line 12334):** "Trick-resolve buffer 1.5s → 2.2s; bot
  delays 1.0s → 1.6s." The bid delay was bumped specifically to fit
  the OGG voice cue length; the BEL delay (1.4s) was added later when
  MP3 voice cues were introduced but never bumped to match the longer
  MP3 duration. **This is the BF-11 root cause.**
- **v0.11.0 (line 6221+, "voice-cue refresh"):** 8-voice-cue audio
  refresh. The MP3 voice files were introduced here but `BOT_DELAY_BEL`
  stayed at 1.4. Confirmed regression.
- **v1.1.0 (line 2528+):** "human-like unpredictability + partner-
  coordination upgrades". Multiple variance fixes for STRATEGIC
  decisions; no pacing variance work. The variance gap audit
  (`v1.5.3_audit_variance_gap.md`) addressed bid jitter for bid
  THRESHOLDS, not for `BOT_DELAY_*`.

The `BOT_DELAY_*` constants have not been touched since v0.5 era
(roughly: introduced as 1.0, bumped to 1.6 once for voice cue length,
never tier-aware, never decision-aware, never jittered). Most of this
report's findings have been latent since 2024.
