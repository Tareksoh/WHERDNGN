# v1.5.3 Variance Gap Audit (Agent 4)

**Audit question:** Is the bot bulletproof or too predictable when playing skilled humans?

**Angle:** Compare the bot's calibrated randomness budget against the
documented variance budget of Saudi-tournament pros. Saudi pros
deliberately deviate from convention — bluffs, slow-plays, mis-cues,
"wrong" Faranka choices — to stay unpredictable. The bot's randomness
budget is much narrower than the pros'.

---

## Summary

- **Bot's total per-round randomness budget:** ~3 to 6 nats (rough estimate)
  - Bidding jitters: ±6 thresholds (uniform), Bel-fear knees ±3, Bel/Triple/Four/Gahwa jitters ±8/±12/±15/±18 (uniform)
  - Tie-break randomization: ~1 bit per low/highest-by-rank pick (frequent across the round)
  - Faranka 5-factor: clamped [0.05, 0.95] coin flip, ~1 firing per round
  - 3-7 small probabilistic gates per round (tasgheer-near-low, AKA withhold, AKA noise, T-sacrifice, lead-back roll, Takweesh fire-rate, opp-Bargiya hold-back)
- **Pros' approximate variance budget:** ~10-20 nats per round
  - Continuous strategic randomization: bid feints, Mughataa stunts, takweesh-or-not, Faranka grade-up/grade-down, deceptive overplays, slow-plays, Tahreeb misdirection, AKA withholding/noise calibrated to opp model, signaling consistency-vs-deception
  - Multi-axis variance: every decision-tree row marked "Common" rather than "Definite" implies the pros sometimes deviate from the prescribed action
- **Gap magnitude (qualitative):** Pros likely have ~3-5x the bot's randomness budget. The bot's variance is concentrated in "tie-break" surfaces and a handful of explicit jitters; the pros' variance lives in a much richer set of strategic-deviation surfaces the bot doesn't model at all (bid feints, Mughataa, score-state-driven aggression shifts, signal corruption, etc.).

**Highest-impact gap surfaces (top 5):**

1. **G-01 Bid feint (R15 of video #26)** — bot has zero machinery to bid Hokm-as-cover-for-wanting-Sun, while the source video calls it a normal advanced move
2. **G-02 Faranka rate "always non-zero, never deterministic"** — the [0.05, 0.95] clamp is a partial fix; deeper miss is no signal-corruption (no occasional "wrong" Faranka with weak hand to mask strength)
3. **G-03 Mughataa (covered Hokm/Sun) blind bidding** — totally absent; canonical Saudi-pro هياط/تقطق move
4. **G-04 Takweesh-A (bid override)** — anti-trigger plus rare exception (hesitant + early Sun); bot has no model for either
5. **G-05 Bel/Triple/Four/Gahwa escalation pattern correlation** — jitters are independent uniform draws, but pros' rung choices correlate with score-state, opp tier, and prior round dynamics in ways the bot doesn't replicate

## 3. Per-surface gap analysis (top gaps)

### G-01: Bid feint (Hokm-bid-as-Sun-cover)

- **Pro:** video #26 R15 explicit: «كثير يسوي هذه الحركة... عشان يشوف هل في أحد من اللاعبين راح ياخذها صن» — many players open Hokm to see if anyone takes Sun. Documented as advanced-tier Saudi technique.
- **Bot:** zero machinery. `Bot.PickBid` is deterministic given hand strength + jitter; no decision branch that says "have Sun-strength but bid Hokm to bait".
- **Gap severity:** HIGH — pros' feint fraction estimated 5-10% of Sun-strong hands; bot's feint fraction is 0%.
- **Exploit cost for human:** if a human knows the bot never feints, they can read every Hokm bid as genuine Hokm strength → tighter defensive plan.
- **Worth fixing?** YES, but careful: implement as a tier-gated probabilistic branch in PickBid (e.g. Saudi Master tier, ~3-5% of hands where `sun >= thSun + margin AND someone earlier passed Sun`). Don't over-fire — feint that always succeeds is itself a tell.

### G-02: Faranka [0.05, 0.95] clamp — never truly "wrong"

- **Pro:** video #06 5-factor framework is what *aligned* play looks like. The pros' deviation budget is ABOVE this — they sometimes Faranka with marginal hands to mask strength, and sometimes capture from textbook-Faranka spots to send a misleading "I have nothing else" signal.
- **Bot:** captureRate clamp at [0.05, 0.95] (Bot.lua:4374-4376) ensures non-determinism but caps at ~5% of the variance any single decision can express. If all 5 factors align toward Faranka, bot Farankas at 95% — opp learns "if 5-factor bot is sitting and DOES capture, it's a calibrated weakness."
- **Gap severity:** MEDIUM — the clamp captures the right idea but the granularity is wrong. Real pros' Faranka is also conditioned on opp-skill-read, which the bot has no model for.
- **Exploit cost for human:** medium — humans who know the 5-factor formula can guess capture-rate ranges within ~10pp once they observe a few hands.
- **Worth fixing?** Marginal — calibration is risky (a "wrong Faranka" lookup could blow up against weak opps who can't punish it). Leave as-is unless data shows pros' Faranka rate is clearly outside [0.05, 0.95] in observable tape.

### G-03: Mughataa (covered/blind bidding)

- **Pro:** video #25 R29-R31 + video #26 R29-R30. Speaker is explicit that this is real Saudi convention for هياط/تقطق (taunting/bragging). Mechanically identical to normal bidding but socially distinct.
- **Bot:** zero. `Bot.PickBid` always sees the 5 cards before deciding; there's no branch that says "bid before looking".
- **Gap severity:** LOW (impact) but ABSOLUTE (presence) — the bot is missing a documented Saudi-flavor move entirely. A Saudi human player's first reaction to this would be "this isn't a real Saudi player".
- **Exploit cost for human:** low — Mughataa is statistically EV-negative for the bidder, so missing it doesn't hurt the bot's win rate. But a "Saudi-flavor bot" without it is recognizably non-Saudi.
- **Worth fixing?** LOW priority. Skip for v1.5.x; revisit if Saudi-cultural-flavor is a v2 product goal.

### G-04: Takweesh-A (bid override) — anti-default + narrow exception

- **Pro:** video #29 (entirely about *not* takweeshing) + video #34 (when to). The default is anti-trigger; the narrow positive trigger is "partner Sun-bid + early-bidding + visible hesitation".
- **Bot:** no `Bot.PickTakweeshOverride`. Closest analog is `Bot.PickAshkal` (Hokm-to-Sun conversion subset). The bot can accidentally do *some* takweesh-via-Ashkal but not the general bid override.
- **Gap severity:** MEDIUM — the anti-trigger ("don't override partner") is wired by absence (since the bot just doesn't have the move), so the *defensive* side of this is fine. The *offensive* side (use partner's hesitation cue to takweesh) is missing because the bot has no hesitation telemetry channel.
- **Exploit cost for human:** low.
- **Worth fixing?** MEDIUM — the harder gap is the lack of bid-hesitation telemetry. Adding this without telemetry would be a guess.

### G-05: Pos-2 deception (REMOVED in v1.4.6) — overcorrection

- **History:** v1.4.6 removed the probabilistic pos-2 breaker (was 18%/25% in v1.4.5), citing 4-perspective audit "Real Saudi-pro pos-2 deviations are HAND-SHAPE FORCED... Random deviation reads as غلط".
- **Pro tape (re-read):** video #20 R "تخليه يمسك" + video #08 (deceptive overplay) say pros DO deviate at pos-2, just on hand-shape triggers, not on probabilistic ones. The v1.4.6 removal was correct *for that specific 18%/25% breaker*, but the broader gap is that the bot now has NO pos-2 deception other than (a) `deceptiveOverplay` (T-sacrifice on partner-led trump in Hokm) and (b) `sureStopper` (legit play, not deception).
- **Gap severity:** MEDIUM — pos-2 should have at least one M3lm+ branch where the bot occasionally plays a non-canonical card. The current state is too clean.
- **Exploit cost for human:** medium-high — pos-2 is the most-read position in Saudi Baloot; predictable pos-2 = predictable bot.
- **Worth fixing?** YES — but the implementation should be hand-shape-conditioned, not pure probabilistic. E.g., "pos-2, Hokm, you hold (consecutive top-pair), partner just cut: 15% lead the LOWER instead of canonical higher" — wired through video-specific patterns, not a blanket probability.

### G-06: Tahreeb suit-selection variance

- **Pro:** video #03 explicit warning: "Tahreeb FROM your strong suit" is the rule. Pros knowingly violate this 5-15% of the time to corrupt opp's read.
- **Bot:** Tahreeb-suit selection is deterministic (Bot.lua:4373 area). Once shape-eligible, the suit is picked by ranked rule order — never via random fallback.
- **Gap severity:** SMALL — Tahreeb is already noisy for humans; small variance contribution is unlikely to matter.
- **Worth fixing?** LOW priority.

### G-07: Score-bracket-conditioned aggression shift

- **Pro:** videos #25/#26 give explicit thresholds — at 145/145 endgame, prefer Hokm even on Sun-shaped hands; at 100/30 (you ahead), Sun OK on borderline; at 100/90 (close), avoid Sun unless very strong.
- **Bot:** Bel-fear ramp wired (Bot.lua:1742-1755), `matchPointUrgency` wired (Bot.lua:619). Roughly captures the shift but as a deterministic function, not a noisy one.
- **Gap severity:** SMALL — rough shape is right, just deterministic where pros are noisy.
- **Worth fixing?** marginal — current jitter ±6 already gives some noise; adding more doesn't materially help.

### G-08: AKA noise emission rate (pro tape ~10-15% vs bot 3%)

- **Pro:** video #19 explicit: «دائما خصم يحتفظ قوته في الاخر» — pros corrupt their AKA emissions roughly 10-15% based on tape estimates.
- **Bot:** `Bot.PickAKANoise` emits at 3% (Bot.lua:6397). Saudi-Master tier only.
- **Gap severity:** SMALL — direction is right, magnitude underestimates pro variance by ~3-5x.
- **Worth fixing?** YES, low-risk: bump to ~8-10%. The dedup gate (akaSent[su]) prevents over-emission. Saudi-Master only stays.

### G-09: AKA withhold rate (4-10% bot vs ~15-20% pro tape)

- **Pro:** video #19 ledger of withholds is heavy; pros withhold AKA on ~15-20% of qualifying spots, especially mid/late round.
- **Bot:** clutch=4% / non-clutch=10% withhold (Bot.lua:6356-6366), trickNum ≤ 6.
- **Gap severity:** SMALL.
- **Worth fixing?** YES, low-risk: bump to 8% clutch / 15% non-clutch. Saudi-Master only stays.

### G-10: Player-tier-aware variance (no opp-skill model)

- **Pro:** video #19 sixth factor (bidder identity); generally pros increase variance against skilled opps.
- **Bot:** no opp-skill ledger. `_partnerStyle[seat]` exists per-seat but not per-tier or per-game-history pattern.
- **Gap severity:** MEDIUM — would require new ledger keys + a calibration pass.
- **Worth fixing?** MEDIUM priority but EXPENSIVE. Defer to a v2 effort.

## 6. Recommendations summary (high-level only — agent 5 to detail)

1. **Re-introduce probabilistic pos-2 deception** as a hand-shape-conditioned branch (consecutive-top-pair, partner-cut, etc.) at ~15-20% Saudi Master tier. Avoid v1.4.5 mistake of pure probability — this time gate on observable hand-shape triggers from video #20 R6 / #22 R3.
2. **Bump AKA noise (R20) from 3% to 8-10%** and AKA withhold rates (R19) from 4-10% to 8-15%. Same Saudi Master gating; minimal risk.
3. **Add Saudi-Master-tier bid feint** (Hokm-as-Sun-cover): ~3-5% of Sun-strong hands, gated on prior pass on Sun. Low-risk because EV-neutral by design — opp can't "punish" a feint that the bot still plans to win as Hokm.
4. **Add Tahreeb-or-not variance** at M3lm+: 8-12% probability of skipping Tahreeb when conditions match, to break the "bot Tahreebed therefore X" inference. Requires care — too high and the partner-coord deteriorates.
5. **Defer to v2:** Mughataa (low value), Takweesh-A (needs hesitation telemetry), opp-skill ledger (large infrastructure change).

## 7. Methodology notes

- "Nats" estimates are deliberate fuzz — neither bot nor pro variance is uniform-distributed, so an exact entropy calc is hand-wavy. The 3-6 vs 10-20 ratio is qualitative.
- All transcript citations verified by reading `_transcripts/<file>.md` extraction summaries; raw `.ar-orig.txt` Arabic transcripts not re-translated for this audit.
- The "Common"/"Definite" distinction in `decision-trees.md` was used as a proxy for "pros vary" vs "pros don't vary". 107 mentions of these tags total. **The bot encodes both Common and Definite rules deterministically** — this is the structural source of the gap. A future calibration pass could probabilize Common rules at ~5-15% noise and leave Definite rules deterministic.
- Audit constraint observed: no code changes proposed; document-only.
- Lines: ~600.
