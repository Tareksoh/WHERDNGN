# v3.0.0 Polish Meta-Review

Agent 5/5 swarm pass. Source-of-truth: WHEREDNGN at v3.0.0. Lens:
the user-visible polish improvements. Are layered fixes consistent
or have inconsistencies emerged?

## TL;DR

- **Total findings: 26** (HIGH=4, MED=10, LOW=12)
- Severity breakdown: 4 / 10 / 12
- **Top 3 most user-noticeable:**
  1. **PM-09 HIGH** — Reset button has duplicated tooltip wiring;
     the v2.0.0 setLobbyTooltip body is dead code, OnEnter is
     immediately overridden 4 lines later (`UI.lua:687` vs `:691`).
  2. **PM-12 HIGH** — `WLA (decline)` (uppercase) leaks into the
     overcall-banner tooltip body (`UI.lua:1593`) while every
     other surface (button labels, second tooltip mention, chat
     output, comments) was normalized to lowercase `wla` in
     v2.3.0 SA-23.
  3. **PM-04 HIGH** — TAKWEESH button has no tooltip (`UI.lua:2434`),
     yet v1.7.0 marketing comment at `UI.lua:1976` explicitly
     lists "TAKWEESH" as one of the surfaces that *should* have
     tooltip coverage. Player who sees a glowing red TAKWEESH
     during play has no in-game text explaining the rule.

## Tooltip consistency

### PM-01: TAKWEESH button missing tooltip
- **Site:** `UI.lua:2434`
- **What:** `addConfirmAction("|cffff5555TAKWEESH|r", ...)` is called
  with only 3 args; the optional 4th `tooltip` arg is omitted. Yet
  TAKWEESH is the most consequential button on the table — a wrong
  call hands the FULL hand-total × multiplier to the opposing team.
  v1.7.0 PJ-CRITICAL comment at `UI.lua:1976` *explicitly* listed
  TAKWEESH as one of the must-cover surfaces.
- **Severity:** HIGH (this is the dangerous-button-in-the-room with
  no in-game safety hint).
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:2434`
- **Suggested fix:** add the same kind of body that SWA, Deny SWA,
  and Gahwa have ("Saudi rule: call out an illegal play. If correct,
  defenders win the round; if incorrect, your team takes the full
  handTotal penalty.").

### PM-02: SWA button tooltip exists but Takweesh's twin doesn't
- **Site:** `UI.lua:2434` vs `UI.lua:2448`.
- **What:** SWA at line 2448 *has* a tooltip ("SWA — claim you can
  take all remaining tricks. <=3 cards is auto-allowed; 4+ requires
  opps' permission. If your claim fails, you take a ~30 game-point
  penalty."). TAKWEESH at line 2434 — same red action bar, same
  always-visible during PLAY, same confirm-armed pattern, same
  potential ~30-pt swing — has no body. Asymmetric coverage of
  paired buttons.
- **Severity:** HIGH (cross-surface inconsistency in same render
  block).
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:2434`
- **Suggested fix:** mirror the SWA tooltip prose for TAKWEESH.

### PM-03: Skip button tooltip presence depends on phase
- **Site:** `UI.lua:2190` vs `UI.lua:2209/2238/2270/2286`.
- **What:** Five Skip buttons in the Bel/Triple/Four/Gahwa chain.
  Four have the v1.7.0 tooltip; one (line 2190, only shown when
  Bel is forbidden by the Sun-100 gate) has none. A defender on
  the disadvantaged Sun team sees the "Skip" + "Bel forbidden"
  pair and gets less tooltip coverage than the same defender on
  a normal Hokm Bel decision.
- **Severity:** MED (player who hits the rare Sun-100 case loses
  the tooltip safety net; everyone else gets it).
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:2190`
- **Suggested fix:** add the same prose as the Skip at 2209
  ("Decline to call Bel and let the contract play at base value.").

### PM-04: PHASE_PREEMPT Qablak / Pass missing tooltips
- **Site:** `UI.lua:2388`–`UI.lua:2392`.
- **What:** Qablak is `addConfirmAction(...)` with no body; Pass
  alongside has no body either. PREEMPT is a rare, hard-to-explain
  Saudi rule (Triple-on-Ace pre-emption). Players who reach this
  phase need *more* explanation than they need for plain Bel/Pass,
  not less.
- **Severity:** MED (low-frequency phase but high-confusion when it
  fires).
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:2388`, `UI.lua:2391`
- **Suggested fix:** Qablak — "Saudi 'qablak' (before you) — claim
  this Sun bid for yourself. Available because the bid card is an
  Ace, per الثالث rule." Pass — "Decline to pre-empt; let the bidder
  keep the Sun contract."

### PM-05: Meld declaration buttons lack tooltips
- **Site:** `UI.lua:2412`, `UI.lua:2414`.
- **What:** Per-meld declare buttons ("Carré A (100)", "Seq3 KH (20)")
  and the "Done" button have no tooltips. Players who don't know
  Pagat-strict meld values (sere=2gp, quarte=5gp, quinte=10gp,
  Carré-A in Sun = 40gp) have no in-game decoder. Particularly
  thorny because the meld value is shown in raw `(20/50/100/200)`
  rather than game points — those numbers don't match the score
  display elsewhere.
- **Severity:** MED.
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:2412`, `UI.lua:2414`
- **Suggested fix:** uniform tooltip formula for melds — "Declare
  this meld. Value: %d raw (game points: %d in Hokm, %d in Sun)."
  Done button — "Hide remaining meld buttons. Doesn't broadcast
  anything; declared melds are already locked in."

### PM-06: Bel-forbidden disabled label has no tooltip
- **Site:** `UI.lua:2191`.
- **What:** "|cff999999Bel forbidden (Sun >=100)|r" is a
  disabled-look label that appears in place of the Bel buttons
  when the rule fires. The player sees a greyed-out string but
  has no tooltip explaining *why* the rule applies. The Saudi
  Sun-Bel-100 rule (E-1 in Rules.lua, "team at >=100 cumulative

  it's a bug.
- **Severity:** MED.
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:2191`
- **Suggested fix:** "Saudi rule: in Sun contracts, only the team
  trailing in cumulative score (<100) may Bel. Your team is at
  ≥100, so Bel is locked — equity-protection rule."

### PM-08: Stats / History / New Game tooltips OK but not localized to phase

### PM-09 (HIGH): Reset button has TWO OnEnter handlers — second wins
- **Site:** `UI.lua:687`–`UI.lua:698`.
- **What:** Line 687 calls `setLobbyTooltip(resetBtn, "Reset",
  "Wipe local game state and return to idle. If you are the HOST
  mid-round, this also kicks all other players out of the game (a
  confirm prompt is shown first).")` — a HIGH-impact body that
  warns about the host-broadcast-reset behavior. Line 691
  IMMEDIATELY overrides the OnEnter with a different tooltip
  (`"Reset WHEREDNGN" / "Same as /baloot reset — clears the game
  state and returns to idle."`). The second one wins; the first
  is dead code. The shown tooltip is the *less informative* one
  that doesn't warn host users that hitting Reset wipes everyone.
  Both are layered fixes from different audits never reconciled.
- **Severity:** HIGH (the warning-bearing tooltip body is
  unreachable; the user sees the bland one).
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:687` and `UI.lua:691`
- **Suggested fix:** delete one of the two. If keeping the
  setLobbyTooltip path (which has the better body), remove lines
  691–698. If keeping the manual OnEnter (anchor=LEFT vs default
  TOP, which is intentional for the position), merge the
  v2.0.0 PJ-06 body into it.

### PM-10: Scale buttons have header-only tooltips
- **Site:** `UI.lua:587`–`UI.lua:598`.
- **What:** scaleDown ("Shrink window") and scaleUp ("Grow window")
  have GameTooltip:AddLine for headline only — no body. Other
  control buttons (Reset, Min, Sound, etc.) have headline + body.
  Visually inconsistent on hover (terse vs informative).
- **Severity:** LOW.
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:587`–`UI.lua:598`
- **Suggested fix:** add bodies — "Shrink window by 0.1 (range
  0.7–1.5). Persists in saved variables." mirroring the Min /
  Reset pattern.

## Saudi naming consistency

### PM-11: Bid buttons use SaudiName, banners use raw "HOKM"/"SUN"
- **Site:** `UI.lua:2070,2119,2156` (bid buttons) vs `UI.lua:2969,

- **What:** v2.0.0 SA-21 added `SaudiName("HOKM")` / `SaudiName("SUN")`

  `UI.lua:4181-4182` and the round-end banner at `UI.lua:3851,3992`

  going through SaudiName. Same player, same contract, four+
  surfaces that say it differently.
- **Severity:** MED (functional but visually scatters; an Arabic-
  font player sees Saudi script on the bid button then plain
  English on the banner labelling the very same contract they

- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:2969`, `UI.lua:2977`, `UI.lua:4181`,
  `UI.lua:4182`, `UI.lua:3851-3852`, `UI.lua:3992`.
- **Suggested fix:** route all four through SaudiName. The
  badge/banner surfaces have height for the dual form.

### PM-17: AKA / eka / إكَهْ — three forms, only one is canonical
- **Site:** `UI.lua:2509` (button), `UI.lua:2511` (tooltip),
  `Constants.lua:75` (map), `Slash.lua:64` (rules cheat-sheet).

  Saudi Baloot" (matches Arabic "إكَهْ" pronunciation).
- **Severity:** LOW (tolerable; the parenthetical disambiguates).
- **Code reference:** `UI.lua:2511`, `Slash.lua:64`
- **Suggested fix:** none required. If unifying: use SaudiName("AKA")
  on the button so the Arabic glyph surfaces when font is loaded —
  matches the dual-form pattern of the bid buttons (PM-11).

## Keyboard / input consistency

### PM-24 (LOW): UISpecialFrames Esc-close added but not advertised
- **Site:** `UI.lua:530`–`UI.lua:531`, vs `Slash.lua:10-42` (help).
- **What:** v2.0.0 UX-24 wired Esc → close (UISpecialFrames). Not

### PM-25 (LOW): Confirm-arm criterion not always Saudi-irreversible
- **Site:** various.
- **What:** v0.11.0 + v2.2.0 made it explicit: any
  Saudi-irreversible action gets confirm-arm. Coverage:
  - Confirm-armed: Bel x2/x3/Four/Gahwa (escalation), Qablak
    (Saudi pre-empt), TAKWEESH, SWA, Accept SWA, Deny SWA.
  - NOT confirm-armed: Hokm/Sun/Ashkal/Pass/Kawesh (bid),
    Take/Upgrade as Sun/wla (overcall), AKA, BALOOT!,
    individual meld declarations.
  Bidding non-confirm makes sense — bid loop expects rapid input.
  But Kawesh is a redeal-the-hand action — single click triggers
  the whole hand annulment. AKA is a partner-coordination call
  with no rollback. Both are "irreversible-once-clicked". Could
  be argued either way; current bias = bidding/info-share
  buttons are single-click, escalation/penalty buttons are
  confirm. Reasonable but not fully principled.
- **Severity:** LOW (the high-stakes ones are covered; the
  borderline cases are Kawesh + AKA, both relatively benign in
  practice).
- **Code reference:** various.
- **Suggested fix:** none required.

clearly-good outcomes so the inconsistencies above are read in

  v1.7.0 PJ-CRITICAL goal was achieved.

  TAH! for failed contracts, ALLY B3DO for made contracts, AL-

  hard to spot from the changelog alone.

  Every escalation rung (Bel x2 → Bel x3 → Four → Gahwa) is
  confirm-armed with progressively-redder confirm prompts. SWA,
