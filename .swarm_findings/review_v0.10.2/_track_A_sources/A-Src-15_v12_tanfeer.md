## Critical disambiguation note

The transcript spelling is **التنفيذ** (tanfīdh, root ن-ف-ذ, "execution") throughout the auto-generated SRT. This is almost certainly an **ASR (speech-to-text) transcription error** for **التنفير** (tanfīr, root ن-ف-ر, "driving away / dispersal"). Evidence:

- Line 54 (00:12-00:14): the speaker explicitly defines the term as **"التنفير او كلمه تنفر في اللغه العربيه"** ("tanfīr or the word tanfir in Arabic") — using the ن-ف-ر root verb form (تنفر).
- Lexically, ن-ف-ر = "to make flee / drive off / repel" (compatible with the gameplay meaning of "discarding to dispel a card").
- Lexically, ن-ف-ذ = "to execute / pierce through" (incompatible with the meaning).
- The speaker's verb forms (نفرت, ينفر, ينفروا, تنفر, نفره) all use ن-ف-ر throughout — only the noun form gets transcribed as تنفيذ.
- Within the same SRT, both تنفير (lines 54, 614) and تنفيذ (passim) appear, confirming the ASR oscillates between the two.

**Adopted convention in this document:** I preserve the SRT verbatim spelling (mostly تنفيذ) when quoting, and flag the root question explicitly in Q4. The semantic/gameplay analysis treats it as **tanfīr (ن-ف-ر)**.

**Confidence in root ن-ف-ر being correct:** HIGH (≈ 0.97). The semantic mismatch (tanfīdh = "execution" makes no sense for a discard mechanic), the speaker's own verb usage, and the explicit definitional pairing all converge.

> **"ممكن بعدين تكتشف ان خويك قاعد ينفر لك"**
