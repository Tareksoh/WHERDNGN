## Q3 — Sun 10-hoarding by suit length

The video does **not** state a clean "2 cards = give partner; 3–4 =
give unless other strength; 5+ = hoard" rule with those exact
thresholds. Instead, it teaches a *general* Sun-priority for "play
the highest of consecutive cards."

**The rule actually taught (verbatim, ≤15 words):**
> "اذا كان ورق متتالي العب اكبر ورقه ولا تنسى انه في النهايه الورقه المتتالي كله تقريبا في نفس القوه"
>
> *"If the cards are consecutive, play the biggest one — don't forget
> that consecutive cards are roughly equal in strength."*

- **Timestamp:** 00:04:26.270 → 00:04:33.469 (lines 836–863)
- **Implication for 10-hoarding:** When the 10 sits at the **top**
  of a consecutive run (e.g. 10–J or 10–J–Q–K), playing the 10 is
  takbeer. When the 10 sits *below* a gap (e.g. 10 + Q with no J
  between), it is **non-consecutive** and the receiver should play
  the **larger** card (Q), which leaks the 10's location to the
  opponent's read.
- **Confidence:** **Medium.** The video frames the choice in terms
  of *consecutive vs non-consecutive*, not in terms of suit length
  thresholds. The "2 cards / 3-4 cards / 5+ cards" thresholds in
  the question prompt are **not present** in this video. They may
  come from a different source (likely v#41 — Sun basics) and
  should be cross-checked against `A-Src-10_v41_sun_basics_K.md`.

**Sub-claim that IS in the video (verbatim, ≤15 words):**
> "لو تعطيها قويه افضل انك تعطيها الشايب"
>
> *"If you're going to give it (the trick) heavily, better you give the
> King."*

- **Timestamp:** 00:03:57.890 → 00:03:59.750 (lines 753–762)
- Says: when partner has won and you're dispatching, give King
  rather than 10, because the 10 is more valuable elsewhere
  (you may lose the 10 to the OTHER opponent's queen on a future
  trick, whereas the King is already at top).
- **Confidence:** High for *that* claim; the broader "hoard 10
  if 5+ cards in suit" generalization is **not** stated here.

## Q4 — When to magnify vs miniaturize (dispatch)

The video's title is `21_magnify_sun` so the operational dispatch
rule is the centerpiece.

**Magnify (= takbeer = play your highest) when:**

a. Partner led the trick and won it (or is about to win it), and you
   hold ≥2 consecutive cards including a top-of-suit. Verbatim:
   > "اذا خويك ماكله الحل وجاء الدور عندك وعندك ورقتين او اكثر متتاليه العب اكبر ورقه فيهم"
   >
   > *"If your partner is eating the trick and your turn comes and you
   > have two or more consecutive cards, play the biggest one of them."*
   - **Timestamp:** 00:02:47.150 → 00:02:53.089 (lines 533–558)
   - **Confidence:** High.

b. You're cutting (قاطع) — out of suit — and your top non-trump in a
   consecutive set is in hand. Same verbatim rule: "100%, no debate."
   - **Timestamp:** 00:02:56.099 → 00:03:01.500 (lines 568–588)
   - Verbatim (≤15 words):
     > "العب اكبر ورقه 100% ما في انيقاش ولا فيها حالات تلعب اصغر"
     >
     > *"Play the biggest card 100%, no debate, no cases where you play
     > smaller."*
   - **Confidence:** High.

**Miniaturize (= play the smaller of two consecutive) — basically
NEVER in Sun:**

The video gives **one explicit exception** for non-consecutive cards:
when you hold 10 + Q (with the J missing/unclear) and partner has
already won the trick **strongly**, AND you have other "controlling"
suits to lead later, you may play the **Queen** to keep the 10 as a
suit-control card. Verbatim (≤15 words):

> "هذه الحاله الوحيده اللي ممكن ما تلعب العشره تلعب فيها البنت"
>
> *"This is the only case where you might not play the 10 — you play
> the Queen instead."*

- **Timestamp:** 00:07:12.840 → 00:07:15.590 (lines 1481–1486)
- **Critical caveat:** the teacher explicitly says this leaks
  information. "If you play the queen, partner will understand you
  have neither the 10 nor the King" (timestamp 00:07:15.600 →
  00:07:18.290). So *miniaturize* in Sun is permitted **only** when
  you have other suit-control cards and accept the partner-side
  read loss.
- **Confidence:** High.

## Q5 — Takbeer signal-reading on partner-side

**What the receiver (partner) should infer when teammate plays a
high card and "does takbeer":**

Verbatim (≤15 words):

> "هذه هي الرساله المهمه… ما عندك اكبر من هذه الورقه"
>
> *"This is the important message: you have nothing bigger than this
> card."*

- **Timestamp:** 00:00:54.420 → 00:00:56.760 (lines 191–204)
- **Confidence:** **Very high.** This is the teacher's explicit
  framing.

**Refined inference (consecutive vs non-consecutive):**

- **Consecutive top played (e.g. partner plays K of clubs, you don't
  have A of clubs in non-trump Sun where K is the top):** receiver
  knows partner has nothing above K *in that suit*, but may have
  other consecutive cards below.
- **Non-consecutive top played (e.g. partner plays K with no Q in
  hand):** receiver knows partner has nothing above K and *also* the
  card-just-below-K is missing. Verbatim (≤15 words):
  > "لو لعبت الشايب هيفهم انه ما عندك العشره"
  >
  > *"If you play the King, partner will understand you don't have the
  > 10."*
  - **Timestamp:** 00:04:46.020 → 00:04:49.730 (lines 922–933)
  - **Confidence:** High.

## Q7 — Glossary collision: should "Takbeer" identifier be renamed in code?

**Question recap:** v0.10.0 disambiguation surfaced that the code
identifier `MULT_TRIPLE` / `Bot.PickTriple` is sometimes referred to
in older docs/comments as "takbeer" / "تكبير" — but per this video,
**Takbeer is unambiguously play-direction, NOT escalation**.

### Source authority assessment

- **Video #21 (this source):** Takbeer = play-direction signal.
  Zero ambiguity. **Verbatim definition + 9 contextual usages, all
  consistent.**
- **Saudi escalation chain canonical Arabic:** بل / بل×2 / فور / قهوة
  → `Bel` / `Bel×2` / `Four` / `Gahwa`. The Saudi name for the
  third rung (×3 multiplier) is **بل×2** (literally "Bel times two"
  meaning "double the Bel"), **not Takbeer**. This is consistent
  with v0.10.0 R7 disambiguation and with the project's
  `CLAUDE.md` glossary.
- **No video in the Phase 1 corpus uses "تكبير" for the ×3 rung.**

### Recommendation

**Rename the code identifier.** The current `MULT_TRIPLE` /
`Bot.PickTriple` is functionally fine because the *English* label
"triple" describes the multiplier rung, but if any docstring,
comment, prompt, or string literal anywhere in the codebase aliases
`PickTriple` to "Takbeer" or "تكبير", that aliasing is **wrong** per
this source.

Concrete actions (out-of-scope for this file but flagged for Track B
or implementation phase):

1. **Search the codebase** for any `Takbeer`, `takbeer`, or `تكبير`
   mention in contexts referring to `MULT_TRIPLE`, `PickTriple`,
   `Bel×2`, or escalation. Replace with `BelDouble` / `BelX2`
   (matching the Arabic بل×2).
2. **Reserve the identifier `Takbeer` exclusively** for
   play-direction signaling — i.e. the picker function or signal
   field that implements "play your highest" partner-direction.
   If no such picker exists yet, leave the identifier free for
   future use and **do not** reuse it for escalation.
3. **Update `docs/strategy/glossary.md`** to record:
   - تكبير (Takbeer) → play-direction signal, "play your highest";
     not yet a code identifier; reserved.
   - بل×2 (Bel×2) → escalation rung 3, code: `MULT_TRIPLE` /
     `Bot.PickTriple`. **Do not call this "Takbeer" anywhere.**

**Source authority for this recommendation:** Video #21 verbatim
definition (Q1 above) + the absence of any escalation usage in
the SRT (Q2 above). **Confidence: Very high.**
