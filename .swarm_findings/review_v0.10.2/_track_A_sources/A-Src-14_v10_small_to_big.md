## Q2 — Ascending sequence rules (verbatim)

**Core rule, SRT blocks #15-19 (00:00:17,119 → 00:00:23,439):**

> "عندنا اليوم التهريب من تحت لفوق او من صغير لكبير وهذا اقوى تهريب في البلوت"

(≤15 words: **"التهريب من تحت لفوق او من صغير لكبير وهذا اقوى تهريب في البلوت"**)

— *"Today we have tahreeb from-bottom-to-top, or from-small-to-big — and this is the strongest tahreeb in baloot."*

**The two interchangeable Arabic names** — "من تحت لفوق" (from below to above) and "من صغير لكبير" (from small to big) — are explicitly given as synonyms. **No third name** (like "ascending" / "tasaa3udi" / تصاعدي) is used in this video; the speaker stays with the spatial/size metaphors.

**Worked-example template, SRT blocks #29-37 (00:00:36,520 → 00:00:48,069):**

> "لعبت اكت الهاص وخويك لعب في البدايه ثمانيه السبيت وبعدها لعبت 10 خويك اطاك ولد السبيت في البدايه هرب ثمانيه السبيت وبعدها هرب الولد"

— *"You played Ace of hearts; your partner first played 8 of spades. Then you played 10; your partner gave you Jack of spades. So first he tahreeb-ed 8 of spades, then he tahreeb-ed Jack — i.e. from-below-to-above, from small to big."*

**Inverse / counter-example, SRT blocks #43-49 (00:00:55,510 → 00:01:00,150):**

> "لو هرب في البدايه الولد وبعدين هرب الثمانيه يصير هنا التهريب من كبير لصغير"

(≤15 words: **"لو هرب في البدايه الولد وبعدين هرب الثمانيه يصير من كبير لصغير"**)

— *"If he first tahreeb-ed Jack, then tahreeb-ed 8, this becomes from-big-to-small."*

**Polarity meaning** (SRT blocks #41-49, 00:00:50,189 → 00:01:00,160):
- Small→big = "wants the suit" (نفس الشكل)
- Big→small = "doesn't want the suit" (ما يبغاها)

**Confidence:** DEFINITE (verbatim, with worked example for both polarities).

## Q3 — Partner re-emit when receiver passes? Does sender re-fire louder?

**Direct answer: YES — the receiver is expected to re-trigger emission by eating again, and the sender is expected to escalate with another small-to-big card.**

**SRT blocks #93-99 (00:01:54,000 → 00:02:03,069):**

**SRT blocks #99-107 (00:02:03,079 → 00:02:13,270) — single-event under uncertainty:**

The instructor says explicitly that the **second small card is what disambiguates.** The natural assumption from one small card is "wants the suit", but he says (block #112-117): "this is the natural assumption, **but some people think outside the box**" — i.e. a single emit is overrideable.

> "ما تروح سبيت الا اذا شفت تهريب خويك وفهمت انه يبغى سبيت"

(≤15 words: **"ما تروح سبيت الا اذا شفت تهريب خويك وفهمت انه يبغى سبيت"**)

This implies the receiver waits for the **completed (≥2) signal** before committing to the suit — i.e. **if the partner only emits once, the receiver should pass on a third eat opportunity to give the sender another chance to confirm.**

**Sender-escalation requirement, SRT blocks #181-187 (00:03:42,470 → 00:03:50,429) — the worked downside example:**

> "نفترض انه خويك فهم انك تبغى شريك جاك بالعشره تمام وهذا الخصم لعب الثمانيه وانت اكلت بالكه وتلعب التسعه الخصم هذا معه بنت السبيد"

— *"Suppose your partner understood you want clubs and came with the 10. Good. The opponent played 8; you ate with King; you play 9; this opponent has Q of spades…"*

— *"Play from the smallest card."*

**Confidence:** HIGH for re-emission expectation; HIGH for sender escalating with next-smallest. The "louder" framing isn't quite right — it's **"smaller → next-smaller-but-still-ascending"** rather than "larger / louder". The sender keeps emitting small cards in the suit, in ascending order.

## Q5 — Is "ascending" defined by rank or point-value?

**Rank-order, not point-value.**

**Direct evidence — SRT blocks #1-13 (00:00:00,080 → 00:00:14,950):**

> "حتى انت لما تهرب ما تهرب 8يه بعدين سبعه صح اوراق ما فيها ابناط لكن انت هدفك الرساله تخيل لما تلعب سبعه بعدين 8مانيه غير لما تلعب ثمانيه بعدين سبعه عكس تماما"

(≤15 words: **"اوراق ما فيها ابناط لكن انت هدفك الرساله تخيل لما تلعب سبعه بعدين ثمانيه"**)

— *"Even when you tahreeb, don't tahreeb 8 then 7. True, the cards have no points, but your goal is the message — imagine when you play 7 then 8 vs. 8 then 7, the complete opposite."*

The phrase **"الاوراق ما فيها ابناط"** ("the cards have no points") **explicitly rules out point-value as the ordering criterion.** In Hokm, 7 and 8 carry zero points each; neither outranks the other in the points sense. The instructor is teaching that the ordering matters for the **signal**, not because of point-arithmetic.

**Reinforced at blocks #234-242 (00:04:46,629 → 00:04:56,029) — the closing recap reuses the same 7-vs-8 comparison.**

**Worked rank-order examples:**
- 7 → 8 (small → big) and 7 → 9 (small → big) [block #251-255, 00:05:07,880]
- 8 → 7 = inverse signal (big → small)
- 7 → J = small → big (block #29-37 example)
- J → 8 = big → small (block #43-49 inverse)

**Implicit rank chain in #10's examples:** 7, 8, 9, J in ascending order. Q and K are not used in #10's tahreeb examples (they appear only in the meta-context, e.g. King in block #181 as a pull). The 10 only appears as the "what you played" / "what you eat with" card, never as a tahreeb-ed card — consistent with 10's role in Hokm as the partner-pull card (not a tahreeb-emit card).

**Confidence:** DEFINITE.

## Q8 — Bargiya vs ascending-Tahreeb distinction — when does the discriminator fire?

**Direct answer: The discriminator fires immediately on COUNT, not on RANK.**

**SRT blocks #19-29 (00:00:23,439 → 00:00:36,510):**

> "تكلمنا عن تهريب البرقيه وقلنا انه اقوى هو تهريب لكن من مره واحده او من تهريبه واحده وتكلمنا عن التهريب بشكل عام راح تلقون المقاطع تحت في الوصف"

(≤15 words: **"تهريب البرقيه اقوى تهريب لكن من مره واحده"**)

— *"We talked about bargiya tahreeb and said the strongest is from a single time / one tahreeb. And we talked about tahreeb in general — you'll find the videos below in the description."*

**Discriminator firing logic.** The instructor explicitly contrasts:

1. **Bargiya** = SINGLE-event special signal (a specific honor or a specific card-rank class signaling intent in one play).
2. **Small-to-big tahreeb** = MULTI-event signal (≥2 plays in ascending rank-order in same suit).

**The discriminator is the EVENT COUNT.** A first emit is **always** classified-pending until a second emit arrives:
- If a second emit arrives and is in same suit, ascending in rank → ascending-tahreeb fires (100%).
- If a second emit arrives and is in same suit, descending in rank → ascending-tahreeb fails; treated as "doesn't want suit" (the inverse big-to-small signal).
- If a second emit doesn't arrive (sender plays a third tahreeb of unrelated kind, or a fourth eat opportunity passes without further partner-emit) → falls back to the bargiya class single-event prior or to the #09 70/25/5 distribution.

**Bargiya class first**, then ascending-tahreeb class. Bargiya can re-fire even before the second emit arrives, because bargiya is a within-single-card signal (e.g. specific rank denoting suit-want). The ascending-tahreeb DISCRIMINATOR — small-to-big polarity — **cannot fire until the second emit lands**. There is no way to know polarity from one card alone.

**SRT blocks #211-219 (00:04:18,440 → 00:04:27,990):**

> "تبغى الشريحه في النهايه مو كل الناس تفكيرها واحد فعشان تتجنب هذا كله العب سبعه وبعدين تسعه"

— *"You want the [partner] to understand at the end — not everyone thinks the same. So to avoid all this, play 7 then 9, i.e. play the two smallest cards in a row."*

This advice presumes the discriminator only fires correctly when the SENDER conforms strictly to ascending rank-order — i.e. the discriminator can mis-fire (or fail to fire) if the sender deviates.

**Cross-source: #09 first-emit.** #09's 70/25/5 distribution says first-emit ALWAYS represents a "wants matching shape" prior at 70%. So the bargiya/ascending discriminator order is:

1. Apply bargiya class check on the first emit (does it match a known bargiya signal pattern?).
2. If bargiya, apply bargiya-specific rule (90%-ish, per other source-D files referencing this).
3. If not bargiya, apply 70/25/5 prior.
4. On second emit landing: check small-to-big polarity → upgrade to 100% if YES, downgrade to "wants other suit" if NO.

**Confidence:** HIGH for the discriminator-on-count rule. MEDIUM for the exact ordering of bargiya-vs-ascending priority on a single emit (the videos don't directly specify, but the standard interpretation is bargiya-first since it's a special-case rule).
