## Headline framing (overview)

| Domain | Card to sacrifice | Goal direction |
|---|---|---|
| Sun (الصن) | J (الشايب) standard; T (العشره) only for true pros | Make opp **continue leading** the suit |
| Hokm (الحكم) | J (الولد) only — never T or 9 of trump | Make opp **STOP re-leading** trump |

Speaker at sub #363–367 (~08:08): «هدفك من هذه الحركه في الصين والحكم انه توهم خصمك … الفرق الثاني هنا في الصن توهم خصمك عشان يجيك في نفس الشكل لكن في الحكم توهم خصمك عشان ما يجيك نفس الشكل». English: "Your goal in both Sun and Hokm is to deceive your opponent … the second difference: in Sun you deceive him so he KEEPS coming with the same suit, but in Hokm you deceive him so he does NOT come with the same suit."

### Q3 — Hokm Q-deception (defender holding Q+T plays Q to suggest no T)

**SRT location:** subs #421–445, timestamps 00:09:24,420 → 00:09:55,550.

**Setup (sub #421–423, 00:09:24,420):** Bidder bought Hokm, led trump-J («ربع بولد»), partner covered with T («خويك عطاء عشائر» — auto-transcription of العشره), and opp played 10 to you. You hold Q (البنت) and 9 (التسعه) of trump.

**Verbatim Arabic ≤15 words (the move):**
> «ايش تسوي في هذه الحاله تلعب البنت لو لعبت البنت هذا حيفترض انه انت عندك الايكا»

(SRT subs #427–431, 00:09:30,480 → 00:09:38,030.)

**English:** "What do you do here? You play the Q (البنت). If you play the Q, he'll assume you hold the A (الإكَه)."

**Verbatim Arabic ≤15 words (the secondary inference — opp will think you also hold T):**
> «وايضا هيفترض انه هذا عنده لكه»

(SRT sub #431–433, 00:09:38,030 → 00:09:40,800.)

**English:** "And he'll also assume that this one [your partner] has the T."

**Verbatim Arabic ≤15 words (full deception payoff):**
> «راح يرتاح من ناحيتك حيقول ما عندك شيء»

(SRT subs #437–439, 00:09:46,019 → 00:09:50,630.)

**English:** "He'll relax about your side; he'll say 'you've got nothing.'"

**Verbatim Arabic ≤15 words (logical kicker — why opp must believe you hold no smaller trump):**
> «الاول لعب الشايب اذن اللعب هذا اكبر ورقه موجوده عنده فمستحيل يكون عنده اصغر منه»

(SRT subs #441–445, 00:09:50,630 → 00:09:55,550.)

**English:** "First he played the J [the Q in this Hokm scene — speaker reuses 'الشايب' as the deception card], so this is his biggest remaining card; it's impossible he has anything smaller."

**Note on terminology:** In Hokm context, the speaker's "you played the biggest remaining card" framing applies to whatever card you just dropped. Here you played the **Q (البنت)** as the deception card while still holding the **9** — the deception is "you must be void of trump-9 too, otherwise you'd have played it for cheaper". So the inference chain is: opp thinks you have **no card below the Q in trump**, AND from there infers your partner must hold the T (because the T is missing from the scene). Speaker's logic chain at subs #441–445 is symmetric to the Sun-J scenario.

**Confidence:** Very high. The Q+T-vs-Q+9 specifics are visually presented in the video; the SRT preserves the verbal commentary cleanly.

### Q6 — SWA pursuit motivation (why this move when chasing SWA)

**SRT location:** subs #103–115, timestamps 00:02:04,860 → 00:02:22,729.

(SRT subs #103–107, 00:02:07,740 → 00:02:13,070.)

**Verbatim Arabic ≤15 words (SWA motivation — the explicit one):**
> «او عشان تساوي اذا عندك سوا»

(SRT sub #115, 00:02:20,280 → 00:02:22,729.)

**English:** "Or so that you SWA, if you have a SWA [pending]."

**Surrounding three-motivation enumeration (subs #109–115, ~00:02:13–00:02:22, full chain ≤30 words):**
> «جنب السميد فانت تسوي ذي الحركه عشان يجيك سميت بسرعه عشان تخسرها علينا اذا كان هو مشترين او عشان تنقذك من خساره اذا كنت انت مشتري او خويك او عشان تساوي اذا عندك سوا»

**English:** "Alongside the suit — you do this move so he comes in the suit fast: (1) so they lose [the round] over us if they're the buyers, (2) so it rescues you from a loss if you or your partner are the buyer, (3) or so that you SWA if you have a SWA."

**Three motivations, plain language:**
1. **Break Kaboot («تكسر كبوت»)** when opp team is sweeping all 8 — getting opp to feed you a winner kills the sweep.
2. **Rescue a failing bid** («تنقذك من خساره اذا كنت انت مشتري او خويك») when bidder team is behind.
3. **Set up SWA** («تساوي اذا عندك سوا») — the deception buys tempo to surface SWA on the back-end.

**Confidence:** Very high. All three motivations stated verbatim and consecutively.
