# 21_magnify_sun — Takbeer in Sun

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xxsf2QvaiU0
**Title:** التكبير في الصن | 5 | #احترف_البلوت
**Topic:** How and when to "magnify" (play your highest spare card) for partner in Sun. Sun-side companion to video #22 (Hokm Takbeer).

## 1. Vocabulary check

| Arabic | Saudi English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| تكبير (takbeer) | "magnification" — playing your largest available card to feed partner | New term. The verb كبّر = "to make big / promote". Applies whenever partner is taking the trick OR when you ruff into partner's win. |
| مجاوب (mujaawib) | "responder" — you are following the led suit (have it) | Position-of-play term. |
| قاطع (qaati`) | "cutter" — you are void in led suit and ruffing/sloughing | Same speaker uses قطع to mean ruff. |
| ابناط / بنات | "girls" = Q-points (ranks Q+ in non-trump = scoring cards). Speaker uses بنات loosely to mean "scoring tens/face values" not literally Queens. |
| متتالي / غير متتالي (mutataali / ghayr mutataali) | "consecutive / non-consecutive" cards — the central distinction in this video. ولد + 9 in same suit = consecutive (no card between). 10 + بنت = NON-consecutive (شايب is between). |
| الحلَّة (al-hilla) | "the trick" — already in glossary. |
| رساله (risaala) | "message" — the inferential signal partner reads from your card. |
| تصقير (tasqeer) | "falconing" / wasted-feed — opposite of takbeer; throwing low when high was needed. |

## 2. Game-state setup

Standard Sun (صن) contract. Mid-trick play, position 2 / 3 / 4. Trick already shows partner's card OR opp's lead. The decision: which of YOUR cards in the led suit (or, if قاطع, which suit/card to throw).

---

## 3. The decision rules

### Rule A — Sun, partner is winning, you have CONSECUTIVE cards in the suit → play the HIGHEST. 100%, no exceptions.

> "اذا خويك ماكله الحل وجاء الدور عندك وعندك ورقتين او اكثر من ورقتين متتاليه العب اكبر ورقه فيهم سواء كنت مجاوب او كنت قاطع. لو كنت قاطع مثلا تقصد فيها تهريب وغير تهريب العب اكبر ورقه 100% ما في انيقاش ولا فيها حالات تلعب اصغر."

Example: partner led ولد الشريف (J of hearts, won trick); you hold بنت + شايب of hearts (Q+K, consecutive — nothing between). Play **شايب**, not بنت. Reason: if you hold back the شايب and partner has no re-entry, شايب dies in your hand or on opp's 10 next round.

Edge case: 2-card unwanted suit dump (J + 9): **dump LARGER first** (J before 9). Smaller-first reads as bottom-up Tahreeb (positive signal). This already in Section 8.

### Rule B — Sun, partner winning, you have NON-CONSECUTIVE cards in the suit → still play HIGHEST in the normal case.

> "لو عندك عشره وعندك بنت هل هي متتاليه لا بينهم ايش بينهم الشايب اذا خويك لعب اكتشف الشريعه هذا يعطيك تسعه جاء دور عندك الاصل والافضل تلعب اكبر."

Example: partner led A of hearts (won); opp under-played 9; you hold T + بنت (gap = شايب is missing, presumed at opp). Default = play **T** (the higher of your two).

**The "message" reasoning:** if you play T, partner infers "you do NOT hold شايب" (since شايب > T and is consecutive with T from above). If you instead play بنت, partner infers "you hold neither T nor شايب of this suit". The smaller card sends the **stronger negative** message — useful only when you genuinely want partner to give up on the suit.

### Rule C — Sun, non-consecutive case: the ONE allowed exception is "I am setting up a Faranka-style trap (مسك لعب)".

> "هذه الحاله الوحيده اللي ممكن ما تلعب العشاء تلعب فيها البنت ولو لعبت البنت فيها خوالي اشي يفهم ما عندك الشايب لانه متتالي لانه اكبر من البنت ومتتاليه اصلا الشايب عنده لكن يفترض انه مو عنده."

You may downshift (play بنت instead of T) ONLY when you intend to seize control of subsequent leads — i.e. you have a separate strong suit / cutting plan. The deception risks ~10% reverse-tell (opp may read "T probably still with you" from very-low play and counter), increasing as your downshifted card gets smaller (7/8 leak more than the بنت).

### Rule D — Sun, you are قاطع (ruffing/discarding void), throwing into partner's win → play HIGHEST of the spare suit. Tahreeb intent doesn't change this.

> "تقصد فيها تهريب وغير تهريب العب اكبر ورقه."

Example: opp led 7 of suit X; partner ruffs / wins; you are void in X and must throw from a different suit. Hand has بنت + ولد + 9 in some side suit Y. **Throw بنت** (the largest), regardless of whether you intend the discard as a Tahreeb signal in Y.

### Rule E — Reverse direction: you are ahead, partner reads YOUR played-card as a ceiling.

> "لو ليله الشايب راح يفهم انه ما عندك العشره."

If you play شايب, partner reads "no T above". If you play T, partner reads "no شايب above" (because consecutive). If you play بنت in a non-consecutive case, partner reads "no T AND no شايب" — strong double-negative.

Symmetric: **when partner plays a card, apply the same inference back.** Partner played T → partner does not hold شايب of that suit (شايب must be at opps).

## 5. Non-rule observations

**Takbeer in Sun — the rule** — On any trick where partner is taking it (or you are ruffing into partner's win), play the LARGEST non-trump-relevant card from the dump-set: 100% mandatory when you hold consecutive cards in the suit; the default (with one Faranka-style exception) when the cards are non-consecutive.

**Difference from Hokm Takbeer** — Sun has no trump-rank-quirk (J=20, 9=14 reordering) to distort the laddering, so the simplification holds: **"highest" in Sun is always the highest card-value card by Saudi rank order** (A>T>K>Q>J>9>8>7). In Hokm Takbeer (#22), the "highest" of trump can mean different things depending on whether you're inside the trump suit (J/9 jump) versus a side suit. **In Sun the rule simplifies to: highest face card you hold of the relevant suit, full stop.** No inversion in Sun for non-consecutive cards — the speaker is explicit "الاصل والافضل تلعب اكبر."

**Edge cases — when NOT to play highest:**
1. **مسك لعب / control-play** intent (Rule C): you intentionally hide your top to retain initiative; requires a separate strong suit elsewhere.
2. **2-card unwanted-suit Tahreeb dump**: J+9 of an unwanted suit — already Rule A's "highest first" suffices, but speaker reaffirms ordering.
3. **No-بنات (no high cards) at all**: when entire dump-set is 9/8/7 (rank-zero cards), the "highest" choice is cosmetic — speaker says preference is still 9 over 8 over 7, but emphasis drops because no scoring is at stake. Following أصول (form) > minor EV.
4. **Partner's winning card is already the trick-ceiling and the trick is locked**: still play highest from your spare — speaker treats this as the dominant case throughout.
