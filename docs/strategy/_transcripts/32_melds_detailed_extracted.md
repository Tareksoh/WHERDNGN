## 2. Decision rules

| WHEN | RULE | WHY |
|---|---|---|
| Declaring meld type at start of trick 1 | **Announce meld name BEFORE playing your first card.** Naming after the card is laid = meld lost (not counted). | Strict timing rule. |
| Showing meld in trick 2 | **Lay meld face-up on table for 3-5 seconds** in trick 2 before playing your card; then pick it up and play. | Too short (1s) or too long (10s+) → potential qaid. Standard window is 3-5s. |
| You hold multiple melds | Announce all of them in trick 1 (e.g. "double sera", "fifty and sera"). When laying, **lay highest first, then lower.** | Order matters in strict (military) games; some games allow casual. |
| Holding 6+ consecutive cards in same suit | **Score is still 100 (SEQ5).** When laying, **lay only the top 5 cards** — laying more than 5 risks qaid in strict games. | Conservative: avoid over-declaring. |
| You hold a SEQ that overlaps the K+Q of trump (Belote) | In trick 1: announce only "sera" (or whichever SEQ). **Do NOT name Belote in trick 1.** Reserve "Belote" announcement for the moment you actually play the second card of K+Q on the table. | Belote requires K+Q both played to count; pre-announcement = qaid. |
| Belote announcement timing | Say "Belote" when **playing the second of the K+Q of trump pair** during trick play. Alternative: state at end-of-round score-up: "ma'ai Belote". | Belote is announced on play, not on declaration. |
| You hold meld whose components overlap (e.g. K+Q+J of one suit can form SEQ3 of figures OR partial SEQ4 with T) | **Pick the higher-scoring single meld; do not declare overlapping melds with shared cards.** Overlapping cards across two declared melds = qaid. | A card cannot count for two melds simultaneously. |
| Meld + Belote in trick-1 announcement | If SEQ contains the K+Q of trump (e.g. Hokm = Hearts and you hold Q+J+T of Hearts plus K of Hearts elsewhere as part of SEQ): **declare the SEQ in trick 1; declare "Belote" when playing the K+Q pair.** "بطنه" / "ma'ah" wording allowed at end. | Same Belote-timing rule applies even when overlapping with sequence. |
| Two players (both teams) declare melds | **Only the higher-ranking meld scores.** Lower meld → not laid; if accidentally laid → qaid on the lower-meld holder. | Trump-and-bidder analog: only winner counts. |
| Same meld TYPE both players (tie on type) | Compare by **highest card in the meld.** | Standard tiebreaker. |
| Melds tied even on highest card | **An-Nazil (the player who plays first after the dealer = dealer's RHO) wins the tiebreak.** | Tempo advantage rule. The first-player privilege extends from bidding to melds. |
| Same team both hold melds | **Both score** regardless of rank — only inter-team conflicts matter. | Partner-team additivity. |
| You announced a meld but partner has higher OR opponent has higher | **Do NOT lay it down.** Laying lower meld → qaid. Just stay quiet at trick 2. | Restraint after better is announced by another. |
| Conflict between two same-rank melds (e.g. one is SEQ4 = 50, other is Carré-of-figures = 100) | **Sequence wins** in most games (per video, "اغلب الجلسات تقول 100 تسلسل اقوى") between SEQ5 (Miyah Tasalsul) and Carré-figures (Miyah Suwar). Speaker defers full discussion to a separate video. | Conflict resolution heuristic; jurisdictional. |
| Carré of Aces, Sun contract, **first round (sakkah) of game** | **Some Saudi tables do NOT score the 400 in the very first sakkah** (deal hasn't shuffled enough). | Common house rule; speaker explicitly notes. Not universal. |
| Carré of Aces appears in a Hokm round | **Score it as 100 (treat it like other Carré).** Declaring "400" in Hokm = qaid. | Critical rule — 400 is Sun-only. |
| 400 (Carré-Aces in Sun) was successfully declared | **Round is essentially won unless a qaid is incurred.** "Mustaheel takhsar." | Practical signal — Sun + 400 = nearly auto-make. |

## 3. Conventions confirmed

- **Card-rank order for melds:** A > K (Shayib) > Q (Bint) > J (Walad) > T > 9 > 8 > 7. **NOT the Sun-rank order** (where T sits between A and K). Speaker emphasizes this distinction multiple times — it is the **most common confusion source** for beginners.
- **Meld naming convention:** A meld is named by its **highest card.** "Sera Shayib" = K-Q-J SEQ3. "Sera 9" = 9-8-7 SEQ3 (alias: cycle/Cikel). "Khamseen Walad" = J-T-9-8 SEQ4. "Miyah Bint" = SEQ5 ending at Q.
- **All sequences must be same suit.** Cross-suit "sequences" (e.g. T of Spades + 9 of Hearts + 8 of Spades) are NOT melds. Speaker explicit.
- **Carré ranks for melds:** A, K, Q, J, T (i.e., Carré of Aces, Kings, Queens, Jacks, or Tens).
- **9s, 8s, 7s do NOT form Carré.** Speaker explicit: "أربعه تسعات... هل يعتبر مشروع لا. باربع الثمانيات... ما يعتبر، اربع سبعات ما يعتبر."
- **Belote is Hokm-only**, K+Q of the trump suit specifically. 4 Aces is the Sun-only equivalent prestige meld.
- **Same point-value across contracts:** SEQ3 = 20 in Hokm = 20 in Sun. Speaker states the difference between two same-tier melds is *which one is laid*, not the points.

- **Belote (K+Q of trump, 20 pts)** — Confirmed. Hokm-only. Multiplier-immune behavior NOT mentioned in this video (out of scope), but Belote-as-meld value of 20 corroborated. `K.MELD_BELOTE=20`. **No discrepancy.**

**Possible refinement (not a discrepancy with `Rules.lua`, but a UX/declaration rule not in the table):**

- **Declaration timing rules** — `saudi-rules.md` says "no running announcement rule; melds simply declared in trick 1." Video adds **specific timing**: meld name announced **before** playing first card of trick 1; meld laid face-up for **3-5 seconds** in trick 2 before playing. Belote is **announced on play of the second K+Q card**, not in trick 1. These are real Saudi-table conventions; not currently captured in `Rules.lua` since the addon implementation likely auto-handles them, but worth noting in `glossary.md` and signals/conventions documentation.
- **Tiebreaker rule** — When two players hold equal-strength melds, **An-Nazil** (the player who acts first after the dealer = dealer's RHO) wins the tiebreak. This is the same tempo-priority rule that applies in bidding. `saudi-rules.md` does not mention meld tiebreakers. **Recommend adding to `saudi-rules.md` melds section.**
- **Carré-Aces-in-first-sakkah house rule** — Speaker says many Saudi tables don't count 400 if it lands in the very first deal of the match. This is an optional/jurisdictional rule, not a hard `Rules.lua` constraint. Worth noting as a comment in `Rules.lua` and in `saudi-rules.md`.

**Bottom line:** No rule-correctness discrepancies between video and `saudi-rules.md` / `Rules.lua` constants. All meld scores and qualification rules are confirmed.
