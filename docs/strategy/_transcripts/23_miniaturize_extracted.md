# 23_miniaturize — Tasgheer (التصغير) — Sun and Hokm

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=STQuiYmv9dk
**Title:** التصغير في البلوت — صن وحكم
**Slug:** 23_miniaturize
**Topic:** Tasgheer (التصغير) — when/which LOW card to play. Inverse of Takbeer (#21, #22). Covers BOTH Sun and Hokm.

---

## Summary

The speaker frames Tasgheer as the **mirror of Takbeer**. Takbeer = play biggest when partner (الخوي) will eat the trick; Tasgheer = play smallest when **opponent (الخصم) will eat the trick**, so as to deny them ابناء (point-cards / "kids"). Same prerequisite: you must be **certain or strongly expect** the opponent wins the حله. Examples are presented as direct inverses of Takbeer examples.

The rule is **contract-conditioned**:
- **Sun:** play the absolute smallest of the suit; prefer cards "without ابناء" (no point value), e.g., the 9 over a face card.
- **Hokm side-suit:** same — smallest non-point card.
- **Hokm trump-follow:** when forced to follow trump and an opponent will win, dump the **lowest trump** (matches existing Section-4 Hokm losing-trump rule).

The speaker also applies Tasgheer when partner played AKA (إكَه / إكَهْ) and the opponent is positioned to take: throw the **smallest** card of that suit (e.g., 7) — keep face cards for later.

## Possible contradictions

**Section 4 row (currently Definite, sources 05+09) says:**
> Sun, you are losing-side off-suit follow → dump the **HIGHEST** spare you can — never the lowest.

**This video (23) says:**
> Sun, opp wins the trick → dump the **smallest** / no-ابناء card.

**Resolution candidates:**

1. **Context disambiguation:** The Section-4 rule is the **Tahreeb / signaling** context — when you are still **following the led suit** in Sun and your dump is a directional message to partner. Tasgheer is the **opp-wins certainty** context where signaling is moot — no point-cards donated. The two rules apply to disjoint sub-cases; the predicate distinguishing them is *"is partner positioned to use the discard as a signal?"* If partner is winning or about to win → dump highest (signal). If opp wins certainly → dump smallest (point-deny).

2. **Possible re-read of Section 4:** the "dump highest" rule may have been *Tahreeb-conditioned* in the source (#05, #09) — i.e., it's the signaling convention, not the universal Sun off-suit rule. Tasgheer carves out the opp-winning case explicitly.

**Suggested resolution:** add a refined Section-4 row:
- *"Sun, opp will certainly win the trick (Tasgheer); you must follow off-suit / discard"* → **dump smallest, prefer no-ابناء.** Sources: 23.

This does NOT replace the existing dump-highest row — it carves out the certainty-of-opp-win case as a sibling rule. Both rules survive with explicit predicates.

## Non-rule observations

**Tasgheer in Sun** — when to play low / which low.
The trigger is **certainty or strong expectation** that opponent wins this trick. Choose the **smallest** of the legal suit, preferring cards with no ابناء (9/8/7). Speaker's running example: opp leads إكه, partner plays low, you have شايب+بنت+9 → play 9; don't burn T (العشره). The "biggest mistake" is dumping the T to avoid a face card — the T carries ابناء and you've given it freely. With a 2-card non-consecutive (e.g., ولد+9) dump the smaller (9) first.

**Tasgheer in Hokm** — when (especially trump-follow).
On **side-suits** (non-trump), behavior matches Sun: smallest, no ابناء. On **trump-follow** when opp will win, dump the **lowest trump** — matches the existing Section-4 Hokm losing-trump rule (this video adds confidence). For specific shapes — e.g., bidder led ولد (J of trump) and you have non-consecutive trumps + T-of-trump — preserve the T; spend a smaller spare. Tasgheer also fires on **partner's AKA**-led trick when you are NOT the winning side: play smallest (e.g., 7), don't burn high cards.

**Combined Takbeer/Tasgheer rule** — given both videos, the unified contract-conditioned rule is:

> **When the trick-winner is certain (or near-certain), shape your play to the winner's identity:**
> - **Partner will win (certain):** play your **biggest** card of the suit you can spare ("Takbeer" #21, #22) — gift partner ابناء, maximize captured points.
> - **Opponent will win (certain):** play your **smallest** card of the suit, preferring no-ابناء (9/8/7) ("Tasgheer" #23) — deny opp ابناء.
> - **Winner uncertain:** fall through to the signaling layer — Tahreeb if partner-likely-winning, Tanfeer if opp-likely-winning, normal heuristics otherwise. Tasgheer/Takbeer **do not fire** without certainty.
>
> **Contract twist:**
> - In **Sun**, "biggest"/"smallest" use plain rank (A>T>K>Q>J>9>8>7) and ابناء = A/T/K/Q/J.
> - In **Hokm trump**, "biggest"/"smallest" use trump rank (J>9>A>T>K>Q>8>7) and the same Takbeer/Tasgheer logic applies — biggest trump for Takbeer, smallest trump for Tasgheer.
> - In **Hokm side-suits**, identical to Sun off-suit.
>
> The two videos together establish the **certainty-conditioned point-flow rule**: certainty of trick-winner → asymmetric card-value gift/withhold; uncertainty → cede to signaling conventions. This is a foundational rule that should sit at the top of Section 4 (mid-trick play) as the **primary triage** before Tahreeb/Tanfeer/Faranka branch in.
