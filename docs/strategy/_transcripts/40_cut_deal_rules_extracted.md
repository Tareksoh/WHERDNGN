# 40 — Cut & Deal Rules (قوانين القص والتوزيع)

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vuV1mY_jnMo
**Slug:** 40_cut_deal_rules
**Topic:** Pre-bid procedural-error rules — Kasho/Takweesh triggers,
penalty taxonomy, and remediation menu for deal-time mistakes.

---

## 1. Topic & scope

This video covers the **pre-bid (مرحله ما قبل الشراء)** phase only —
specifically what happens when the dealer (الموزع) commits a
procedural error during the cut/deal. Two error categories are
treated:

1. **Mis-deal counts** — dealer gives a player too many or too few
   cards.
2. **Card exposure** — a card is accidentally revealed during the
   deal (by dealer or by any player dropping one).

The speaker also defines the **three-tier penalty taxonomy** that
governs both pre-bid and post-bid errors.

---

## 2. Penalty taxonomy (the three tiers)

Saudi Baloot recognizes three escalating responses when any player
errs. The non-offending team chooses which to invoke.

| Tier | Saudi term | Severity | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Heavy** | **القيد (Qaid)** | Severe | Non-offending team takes full game-points: **26 raw in Sun, 16 raw in Hokm** (assumes no melds for offender); offending team scores 0. Mostly a post-bid remedy. |
| **Light** | **التكويش / الكوشه (Takweesh / Kasha)** | Light | Pre-bid call. Hand is killed and **redealt**, no points awarded either side. |
| **None** | **يمشي اللعب (let it ride)** | — | Both teams agree (or non-offending team chooses) to continue play. House discretion. |

**Speaker's framing:** "اللي يخطي يتحمل غلطه" — *whoever errs bears
their mistake; the non-offending team always chooses the appropriate
penalty from those available.*

> **Rule precedence:** Whether Qaid, Takweesh, or "let it ride" is
> available depends on the *specific error type* and the *jلسة*
> (house/sitting). Some sittings have fixed conventions; others
> vote. The video repeatedly stresses **"على حسب الجلسه" (per
> sitting)** as the meta-rule.

## 3. Decision rules

### Rule 40-R1 — Mis-deal: card count off (extra or missing card)

**WHEN:** Dealer gives a player N+1 or N−1 cards (i.e., not exactly
8). Discovered after the deal completes.
**RULE:** Non-offending team has **three options**:
1. **Qaid (قيد توزيع)** — score the offending team for **16 raw**
   (treated as if Hokm was bid; "نقاط الحكم كانه قد حكم").
2. **Takweesh** — kill the hand, redeal.
3. **Continue (تمشي)** — fix the imbalance by transferring a card
   from the over-dealt player to the under-dealt player, then play.
**SAUDI NAME:** "قيد التوزيع" (deal-Qaid).
**SPEAKER PREFERENCE:** Speaker recommends **Qaid التوزيع (16 raw)
over Qaid صن (26 raw)** even when the contract turned out to be Sun
— rationale: the offender's procedural error preceded the bid, so
the milder Hokm-equivalent score is fairer.
**Source:** lines 122-145.

### Rule 40-R2 — Mis-deal continuation: which card transfers

**WHEN:** Non-offending team chose option 3 (continue) under R1.
**RULE:** The over-dealt player must surrender a card of a suit
that is **NOT singleton (غير مقطوع)** in their hand. Singleton suits
must be preserved (transferring them would void the player and
expose the giver to easy ruffs).
**SAUDI TERM:** "لون غير مقطوع" — *non-cut/non-singleton suit.*
**MINIMUM:** ≥2 cards of the same suit must remain in giver's hand.
**SPEAKER NOTE:** Some sittings let the receiver pick any card;
the "correct" version requires a non-singleton suit.
**Source:** lines 146-178.

### Rule 40-R3 — Card exposed during deal (pre-bid)

**WHEN:** A card is flipped face-up during dealing — either by
dealer error, or a player drops one from their hand.
**RULE:** Non-offending team has **two options**:
1. **Takweesh + "عجب ولا ما عجب"** procedure (see R4).
2. **Continue (تمشي)** — let it ride.
**SPEAKER NOTE:** Qaid is **NOT** available for an exposed card
pre-bid. Only Takweesh or continue.
**Source:** lines 181-205.

### Rule 40-R4 — "عجب ولا ما عجب" (do you like it or not) procedure

**WHEN:** Card-exposure Takweesh invoked under R3.
**RULE:** Each player picks up their hand and the offended team
asks their partner: *"يعجبك الورق ولا لا؟"* ("does the hand please
you or not?"). If **either teammate** says yes (يعجب), they buy/bid
("معجون ورق"). If both say no, they call **Kashe + redeal**.
**MECHANIC:** Functions as a free option for the offended team —
they get to peek at their own hand before deciding whether to
salvage or kill the deal.
**Source:** lines 205-211.

### Rule 40-R5 — Exposed-card severity gate (rank-of-exposed-card)

**WHEN:** Determining whether to invoke R3-R4 vs let it ride.
**RULE:** Convention scales with the **rank of the exposed card**:
- **Low cards (7, 8, low pip)** — most sittings just continue
  ("سبعه ما راح تفرق في الجيم في الاغلب يمشون").
- **Ace (الإكه)** — most sittings invoke Takweesh; speaker calls
  Ace exposure pivotal because the player to the dealer's right
  leads first and can see-and-route around the exposed Ace.
**SAUDI PHRASE:** *"اذا طاح اي شكل غير الاكه تمشي... لكن اذا طاحت
اكه لا"*.
**Source:** lines 196-225.

### Rule 40-R6 — Exposed-card responsibility-asymmetry

**WHEN:** Exposed-card Takweesh under R3.
**RULE:** **The offending side is the one whose card was exposed**,
regardless of how. If dealer exposed it → bidding side (dealer's
side) is offending. If a defender dropped one → defender side is
offending. Non-offending team holds the menu of responses.
**RATIONALE:** An exposed Ace **benefits whoever sits to dealer's
right** (the first lead) — so dealer-side exposure of an Ace
strongly favors the defenders, who would naturally choose Takweesh
to deny the bidder team that information.
**Source:** lines 219-230.

### Rule 40-R7 — Sitting (الجلسة) governs ambiguous cases

**WHEN:** Any pre-bid error where the convention isn't fixed.
**RULE:** Defer to **house rule (الجلسة)** of the current table.
Examples cited:
- Some sittings always let it ride (تمشي).
- Some sittings always Takweesh.
- Some sittings always Qaid.
**META-RULE:** *"ما هو قران منزل" — these aren't divine law.* A
sitting can vote at start of game to fix the convention.
**SPEAKER GUIDANCE:** When stakes are high (jeem حامي, score near
match-target), default to **strict** Takweesh/Qaid; when casual,
default to "let it ride."
**Source:** lines 33-58, 169-180.

### Rule 40-R8 — Dealer's self-protection (concealment opportunity)

**WHEN:** Dealer notices their own miscount BEFORE any opponent
detects it AND no card has been exposed.
**RULE:** Dealer may silently rebalance — give the over-dealt's
extra card to the under-dealt player as the FINAL deal step,
without announcing the miscount. Legitimate provided **no card has
been exposed face-up**.
**GATE:** If any card has been exposed in the middle of the deal
("انفتحت ورقه في الارض"), the dealer can no longer conceal — Takweesh
becomes available to opponents.
**Source:** lines 67-103.

## 5. Code mapping & open questions

### Mapping to `Rules.lua` / `Constants.lua`

This entire video governs **pre-bid** mechanics that the addon does
**not currently model**. WHEREDNGN's Lua code does not have a
deal-time error path — `Rules.lua` begins at the bid phase. To
implement:

1. **New constant proposal:** `K.QAID_DEAL_HOKM_RATE = 16` — the
   "deal-Qaid" rate (R1). Distinct from `K.QAID_HOKM = 16` and
   `K.QAID_SUN = 26`; same numeric value as Hokm-Qaid but applies
   regardless of contract type because pre-bid.
2. **New phase identifier:** `K.PHASE_PREDEAL_ERROR` — needed if
   pre-bid error handling is ever wired into the state machine.
3. **New message types:** `K.MSG_KASHA_REDEAL`, `K.MSG_AJAB_QUERY`
   (the "عجب ولا ما عجب" peek-decision flow).
4. **`R.CanQaidDeal(team)` predicate** — gates whether mis-deal
   Qaid is available; would need a state flag tracking whether a
   procedural error occurred during the deal.

### New Kasho-trigger conditions (FLAG)

Per the task brief, this video **EXPANDS the Kasho-trigger list**.
Existing list (from saudi-rules.md):
- Mis-cut, dropped card, mis-deal (generic).

**Newly-distinguished Kasho triggers from this video:**

| # | Trigger | Saudi name | Penalty rate | Source line |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| K-T1 | Mis-deal count (extra/missing card) | قيد التوزيع | 16 raw (Hokm-rate even if contract becomes Sun) | 122-145 |
| K-T2 | Card exposed face-up during deal — Ace | اكه طايحه | Takweesh + redeal (Qaid NOT available) | 196-205 |
| K-T3 | Card exposed face-up during deal — non-Ace | "تمشي" default | Usually let-it-ride | 200-204 |

**Key clarification vs prior knowledge:** speaker treats
**التكويش (Takweesh)** and **الكوشه (Kashe)** as interchangeable
nouns for the **same light pre-bid penalty**. This confirms the
glossary treatment of Takweesh as the *call* invoking Kasho
mechanics. **No semantic split** between the two terms in this
video.
