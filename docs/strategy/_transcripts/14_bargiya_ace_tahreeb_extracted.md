## 1. Decision rules

### Sender side — when to fire Bargiya

| # | WHEN | RULE | CONFIDENCE |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sun, partner winning trick; you must discard; you hold إكَه (A) of suit X and want partner to lead X next | **Bargiya** — discard the إكَه of X. Strongest possible "want this suit" signal — equivalent to "I have a سوا (SWA) here, come to me". | Definite |
| 2 | Sender has a **single long suit** (5+ cards) AND only one or two suit-shapes (مردوفة/short other suits); partner has just won a trick and led the suit you don't want | Bargiya **early** (don't wait) — partner needs early disambiguation because non-Ace Tahreeb (top-down/bottom-up) is ambiguous when sender lacks the off-suit needed to encode direction. | Common |
| 3 | Sender will Bargiya but partner cannot tell ambiguous "أحمر-on-أحمر" mapping (e.g., partner discarded الهاس → you must say "I want أسود (سبيت or شريع)") and you have no شريع to clarify with normal Tahreeb | Use Bargiya from the **first opportunity** so partner doesn't guess wrong between the two black suits. | Common |
| 4 | Sender about to Bargiya for "shed" reason — i.e. you tracked partner being void in suit Y and you suspect opp will eventually grab your إكَه | Bargiya as a **defensive shed** ("شرد بالاكة"): partner takes it now rather than opp taking it later. NOT a "lead this suit" signal — the Ace is dumped to deny the opponent. | Common |
| 5 | Sender has إكَه of X **and** the T (العشره) of X **with no card between them** (ace-ten doubleton or near-doubleton); endgame; you played إكَه to partner | Ambiguous Bargiya — you may simply be choosing between A and T to discard, NOT necessarily inviting return. Receiver should not assume strong Bargiya semantics; treat as endgame-only soft signal. | Sometimes |
| 6 | Sender has إكَه of X but only 3 cards of X total | Do NOT Bargiya from X — burns your strong suit. Tahreeb from a different (weak) suit. | Common |

### Receiver side — when to lead the Bargiya'd suit

| # | WHEN | RULE | CONFIDENCE |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7 | Partner Bargiya'd you suit X; you are **void in suit X** (cannot play X at all, regardless of any other cards in hand) | Lead X **immediately on next turn**, no further analysis. Universal answer regardless of game phase. | Definite |
| 8 | Partner Bargiya'd you X; you hold X **and** game is in **endgame** (≤ 4 cards each player) | Lead X **immediately on next turn** even if you have other capturing tricks ("اكل زياده"). Endgame ⇒ partner almost certainly holds سوا. | Definite |
| 9 | Partner Bargiya'd you X; you hold X **and** game is in **opening/mid** (≥ 5 cards each player); you have at least one capturing trick available | Do NOT rush — capture **one** (or at most two) tricks of your own first, then lead X. Partner may NOT hold سوا yet at this depth. | Common |
| 10 | Partner Bargiya'd; you have NO capturing trick of your own | Lead X immediately regardless of phase. | Definite |
| 11 | Partner Bargiya'd you X; you hold X but X-suit completion is uncertain (e.g., you hold بنت/شايب of X but the شايب of X is missing and may be at opp) | Still lead X — Bargiya overrides the side-card analysis; partner expected the gap. | Common |
| 12 | You receive Bargiya, you have إكَه/sequence in suit X already and want to "burn one trick first" before returning to partner | Burn at most **one strong trick** (not two), then return. Each extra trick you take before returning **erodes partner's سوا** as opponents discard. | Common |

### Receiver — when you cannot return the suit at all

| # | WHEN | RULE | CONFIDENCE |
|---|---|---|---|
| 13 | Partner Bargiya'd you X; you are void in X AND have no winning lead of your own | Apply general Tahreeb-conventions: lead **عكس الشكل** (opposite-color/shape inferred suit). If opp is winning, use تنفير on opp. | Common |

### Anti-Bargiya (you have إكَه but should NOT Bargiya)

| # | WHEN | RULE | CONFIDENCE |
|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | You hold إكَه of X but partner is NOT currently winning the trick | Do NOT Bargiya — Bargiya is structurally a Tahreeb (partner-winning-discard); throwing إكَه to opp's winning trick is a gift. | Definite |
| 15 | You hold إكَه of X, partner is winning, but you also hold strong continuation in X (T+شايب+بنت etc.) AND you have ≥ 4 cards of X | Don't Bargiya — keep the إكَه for your own future lead; Tahreeb a different suit instead. | Common |
| 16 | Endgame with 3 cards left in your hand including إكَه; opponent holds the only remaining off-suit your partner needs | Acceptable defensive Bargiya (rule 4 above) — but distinguish it from "lead-X" Bargiya in your own head when reading partner's later play. | Sometimes |

### Cross-trick — opponent Bargiya

| # | WHEN | RULE | CONFIDENCE |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17 | Opp Bargiya'd to their partner | Do NOT lead that suit. Treat as opp's strong suit. | Common |
| 18 | You are tempted to give opp إكَه (i.e., you're in a Tahreeb-style discard situation but trick-winner is opp) | Allowed in **one** narrow case: you've tracked the cards and know opp at the table is on their **last card** of the suit, AND you hold the T behind. Sacrifice إكَه to free the T to capture. Outside that, do NOT give opp the Ace. | Sometimes |

## 2. New terms (added to glossary)

- **برقية / Bargiya** ("telegram") — confirmed: Ace-discard form of Tahreeb. Already in glossary; this video provides authoritative trigger detail.
- **التبريك (at-tabreek)** — alternate name for Bargiya used in this video ("التبريك" = "the telegraphing"). Same mechanic.
- **شرد بالاكة (sharrad bil-ikah)** — "ran with the إكَه" / defensive Ace-discard. The Bargiya-as-shed variant (rule 4). Distinct semantic from "come-to-me" Bargiya.
- **محشور بلون واحد (mahshoor bi-lawn waahid)** — "stuffed/cornered into one suit". Sender-side condition that justifies early Bargiya (rule 2).
- **مردوفه (mardoofa)** — "doubled / paired-down" — used here to mean a short side-suit holding (2 cards). Already partially captured in glossary section 8.
- **بدايه اللعب / نهايه اللعب** ("opening / endgame") — explicit phase boundaries: 5+ cards/hand = opening; ≤ 4 cards/hand = endgame. Sharper than prior glossary text.
- **كسر كبوت (kasar kaboot)** — "broke the kaboot" / first-success-against-sweep. Already implied in `endgame.md`; this video uses the verb form explicitly.

## 5. Non-rule observations (the brief detailing requested)

**Bargiya (برقية) — refined definition.**
The Ace-discard variant of Tahreeb. Functionally a "telegram" (literal translation): a partner-coordination signal in **Sun**, fired by discarding **إكَه (A) of suit X** while partner is winning the current trick. Two semantic flavors share the mechanic:
1. **Come-to-me** ("invite") — the strong reading; sender claims "I have سوا in X, lead X to me".
2. **Defensive shed** ("شرد بالاكة") — sender discards إكَه to deny opp; NOT a lead-X invitation. Distinguished by game phase + sender's prior void-reads on opp.

Speaker calls Bargiya the **strongest possible Tahreeb signal**, with ~90%+ reliability when fired in proper conditions. Saudi-language synonyms in this video: البرقية, التبريك, تهريب الاكة.

**Sender preconditions — exactly when does Bargiya fire?**
1. Contract = Sun (the convention is Sun-only in practice; Hokm has different signal layer).
2. Partner is **currently winning** the trick (universal Tahreeb precondition).
3. You must discard (void in led suit, or discard of free choice).
4. You hold إكَه of suit X.
5. Either:
   - (a) You hold strong continuation behind the إكَه (T + شايب/بنت/ولد) and want partner to lead X to enable your سوا; OR
   - (b) You suspect opp will capture إكَه if you keep it (defensive shed); OR
   - (c) Your hand is **محشور بلون واحد** (5+ in one suit, short side suits) AND your normal top-down/bottom-up Tahreeb is ambiguous between two candidate suits — Bargiya disambiguates immediately ("from the first opportunity").

**Anti-triggers — when do you have إكَه but should NOT Bargiya?**
1. Partner is NOT currently winning the trick (rule 14 — Bargiya requires partner-winning).
2. You hold ≥4 cards of X with strong continuation — keep إكَه for your own lead, signal a different suit.
3. You hold **only** إكَه + one other in X (e.g., إكَه + 7) and partner is not Sun bidder — ducking would be safer.
4. Partner is bidder of a different contract (Hokm) — Bargiya isn't the convention there.
5. You're contemplating giving إكَه to opponent's winning trick — NEVER (waste). Single narrow exception: rule 18 (last-card sacrifice to free your T).

**Receiver expectation — what does partner do on receipt?**
- **Endgame (≤ 4 cards each)**: lead the Bargiya'd suit on **next turn**, immediately, without analysis. Default assumption: sender holds سوا.
- **Opening (≥ 5 cards each)**: capture **one** (max two) of your own tricks first to establish position, **then** lead the Bargiya'd suit. Sender may not hold سوا at depth — and may have Bargiya'd as defensive shed (case (b)).
- **Void in the suit**: lead it anyway, immediately, regardless of phase. (You can't capture anything yourself in that suit.)
- **No capturing tricks at all**: lead Bargiya'd suit immediately, regardless of phase.
- **Don't burn ≥ 2 own tricks before returning** — each extra trick you take, opp may discard their X holdings, weakening sender's سوا.

**Compare with non-Ace Tahreeb (bottom-up) — the mechanic differs how?**
| Aspect | Bottom-up Tahreeb | Bargiya |
|---|---|---|
| Encoded card | Low (7/8) → higher (9/J) **across two tricks** | إكَه, **single discard** |
| Speed | Requires 2 partner-winning tricks for full reliability (~70% → ~90%) | One discard, immediately ~90% |
| Receiver delay tolerance | Receiver can wait, gather info, decide | Receiver must act fast (especially in endgame) |
| Sender's hand shape | Sender has T or shape that justifies wanting return | Sender has **سوا-grade** (إكَه + sequence behind) |
| Risk if misread | Opp may capture next round | Opp may capture إكَه if shed-variant misread as invite |
| Ambiguity | Direction-based (which suit) | Suit-clear, intent-ambiguous (invite vs shed) |
| Phase sensitivity | Phase-agnostic | **Phase-sensitive**: endgame = act now; opening = delay |

The key structural difference: bottom-up Tahreeb says "I want this suit, no Ace"; Bargiya says "I want this suit, **and the Ace is gone from my hand** (you'll be drawing into my T+J+شايب behind)". The Ace gone from sender's hand is the load-bearing fact for receiver — it transforms partner's remaining sequence into a سوا candidate.
