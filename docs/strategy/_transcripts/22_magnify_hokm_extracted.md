# 22_magnify_hokm — extracted rules

**Source:** Rmx3GipsNuo_22_magnify_hokm.ar-orig.txt
**Title:** التكبير في الحكم - بلوت | 6 (Magnification in Hokm — Baloot ep 6)

This is the Hokm companion to the Sun Takbeer video (#21). Same core rule — when partner leads and you're in pos-2 or beyond, **play your largest available card** — but the exemplars are built around the trump rank order (J / 9 / A / T / K / Q / 8 / 7) and the speaker carves out one major exception (the **non-consecutive holding with تسعه**).

## Distilled rules

### R1. Hokm pos-2 / pos-3 with **consecutive top trumps** — play largest, no debate
- **WHEN:** Hokm contract; partner has led trump (e.g., ولد / J of trump); you are next-to-act and hold a **consecutive sequence** of high trumps (e.g., تسعه + عكه; or عشره + شايب; or بنت + ثمانيه after ولد is gone — i.e., your two top live trumps are adjacent in rank order).
- **RULE:** Play the **highest consecutive card** unconditionally. "العب اكبر ورقه موجوده عندك بدون نقاش".
- **WHY:** With consecutive tops you cannot lose value by leading the bigger one — it captures whatever the opponent had, and your remaining card stays as the next-rank cover.
- **CONFIDENCE:** Definite (single source, but the speaker is emphatic and corroborates Sun video #21).

### R2. Hokm pos-2 / pos-3 with **consecutive trumps** — same play, but the BIGGER first sends a stronger signal
- **WHEN:** R1 setup; you hold both تسعه and عكه (the two highest after ولد is gone); partner led ولد.
- **RULE:** Lead the **عكه (A)** first, then تسعه next opportunity — *not* the reverse. Either order captures the same trick, but **عكه-then-تسعه** transmits to partner "I have nothing higher live" (because if you held something higher you'd have led that). Reverse order leaves partner uncertain whether you also hold عكه.
- **WHY:** "اللي حفرق عندنا هنا الرساله" — the win is identical, but the signal value differs. Consecutive tops should be played top-down so partner can read your hand.
- **CONFIDENCE:** Definite.

### R3. Hokm pos-2 / pos-3 with **NON-consecutive holding** containing تسعه — DO NOT lead تسعه
- **WHEN:** Hokm; partner led ولد; you hold the **تسعه + a non-adjacent low trump** (e.g., تسعه + ثمانيه — gap, since عكه/عشره/شايب/بنت sit between). You'd "win" by leading the تسعه, but you have no follow-up cover.
- **RULE:** **DO NOT play the تسعه first.** Play the **side card (the lower trump, e.g., ثمانيه)** instead. The opponent is forced to commit a high trump (شايب / بنت / عكه / عشره) to capture, then the تسعه becomes top live trump for the **next** trick.
- **WHY (the speaker's pivotal example):** If you lead تسعه immediately and pos-4 holds e.g. عكه + شايب + عشره, opponent reads you as "must have ولد + تسعه consecutive" and ducks low (سبعه), preserving their three covers. You burn your only top trump for one trick. Play ثمانيه instead → opponent forced to spend شايب → on the re-lead your تسعه picks up the عشره worth +10. **You eat two tricks instead of one.**
- **WHY this is the inverse of R1:** R1 applies only when your two top trumps are consecutive (no gap). The moment the holding is non-consecutive, the strategy flips — preserve the higher card and "fish" the opponent's mid-trumps with the lower side card first.
- **CONFIDENCE:** Definite. This is the central new rule of the video.

### R4. R3 generalizes to "تسعه + 2+ side cards" (long non-consecutive holding)
- **WHEN:** R3 setup, but you hold **تسعه + ثمانيه + سبعه** (or any ≥3-card non-consecutive trump holding with تسعه as your only top).
- **RULE:** Same prescription — **never lead the تسعه first.** Lead from the side cards (e.g., the ثمانيه). Withhold the تسعه as a re-entry. "كل ما زاد الورق عند خويك الافضل ما يلعب اكبر ورقه طبعا ما يكون متتاليه".
- **WHY:** The longer your non-consecutive holding, the more tricks you can guarantee by burning side cards before committing the تسعه. Length amplifies the R3 logic.
- **CONFIDENCE:** Definite.

### R5. Hokm pos-2 with **non-consecutive AND opponent-cut already happened** — DO NOT Takbeer either
- **WHEN:** Hokm; you led ولد in trick N; pos-2 (RHO) **cut** a non-trump suit they were void in. Trick N+1 comes back to you and you hold the تسعه + a non-adjacent trump.
- **RULE:** Still **do NOT lead the تسعه** even though you'd "win". The cutting opponent is now likely to hold ولد + عكه + عشره or similar long-trump shape, and pos-4 may also hold extra trumps. Lead the **side card** (e.g., ثمانيه) and let opponents fight each other for the trick.
- **WHY:** Cutting reveals length; the cutter's partner may also have trump support. Playing تسعه straight into known long-trump opp = wasting your only top live trump.
- **CONFIDENCE:** Common.

### R6. The opponent's pos-4 read also benefits — encourages opp to dump-low
- **WHEN:** R3-R5 — you withhold the تسعه and play a side trump (ثمانيه).
- **RULE (read for the opponent's perspective, not yours):** The opponent in pos-4 reasoning "the leader had ولد + side card, so the تسعه must be still live somewhere" will play **شايب or بنت** to capture cleanly, NOT the عشره or smaller covers — because they're afraid of a hidden تسعه ambush. This is the bait that makes R3 work.
- **WHY:** R3 is not a guarantee; it depends on pos-4 misreading your hand. The convention works because pos-4 expects sub-rationality (Takbeer = always play biggest), so a small lead reads as "no big card in this suit" and they over-commit.
- **CONFIDENCE:** Common. (Implicit reasoning behind R3; the speaker walks through it explicitly.)

### R7. Hokm pos-3 vs. opponent who **cut your partner's lead** — R3 still applies
- **WHEN:** Partner led a side suit; pos-2 cut with low trump (e.g., شريحه); you (pos-3) hold تسعه + ثمانيه of trump.
- **RULE:** Even though your trump beats the cutter, **do NOT play تسعه**; play ثمانيه (the side card). Opp pos-4 will likely take with شايب / بنت, and your تسعه preserves the next trick.
- **WHY:** Same R3 logic applied to ruff/over-ruff. The temptation to over-cut with the تسعه is the trap.
- **CONFIDENCE:** Common.

### R8. Pos-2 with two or more trumps **if you can take the lead** — also play larger when consecutive
- **WHEN:** Same as R1 but the partner-leader is now setting you up for control (partner led ولد expecting you to capture and re-lead).
- **RULE:** If your two trumps are consecutive (e.g., عشره + شايب after ولد + تسعه + عكه gone), play the **عشره first** — same convention. If you hold عشره + سبعه (non-consecutive), play the **سبعه** if you don't want the lead, **عشره** only if you have a follow-up plan to keep tempo.
- **WHY:** Consecutive = full top-down; non-consecutive = play to retain your top for re-entry. Identical to R3 but for mid-rank pairs.
- **CONFIDENCE:** Common.

## Non-rule observations

**Takbeer in Hokm — the rule:** "Play big" in Hokm means *play the largest of a **consecutive top sequence***, never the largest of a *non-consecutive holding*. The Sun rule "always dump highest under partner's win" does NOT translate cleanly — Hokm trump is too short and non-consecutive holdings need their top preserved as a re-entry. The speaker keeps repeating the same dichotomy: **متتاليه → اكبر ورقه; غير متتاليه → الورقه اللي جنب** ("consecutive → biggest card; non-consecutive → the card *next to* the biggest").

**Trump-suit Takbeer — different from off-trump:** Hokm Takbeer is **only** about trump leads. Off-trump Hokm follows the standard losing-side rule (dump *lowest* trump when forced to ruff, per Section 4 row 2). Takbeer is the *partner-led-trump* convention specifically. The video doesn't address off-trump within a Hokm contract because there the question is "ruff or shed" not "play big or small."

**Hokm-specific subtlety — what makes Hokm Takbeer different from Sun Takbeer:** Two things.
1. **Trump rank order is non-natural** (J / 9 / A / T / K / Q / 8 / 7), so "consecutive" in trump is rank-order-consecutive, not numeric-consecutive. تسعه + عكه are consecutive (ranks 7 and 6). تسعه + ثمانيه are NOT consecutive (ranks 7 and 2 — huge gap with A/T/K/Q in between). Saudi players read the rank order natively; agents must compute it from `K.RANK_TRUMP_HOKM`.
2. **The 9 of trump is the centerpiece exception.** Because تسعه is the second-highest trump but has only ولد above it, almost every "non-consecutive with top" Hokm scenario revolves around تسعه + a low trump. The video is essentially "how to play the 9 of trump in second/third position." Sun has no equivalent — there are no trump-rank-order surprises in Sun, all suits are A=high, ladder-natural.

The speaker also mentions briefly: pos-4 with **only the تسعه** (singleton trump, no side cover) — covered earlier in video #04 (Faranka in Hokm) — must take with تسعه if opp Faranka'd. This video does NOT contradict that rule; it applies to a different shape (long non-consecutive holding, not singleton).

## New terms

| Term | Meaning | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **التكبير (Takbeer)** | "Magnification" — the convention of playing your **largest available card** when partner has led and you can win cleanly. Applies to **both Sun and Hokm**, but with critical Hokm carve-out (R3): only valid when your top cards are **rank-consecutive**. | Glossary entry needed. Companion to **Tasgheer** (تصغير, miniaturization, video 23). |
| **متتاليه / غير متتاليه** (mutataaliya / ghair mutataaliya) | "Consecutive / non-consecutive" — refers to **rank-order adjacency** in the trump suit, not numeric adjacency. تسعه + عكه = consecutive (ranks 7+6). تسعه + ثمانيه = non-consecutive (ranks 7+2). | The single most-repeated phrase in the transcript; the entire dichotomy hinges on it. |
| **الرساله (ar-risala)** | "The message" — the partner-readable signal embedded in card-order choice. Speaker uses this to explain why عكه-then-تسعه ≠ تسعه-then-عكه even when both win. | Maps to the existing Tahreeb/Takbeer signaling framework. |
